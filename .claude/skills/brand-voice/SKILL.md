# Brand Voice Skill

## What This Skill Does

Ensures consistent brand voice across all content:
- **Data-driven approach** - Real numbers, not vague claims
- **Practical focus** - Actionable insights over theory
- **Authority building** - Expert positioning without arrogance
- **Value-first** - Help before selling
- **Proof-backed** - Every claim supported by data
- **Authentic** - Honest about what works and what doesn't
- **ROI-focused** - Business outcomes, not just features

## When This Skill Activates

This skill automatically activates for:
- Client proposals and communications
- Social media posts (LinkedIn, Twitter, Reddit)
- Content marketing (blog posts, case studies)
- Email campaigns and outreach
- Sales materials and presentations
- Community engagement responses
- Any external-facing communication

## Brand Voice Principles

### 1. Data-Driven, Not Theoretical

**✅ DO:**
- "I tracked this for 3 months: email time dropped from 2 hrs/day → 20 min/day"
- "Analyzed 342 Reddit mentions: email overload is the #1 pain point"
- "ROI calculation: 5 hours saved/week × $150/hr = $39,000/year"

**❌ DON'T:**
- "AI can dramatically improve your productivity"
- "Our solution makes work easier"
- "Save time with our platform"

**Rule:** Every claim must have a number. No vague statements.

### 2. Show What Didn't Work

**✅ DO:**
- "I wasted 3 weeks trying to integrate Motion manually. Here's what actually worked..."
- "First attempt failed: 45% accuracy. After fixing X, got to 92%"
- "Tried 5 different approaches. 3 failed. Here's what I learned..."

**❌ DON'T:**
- "Our solution works perfectly"
- "Simple 3-step process"
- Skip the failures and iterations

**Rule:** Vulnerability builds trust. Share failures before successes.

### 3. Actionable, Not Inspirational

**✅ DO:**
- "Step 1: Run `stripe trigger payment_intent.succeeded`
- "Exact command: `/email:smart-reply --auto-apply`"
- "Schedule this script for 6 AM daily: [script]"

**❌ DON'T:**
- "Embrace the power of automation"
- "Transform your workflow"
- "Unlock your potential"

**Rule:** Reader should be able to implement immediately after reading.

### 4. Authority Without Arrogance

**✅ DO:**
- "In my 3-month experiment tracking 47 different metrics..."
- "Based on analyzing 15K Reddit posts in r/productivity..."
- "After testing 12 different AI tools, here's what worked..."

**❌ DON'T:**
- "I'm an AI expert"
- "As a thought leader..."
- "Trust me, this works"

**Rule:** Let data establish authority, not self-proclamation.

### 5. Value First, CTA Second

**✅ DO:**
- "Here's the complete implementation guide [1500 words]. Happy to answer questions."
- "I documented every step with code examples. DM if you get stuck."
- "(Subtle mention at end: 'I built this for my consulting work')"

**❌ DON'T:**
- "Book a consultation to learn more"
- "Download my free guide (email required)"
- "Link in bio"

**Rule:** Give 90% value, soft CTA 10%. Build authority through generosity.

### 6. Honest ROI Calculations

**✅ DO:**
- "Conservative estimate (50% success rate): +$85K/year"
- "Your results may vary. This worked for my use case: [specifics]"
- "Week 1: 45% accuracy → Week 12: 92% (learning curve exists)"

**❌ DON'T:**
- "10X your revenue"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Anyone can do this"

**Rule:** Show the math. Caveat the assumptions. Be honest about learning curves.

### 7. Contrarian When Justified

**✅ DO:**
- "Everyone says 'context switching costs 2-3 minutes.' I measured it: 15-20 minutes lost per switch."
- "Popular advice: 'Just use a task manager.' Reality: Task managers don't auto-schedule around energy levels."

**❌ DON'T:**
- Contrarian for shock value
- Attack people/companies
- Controversy without data

**Rule:** Challenge conventional wisdom only when you have data to back it up.

## Voice Guidelines by Content Type

### Social Media Posts

**Tone:** Casual but data-driven
**Length:** Lead with the number/result in first sentence
**Structure:**
```
Hook: Surprising data or result

Context: What you tried (1-2 sentences)

What worked: Specific approach with numbers

Proof: Screenshot, data, or detailed breakdown

Soft CTA: "Happy to share more if useful"
```

**Example (Reddit):**
```
I tracked AI productivity tools for 3 months. Here's the real ROI.

Spent $2,400 on tools (Motion, ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)
Saved 15 hours/week on average
ROI: $9,600 saved - $2,400 cost = $7,200 profit

Breakdown by tool:
• Email AI (smart replies): 5 hrs/week saved
• Motion (auto-scheduling): 4 hrs/week saved
• AI docs (writing): 3 hrs/week saved

Full methodology and data: [link]

Happy to answer questions.
```

### Client Proposals

**Tone:** Professional, ROI-focused, proof-backed
**Length:** 3-5 pages with executive summary
**Structure:**
```
Executive Summary:
- Problem (their specific pain point)
- Solution (your specific approach)
- Expected ROI (conservative estimate)

The Problem:
- Current state (with their metrics)
- Cost of status quo (quantified)
- Why past approaches failed

Our Approach:
- Methodology (specific steps)
- Similar results (case studies with data)
- Timeline and milestones

Expected Outcomes:
- Metrics to track
- Success criteria
- Conservative vs. optimistic scenarios

Investment:
- Pricing (transparent)
- ROI calculation (show the math)
- Risk mitigation (what if it doesn't work)
```

**Example opening:**
```
Executive Summary:

Problem: Your team spends 12 hours/week on manual Stripe data analysis.
At $150/hr, that's $93,600/year.

Solution: Automated Stripe analytics with AI-powered insights.
Reduces analysis time from 12 hrs → 30 min/week.

Expected ROI:
• Time saved: 11.5 hrs/week × $150/hr = $89,700/year
• Investment: $12,000 (one-time) + $2,400/year (hosting)
• Net benefit: $75,300 first year, $87,300 ongoing
• Payback period: 7 weeks

Conservative estimate (50% success rate): $37,650 first year
```

### Content Marketing (Blog Posts, Case Studies)

**Tone:** Authoritative, educational, thorough
**Length:** 1500-3000 words with data/screenshots
**Structure:**
```
Hook: Problem + surprising data

The Challenge:
- Detailed problem description
- Why it matters (business impact)
- What you tried first (and failed)

The Solution:
- Specific approach (step-by-step)
- Implementation details (code, screenshots)
- Challenges encountered and solved

The Results:
- Before/after metrics
- Unexpected benefits
- What didn't work as expected

Implementation Guide:
- Exact steps to replicate
- Code examples
- Common pitfalls to avoid

Conclusion:
- Summary of key insights
- When this approach fits (and when it doesn't)
- Next steps for reader
```

### Email Communications

**Tone:** Direct, respectful, value-focused
**Length:** 150-300 words max
**Structure:**
```
Subject: [Specific benefit or data point]

Hi [Name],

[One sentence: why I'm reaching out]

[One paragraph: specific value I can provide, with proof]

[One paragraph: what I'm asking, with clear next step]

Thanks,
[Name]

P.S. [Optional: Additional value or data point]
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: Reduced our email time from 2 hrs/day to 20 min

Hi Sarah,

Saw your post on r/productivity about email overload.

I had the same problem - spent 2+ hours/day on email until I built an AI email triage system. Now I spend 20 minutes. I documented the entire implementation with code examples and tracked the results for 3 months.

Would you be interested in seeing the breakdown? Happy to share the implementation guide - no strings attached.

Best,
Aaron

P.S. The data showed email takes 2 hours/day on average, but most advice underestimates the actual time saved by proper automation.
```

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

**Data Checklist:**
- [ ] Every claim has a specific number
- [ ] ROI calculations show the math
- [ ] Conservative estimates provided
- [ ] Learning curves acknowledged
- [ ] Failure cases mentioned

**Tone Checklist:**
- [ ] Practical and actionable (not inspirational)
- [ ] Authoritative through data (not self-proclamation)
- [ ] Honest about limitations
- [ ] Value-first (not sales-first)
- [ ] Specific (not vague/generic)

**Structure Checklist:**
- [ ] Leads with data/result
- [ ] Shows what didn't work
- [ ] Provides implementation details
- [ ] Includes proof/screenshots
- [ ] Soft CTA at end

## Common Voice Violations to Avoid

**❌ Vague Claims:**
- "Significantly improve productivity"
- "Dramatically reduce costs"
- "Game-changing solution"

**✅ Specific Claims:**
- "Reduced email time from 2 hrs/day to 20 min (10x improvement)"
- "Saved $89,700/year in manual analysis time"
- "92% accuracy after 12-week learning curve"

---

**❌ Feature Lists:**
- "AI-powered email management"
- "Smart scheduling capabilities"
- "Advanced analytics dashboard"

**✅ Outcome Focus:**
- "Automatically drafts email replies with 92% selection rate"
- "Schedules tasks around your energy levels (measured productivity +18%)"
- "Identifies revenue trends you're missing (found $8K opportunity in first analysis)"

---

**❌ Humble Bragging:**
- "I'm just a guy who loves productivity"
- "Stumbled upon this simple trick"
- "Probably nothing new, but..."

**✅ Honest Positioning:**
- "Spent 3 months tracking 47 metrics to figure this out"
- "Analyzed 15K Reddit posts to identify this pattern"
- "Tested 12 tools before finding what actually works"

---

**❌ Hard Sales:**
- "Book a call today"
- "Limited time offer"
- "Don't miss out"

**✅ Soft Authority:**
- "Happy to share more if useful"
- "Feel free to DM with questions"
- "(I built this for my consulting work, by the way)"

## Data Sources for Proof Points

Use these internal data sources to back claims:

**Productivity Metrics:** (from `/analytics:ai-insights`)
- Time saved per automation
- Accuracy improvement over time
- ROI by automation type
- Learning curves (week 1 vs week 12)

**Market Intelligence:** (from `/analytics:market-intelligence`)
- Pain point frequency (X mentions/month)
- Competitor gaps
- Community sentiment
- Content opportunity scores

**Client Results:**
- Before/after metrics
- ROI calculations
- Implementation timelines
- Success rates

**Personal Experience:**
- Tools tested (with results)
- Failed approaches (and why)
- Time investment required
- Actual costs and savings
