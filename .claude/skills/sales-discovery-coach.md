# Sales Discovery Coach 🎯

**Purpose:** Practice discovery calls with realistic customer personas. Get real-time feedback on pain discovery, qualification, and next steps.

**Use Cases:**
- "Practice a discovery call with a CMO at a health plan"
- "Coach me on this discovery call transcript"
- "What questions should I ask a VP Quality?"
- "Is my opening hook credible?"

---

## 🎭 Customer Personas (5 Interactive Roles)

### 1. CMO / VP Quality (⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Primary Buyer)
**Profile:** Strategic, ROI-focused, HEDIS deadline pressure
**Typical Questions:**
- "What's your current gap closure rate?"
- "How are you handling the ECDS transition?"
- "What's your biggest quality measure challenge?"
- "How would this integrate with our current workflow?"

**Red Flags (Bad Qualification):**
- Talking about technical features too early
- Not discovering financial impact
- Missing mentions of coordinator overwhelm or provider engagement issues
- Launching into demo without understanding pain

**Green Flags (Good Qualification):**
- Prospect mentions specific measure targets or HEDIS deadline pressure
- Questions about timeline to impact
- Interest in pilot approach with metrics
- Asking about other customers' results

### 2. Quality Coordinator (⭐⭐⭐⭐ Secondary)
**Profile:** Operational, time-pressed, overworked
**Typical Questions:**
- "How much work is this going to add to our current process?"
- "Will this work with our current gap tracking system?"
- "What's the learning curve?"
- "Can we prioritize gaps by ROI?"

**Red Flags:**
- Not addressing workflow burden/automation benefits
- Overly technical explanations
- Skipping over time-saving value prop

**Green Flags:**
- Prospect describes manual outreach burden or provider engagement challenges
- Interest in prioritization/automation
- Questions about provider engagement impact
- Asking about peer feedback or reference calls

### 3. Healthcare Provider / Physician (⭐⭐⭐ Tertiary)
**Profile:** Clinical, alert-fatigued, skeptical of vendor solutions
**Typical Questions:**
- "Why should I trust this alert more than the others?"
- "Will this work with our EHR?"
- "How is this different from what we're already getting?"
- "What's the clinical evidence?"

**Red Flags:**
- Leading with vendor hype instead of clinical utility
- Not addressing alert fatigue
- Technical jargon without clinical context

**Green Flags:**
- Prospect expresses interest in patient context/clinical narratives
- Questions about engagement rates or provider feedback
- Interest in integration with existing workflows
- Asking about patient outcomes

### 4. CFO / VP Finance (⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Budget Decision)
**Profile:** Numbers-driven, ROI obsessed, competitive
**Typical Questions:**
- "What's the ROI? Show me the math."
- "How does this compare to our current costs?"
- "What's the implementation cost?"
- "How do we measure success?"

**Red Flags:**
- Unclear pricing or ROI model
- Not quantifying impact in dollars
- Vague "soft benefits" without hard numbers
- Missing competitive comparison

**Green Flags:**
- Prospect asks specific questions about quality bonus capture
- Interest in pilot ROI (early results)
- Questions about other customers' financial impact
- Asking about scalability (pricing model clarity)

### 5. IT / Analytics Leader (⭐⭐⭐⭐ Technical Gate)
**Profile:** Technical, security-focused, integration-minded
**Typical Questions:**
- "How long is implementation?"
- "What's your HIPAA compliance story?"
- "How does this integrate with Epic?"
- "What are your uptime/SLA commitments?"

**Red Flags:**
- Hand-waving on security/compliance
- Unclear integration timeline
- Technical vagueness (no specific architecture details)
- Missing FHIR/HL7/EDI support

**Green Flags:**
- Prospect digs into FHIR compliance
- Asks about data residency/HIPAA specifics
- Interest in integration validation timeline
- Asking about scaling/performance (enterprise-grade concerns)

## 📊 Call Scoring Rubric (0-100 Scale)

Your discovery call will be scored on 5 dimensions:

### 1. **Pain Discovery (0-20 points)**
- ❌ 0-5: No pain identified or mostly talking about solutions
- ⚠️ 6-12: Surface-level pain mentioned, not deeply explored
- ✅ 13-17: Clear pain points identified with examples/data
- ⭐ 18-20: Deep pain discovery with emotional buy-in and financial impact quantified

**Good Discovery Question:**
> "Walk me through how you're managing quality measure performance today. Where does it hurt the most?"

**Poor Discovery Question:**
> "Are you familiar with care gap detection?"

### 2. **Qualification (0-20 points)**
- ❌ 0-5: Talking to wrong person or wrong problem
- ⚠️ 6-12: Right direction but unclear fit
- ✅ 13-17: Clear qualification signals (pain matches product, budget available)
- ⭐ 18-20: Strong qualification with budget, timeline, and authority identified

**Good Qualification Question:**
> "If we could close 35% more gaps, how would that impact your quality bonus this year?"

**Poor Qualification Question:**
> "Do you think this might help your organization?"

### 3. **Objection Handling (0-15 points)**
- ❌ 0-3: No objections raised or avoided
- ⚠️ 4-8: Objection raised but dismissed or unclear response
- ✅ 9-11: Objection addressed but somewhat defensive
- ⭐ 12-15: Objection transformed into deeper understanding

**Good Reframe:**
- **Objection:** "Your pricing seems high compared to our current Epic add-on"
- **Reframe:** "Epic gives you reports once a month. We're real-time, so you catch gaps 30-60 days earlier. That time advantage is worth $100K-$500K in additional quality bonus capture. How do you think that ROI compares?"

### 4. **Next Steps Clarity (0-20 points)**
- ❌ 0-5: No next steps or unclear ask
- ⚠️ 6-12: Next steps mentioned but commitment unclear
- ✅ 13-17: Clear next steps with timeline
- ⭐ 18-20: Specific action items with dates/owners, pilot scoping scheduled

**Good Next Steps:**
> "Based on what you've shared, I think a pilot makes sense. Let's schedule 30 minutes next week with your IT lead to scope what we'd need from Epic. I'll send a pilot outline by EOD Thursday. Does Tuesday or Wednesday work better for you?"

**Poor Next Steps:**
> "Let's stay in touch and we can talk more later"

### 5. **Credibility & Rapport (0-25 points)**
- ❌ 0-5: Prospect sounds skeptical or disconnected
- ⚠️ 6-10: Transactional, but not fully engaged
- ✅ 11-18: Engaged, asking follow-up questions
- ⭐ 19-25: High rapport, prospect volunteering additional context, feeling heard

**Build Credibility By:**
- Asking better questions than they expected
- Showing you understand their specific challenge
- Differentiating from typical vendor pitches
- Being honest about what you don't know

## 💡 Key Discovery Call Principles

### 1. **Listen 70% of the Time**
Your job is to ask questions and let them talk. The more they talk, the more you learn.

### 2. **Ask Follow-Up Questions**
When they mention pain, dig deeper:
- "Can you give me an example?"
- "How does that impact your team?"
- "What would it mean if we could fix that?"

### 3. **Avoid the Demo Trap**
Don't launch into a product demo until you've fully understood their pain. Demo too early and you look like every other vendor.

### 4. **Qualify Before You Advance**
Know the answers to these before moving forward:
- Is there real pain that matches what we solve?
- Do they have budget?
- Is this person the decision maker or a gatekeeper?
- Is there a timeline that works for us?

### 5. **Always Have a Next Step**
End with clarity: "Here's what happens next: [action] by [date]. Does that work?"

## 🔥 Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Better Approach |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Pitch too early | Sounds like vendor spam | Ask 3-5 discovery questions first |
| Talk about price before pain | Sticker shock without context | Establish ROI before mentioning cost |
| Let objections slide | Leaves doubt unresolved | Address objections directly, reframe to strength |
| No next steps | Wastes the momentum | Always propose specific action with date |
| Not qualifying hard enough | Wastes time on bad fits | Be willing to disqualify early |

## 📈 What Great Discovery Calls Look Like

**Opening → Pain Discovery → Credibility → Solution Introduction → Next Steps**

**Example Flow:**
1. **Opening (2 min):** Build credibility, ask permission to understand their world
2. **Discovery (10 min):** 3-5 questions about current state, pain points, financial impact
3. **Understanding Check (2 min):** "So if I'm hearing you right... [summarize]. Did I get that?"
4. **Intro (5 min):** "Here's what we've built to solve that..."
5. **Next Steps (1 min):** "Let's scope a pilot. Can we set up a 30-minute call with your IT lead next week?"

**Total Time:** 20-30 minutes
**Outcome:** Either scheduled pilot or clear disqualification
