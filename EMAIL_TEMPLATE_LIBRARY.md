## TEMPLATE 11: Objection Response - "We Don't Have Behavioral Health Capacity"

**Subject Line:** "Your Concern About Behavioral Health Capacity (And How to Solve It)"

**Preview Text:** "Depression screening works better with a care plan. Here's what other health systems are doing."

---

**Email Body:**

Hi [First Name],

You raised a really important concern during our conversation: "We don't have enough behavioral health capacity to handle more depression referrals."

**That's legitimate.** It's the #1 reason health systems say no to depression screening. And it's valid—why screen if you can't treat?

But here's what we're seeing:

**Option 1: Expand Behavioral Health Capacity (Before Screening)**
- Hire in-clinic therapist(s): $80-120K/year per therapist
- Add telepsychiatry partnership: $50-100K/year for unlimited utilization
- Then implement screening (knowing you have the care capacity)

**Option 2: Implement Screening, Expand Capacity in Parallel (During Months 1-3)**
- Start depression screening in Month 1
- Use Months 1-3 to hire therapists or stand up telepsych
- By Month 4, you're screening + treating
- This approach lets you see the true need (how many patients screen positive) before you make hiring decisions

**Option 3: Start with Low-Risk Screening (Phased Approach)**
- Screen all primary care patients (yes)
- But route based on clinical severity
  - Mild depression (PHQ-9 < 10): Care coordinator follow-up
  - Moderate (PHQ-9 10-19): Refer to telepsychiatry
  - Severe (PHQ-9 > 19): Refer to in-clinic or emergency
- This distributes the load across your existing care network

---

**What Other Health Systems Did:**

- **St. Mary's** partnered with a telepsychiatry provider before screening. Cost: $50K/year. Unlimited access.
- **Community Hospital** hired 1 therapist first ($100K), then saw they needed 2 more. They added the second therapist in Month 6 based on demand.
- **Rural Clinic** used telepsychiatry exclusively. Care coordinators did the initial screening and referral.

---

**The Key Insight:**

Screening doesn't require a psychiatrist. It requires a care plan. That care plan could be:
- Therapy (in-clinic or telepsych)
- Medication management (PCP can prescribe SSRIs)
- Care coordination + behavioral health coaching
- Community resources (support groups, peer counselors)

**What Would Work for Your Organization?**

## CUSTOMIZATION GUIDELINES

**By Persona:**
- CMO: Emphasize quality outcomes, HEDIS improvement, clinical credibility
- CFO: Emphasize financial benefit, ROI, bonus recovery
- CIO: Emphasize technical integration, IT effort, timeline
- Care Coordinator: Emphasize workflow simplification, time savings
- Quality Director: Emphasize gap closure, measure automation, dashboard capabilities
- Medical Director: Emphasize clinical decision-making, patient outcomes

**By Organization Size:**
- 5K-10K patients: Scale financial benefits down 5-10X
- 10K-25K: Scale 2-5X
- 25K-50K: Use St. Mary's case study as-is
- 50K-100K: Emphasize scale and integration complexity
- 100K+: Add references to large-scale deployments

**By Engagement Level:**
- Cold prospect: Lead with case study, offer calculator first
- Webinar attendee: Reference specific webinar content they attended
- High engagement: Move to discovery call earlier, provide more detail
