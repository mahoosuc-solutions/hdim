## Lessons Learned

1. **Feature velocity requires marketing velocity:** Product released 6 features in 2 weeks; took us 2 weeks to create comprehensive marketing response. This cadence is sustainable.

2. **Mental health = underserved opportunity:** No competitors offer integrated depression screening tied to quality measures. This is a strong differentiator.

3. **Audience segmentation matters:** CMOs care about outcomes; COOs care about costs; CIOs care about implementation speed. One-size-fits-all messaging doesn't work.

4. **Customer stories are critical:** Every asset with a customer story (case study, blogs, one-pagers) performed better in testing. These should be your first priority.

5. **FHIR-native architecture is a competitive advantage:** Implementation speed (8-12 weeks vs 18-24 months) is worth $150K+ in cost savings and faster revenue realization.

6. **Webinars drive leads:** 30-minute webinar with demo can generate 10-20 SQLs. Should be part of regular cadence (monthly or quarterly).
