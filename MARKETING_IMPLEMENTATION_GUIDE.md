## Part 3: Sales Team Playbook

### How Each Asset Wins Deals

#### Phase 1: Prospecting & Outreach

**Asset:** Clinical Overview One-Pager  
**When to Use:** Initial outreach, cold email, direct mail  
**Talking Points:**
- "I put together a 2-page overview of HDIM's approach to quality measures"
- "Shows how we compare to Epic/Cerner/current approach"
- "About 5-minute read"

**Cold Email Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

I was researching [Company] and noticed you're focused on improving Star ratings in [geography/market].

We work with health systems like [Competitor] who've closed 15-25% more quality measures using automated gap detection. One client improved Star rating 0.5 points in Year 1 = $150K+ additional revenue.

Thought you might find this useful: [One-Pager PDF link]

Would love to chat for 15 min about whether there's a fit. You free next Wednesday?

[Your name]
```

---

#### Phase 2: Discovery Call (Early)

**Asset:** Sales Quick Reference  
**When to Use:** Before every discovery call, review key talking points  
**Key Sections to Review:**
- Discovery questions (ask 3-5 during first call)
- Pain point mapping (which pain points does prospect face?)
- Competitive positioning (how are we different from alternatives?)

**Discovery Call Structure (30 min):**
- 0-5 min: Icebreaker ("Tell me about your role and what you're focused on")
- 5-15 min: Discovery questions (use 3-5 from Sales Quick Reference)
- 15-25 min: Match their problem to our solution
- 25-30 min: Advance action ("Would it make sense to do a technical walkthrough with [Product person]?")

---

#### Phase 3: Initial Working Session

**Asset:** Clinical Sales Strategy + Case Study  
**When to Use:** Working session #1, share case study before meeting  
**Approach:**
- Email case study 24 hours before meeting ("Wanted to share a relevant customer story")
- During meeting, reference case study numbers
- Connect their situation to the customer story

**Example:**
"I noticed you mentioned gaps in diabetes measure closure. This client [Case Study customer] had a similar challenge—only closing 65% of diabetes measures manually. When we automated gap detection, they jumped to 92% closure in 3 months. That's a big deal because each measure improvement = Star rating points."

---

#### Phase 4: Financial Discussion

**Asset:** ROI Calculator  
**When to Use:** When prospect asks "How much will this save/earn us?"  
**Approach:**
- Say: "Let me show you what this looks like for your organization"
- Use ROI calculator with their specific numbers (patient population, current closure rates, etc.)
- Share: "For a 15K-patient org with 60% current closure rate, we typically see $250K-$350K in Year 1 from improved Star ratings alone, plus another $150K-$200K from operational efficiency"

---

#### Phase 5: Feature-Specific Interest

**Assets:** Mental Health One-Pager OR Risk Stratification Fact Sheet  
**When to Use:** When prospect shows specific interest in mental health OR population health  
**Approach:**

**If they ask about mental health:**
- Share Mental Health One-Pager
- "We screen every patient for depression automatically. When we find positive screens, we trigger care coordination workflows automatically. This links mental health to your other chronic disease measures"
- Talk about Sarah's story (depression + diabetes example)
- Financial impact: $1,700-$2,400 per patient

**If they ask about population health/prevention:**
- Share Risk Stratification Fact Sheet
- "We tier your patients automatically: high-risk get intensive outreach, medium-risk get moderate outreach, low-risk get digital tools only"
- Talk about Robert's story (high-risk patient, prevented ED/hospital)
- Financial impact: $10K-$15K per high-risk patient prevented

---

#### Phase 6: Competitive Objections

**Using Sales Quick Reference (Objection Response section):**

**Objection:** "Epic already has quality measure tracking"  
**Response:** "Epic has quality measure *reporting*. They show you measures you're missing at year-end. We automate *gap identification* in real-time. When a gap appears, you get notified immediately so you can close it. That's why our customers close 25-30% more measures."

**Objection:** "We're already using [vendor name] for gap detection"  
**Response:** "What gaps are you identifying? How quickly? And are you seeing your closure rate improve?" [Listen] "That's helpful context. We typically see closure rate improvements of 20-30 percentage points because we integrate gap detection directly into your workflows instead of just reporting on it."

**Objection:** "This is too much work to implement"  
**Response:** "Implementation is actually 8-12 weeks, which is faster than most alternatives because we're FHIR-native. You're not integrating with a legacy system—you're connecting to a modern platform built for healthcare standards. One client told us it was the smoothest healthcare IT implementation they'd done."

## Part 6: Frequently Asked Questions

### For Sales Team

**Q: How do I know which document to send?**
A: Use the Quick-Find Guide in Content Index. If you need something for initial outreach, use One-Pager. If they're in discovery, use Sales Quick Reference. If they're evaluating, use Case Study.

**Q: The one-pager is too long. Can I just send the first page?**
A: Yes, the first page is a complete standalone executive summary. You can send that alone for initial outreach.

**Q: What if they want to know about [specific feature] that's not in the One-Pager?**
A: Use the feature-specific documents: Mental Health One-Pager for depression screening, Risk Stratification Fact Sheet for population health. Or send the full Sales Strategy document for complete information.

**Q: How do I handle objections not in the Sales Quick Reference?**
A: Reference the Clinical Sales Strategy (has competitive analysis) or Contact Product Marketing for talking points training.

**Q: Can I customize the One-Pager for specific prospects?**
A: Yes, but keep core messaging consistent. You can add:
- Prospect's logo
- Custom ROI numbers (using ROI calculator)
- Local market data if relevant
Get templates from Marketing.

---

### For Marketing Team

**Q: What if a blog doesn't get traction?**
A: Promote it in email, LinkedIn, paid ads (if budgeted). Repurpose as social posts. Turn into webinar topic. Wait 30-60 days before declaring failure.

**Q: How do I measure blog ROI?**
A: Track: page views, time on page, email signups, clicks to sales material. Goal: 1,000+ views/month after 2 months. If under-performing, promote via email and social.

**Q: Should we post all 4 blogs at once or space them out?**
A: Space them out (1 per week) to maintain audience engagement and avoid overwhelming social feeds.

**Q: How do we handle webinar logistics?**
A: Use Zoom for hosting. SendGrid/Mailchimp for email invites. HubSpot/Salesforce for attendee tracking. YouTube for recording publishing. Create checklist 2 weeks before webinar.

**Q: What if webinar attendance is low?**
A: Plan earlier promotion (4 weeks instead of 3). Use email campaigns (5+ emails instead of 3). Offer on-demand version after live webinar to capture interested parties.

---

### For Leadership

**Q: How much will this cost to execute?**
A: Costs are mostly time (you have all assets). Media costs if running ads (suggest $1K-$2K for blog promotion). Webinar platform (included if already using Zoom). Estimate: $2K-$5K total hard costs + 40 hours marketing/sales ops time.

**Q: What's the ROI on this investment?**
A: Conservatively: 5-10 new customers in 6 months = $750K-$2.5M revenue. Marketing investment: $2K-$5K. ROI: 150:1 to 625:1.

**Q: When will we see results?**
A: Week 1: Sales team equipped. Week 3: Blog content live. Week 8: Webinar generates leads. Week 12: First qualified opportunities from new messaging. Month 6: Revenue impact measurable.

**Q: What if sales team doesn't use the new materials?**
A: Have sales manager reinforce adoption. Track usage metrics. Celebrate reps who use materials (leads generated, deals advanced). Tie to comp if applicable.
