## Email Strategy

### Best Practices
- **Subject line**: Specific, relevant, non-salesy (avoid "Demo" or "Meeting")
- **Length**: 100-150 words max (30 seconds to read)
- **Personalization**: Reference their specific situation
- **Value proposition**: Clear WIIFM ("What's In It For Me")
- **Single CTA**: One specific ask
- **Timing**: Send Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am or 2-4pm their local time
- **Follow-up**: 3-day cadence (Day 1, Day 4, Day 7)

### A/B Testing Approach
For each prospect, prepare 2 versions:
- **Version A**: Problem-focused (their pain point)
- **Version B**: Opportunity-focused (what they could achieve)

Send Version A first. If no response, send Version B in follow-up.

## Email Tracking & Optimization

### Metrics to Track
- **Open rate**: Target 25-35% (use tools like HubSpot, Outreach.io, or Mailtrack)
- **Response rate**: Target 10-15% for highly personalized emails
- **Meeting scheduled rate**: Target 5-8% from cold outreach
- **Time to response**: Track to inform follow-up timing

### A/B Testing Variables
1. **Subject lines**: Problem vs. Opportunity vs. Question
2. **Email length**: Short (75 words) vs. Medium (150 words) vs. Long (200+ words)
3. **CTA**: Direct ask vs. Soft ask vs. Question
4. **Social proof**: Numbers vs. Story vs. None
5. **Send time**: Morning vs. Afternoon vs. Day of week

### Winning Patterns to Scale
- Which subject lines get highest open rates?
- Which email bodies get highest response rates?
- Which CTAs get most meetings scheduled?
- Which prospect types respond best to which messages?
