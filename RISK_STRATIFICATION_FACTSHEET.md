## THE PROBLEM YOU'RE FACING

### 80% of Costs Come From 20% of Patients

In your patient population:
- **20% of patients drive 80% of healthcare costs**
- These high-risk patients have multiple chronic diseases, mental health issues, and poor medication adherence
- Your care coordination team spends time on ALL patients equally
- Result: Limited resources spread too thin; high-risk patients don't get sufficient focus

### Current Risk Identification is Manual & Incomplete

| Challenge | Current State | Impact |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **No automated risk tiers** | Manual review (when it happens) | Inconsistent; many high-risk patients missed |
| **No visibility into true complexity** | Only see diagnosed conditions | Mental health, social factors invisible |
| **Reactive interventions** | Wait for ED visit or hospital admission | Too late; damage already done |
| **Time wasted on low-risk patients** | Same effort for all patients | High-risk patients under-served |
| **No predictive capability** | Can't forecast who's likely to decompensate | No prevention, only reaction |

### Result: Inefficient Spending

- High-risk patients get care only after crisis (ED visit, hospitalization)
- Low-risk patients receive unnecessary interventions
- Care coordination resources aren't optimized
- ED/hospital costs skyrocket while care coordination costs stay static

---

## THE SOLUTION: Automated Risk Stratification

### How It Works

**Step 1: Automatic Risk Assessment**
- HDIM continuously analyzes patient data (claims, EHR, pharmacy, lab)
- Risk algorithm considers:
  - **Clinical factors:** Number of chronic diseases, lab values (HbA1c, ejection fraction), comorbidities
  - **Mental health factors:** Depression, anxiety, substance use disorders
  - **Social determinants:** Housing, food security, transportation, social support
  - **Utilization patterns:** ED visits, hospitalizations, hospital readmissions
- Each patient assigned to risk tier: **Low / Medium / High**

**Step 2: Automated Prioritization**
- Care coordinator dashboard automatically shows patients by risk tier
- Top priorities: High-risk patients (immediate attention)
- Secondary focus: Medium-risk patients (structured intervals)
- Maintenance: Low-risk patients (annual check-in)

**Step 3: Tier-Specific Interventions**
- **High-risk patients:**
  - Weekly outreach (vs. monthly for low-risk)
  - More intensive case management
  - Behavioral health integration
  - Home monitoring if applicable
  - Early ED/hospitalization prevention

- **Medium-risk patients:**
  - Bi-weekly outreach
  - Care coordination for specific measures (diabetes, HTN)
  - Self-management support

- **Low-risk patients:**
  - Quarterly/annual outreach
  - Preventive care reminders
  - Self-directed resources

**Step 4: Dynamic Reassessment**
- Risk tiers updated weekly as new data arrives
- Patient improves → moves to lower tier
- Patient decompensates → escalates to higher tier
- Real-time care model adaptation
