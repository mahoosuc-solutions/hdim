**In initial discovery, listen for these phrases:**

- "We don't know which patients have gaps until the claims come in"
- "Our care coordinators spend too much time in the EHR looking for gaps"
- "We're missing quality measure targets"
- "Our Star rating is stuck at [X]; we're not improving"
- "Alerts are everywhere; nobody pays attention to them"

**When you hear these, mirror back:**

## Objection Responses (Quick Version)

**"Our EHR does this already"**
> "Most EHRs have quality modules, but they're designed for charting, not proactive gap management. You're probably checking it weekly or monthly. HDIM is designed for daily/real-time use, with data that's updated multiple times per day. Very different use case."

**"We've tried quality tools before and staff didn't adopt them"**
> "Most tools fail because they're separate from the clinician's workflow. You have to leave the EHR, log into another system, deal with irrelevant alerts. HDIM integrates into your workflow—alerts appear where you work. That's why adoption is typically 80%+ in month one."

**"This sounds expensive"**
> "Total investment is $150-200K year one. But you typically see payback in 4-5 months. So by month 6, it's paying for itself, and months 7-12 are pure benefit. Year one net ROI is typically $200K-$300K. Want to calculate YOUR specific number?"

**"We don't have time for implementation"**
> "Implementation is 8-12 weeks, and it's mostly on our side—data integration, testing, training. You invest staff time for training (2-4 hours per person) and go-live. Then it becomes part of your normal routine. We handle the complexity."

**"We need to check with IT/EHR vendor first"**
> "Makes sense. Here's what we need from IT: connectivity to your EHR FHIR APIs, claims data feed (or secure file transfer), and any single sign-on requirements. Most organizations have this already. Let's get IT on a call to confirm."

**"We're not psychiatrists—why do we need mental health screening?"**
> "Great question. You don't need to treat depression—you need to *identify* it and coordinate care. Depression makes everything worse: diabetes control, blood pressure, medication adherence, ED utilization. Our PHQ-9 screening identifies these patients, creates a care gap for referral, and tracks outcomes. Most of your undiagnosed depression is driving ED visits and complication costs right now. We're just making it visible. Typical benefit: +$95K-$190K/year from prevented complications alone."

**"I need to see proof this works"**
> "Absolutely. Let me share a customer case study—similar organization, similar patient population. They saw +23% gap closure improvement, +$265K first-year ROI. Then let's run your numbers through our ROI model to see what YOU could achieve. Want to do that?"

## Red Flags & Green Flags

**Green Flags (This is a Good Prospect):**
- ✅ Gap closure rate below 60%
- ✅ Care coordinators struggling with manual workflows
- ✅ Star rating stuck or declining
- ✅ Quality improvement is stated priority
- ✅ Budget is allocated for tools
- ✅ Visible champion (MD or Ops leader)
- ✅ Competitive pressure ("other orgs are ahead")

**Red Flags (Might Not Be Ready):**
- ❌ Gap closure already 75%+ (limited upside)
- ❌ "We'll decide next year" (no urgency)
- ❌ No clear budget for quality initiatives
- ❌ Organizational change/restructure happening
- ❌ EHR implementation in progress
- ❌ No executive champion identified
- ❌ "We're fine; not urgent" (wrong time)

## Follow-Up Frequency

**If prospect engages (one-pager view, email click):**
- Follow-up call within 2-3 days
- Send case study + working session invite
- Follow-up again if no response (48 hrs)

**If prospect goes silent:**
- Wait 1 week, send Email #6 (risk of inaction)
- Wait 1 week, send Email #7 (direct CTA)
- Move to nurture list (check back quarterly)

**If prospect schedules working session:**
- Confirm 48 hrs before meeting
- Send prep info (help them gather baseline data)
- Have data scientist/product person present (show demos)
- Send proposal within 48 hrs of meeting

## Closing Techniques

**Trial Close:**
> "Based on what I'm hearing, this sounds like the gap between knowing your measures and acting on them. Should we schedule time to explore what this could look like for your organization?"

**Risk Reframe:**
> "The question isn't whether you should do this—it's whether you can afford to wait while competitors improve their Star ratings. How long can you stay at 45% closure?"

**Momentum Close:**
> "You've got a clear opportunity here ($300K+ annually). Let's not overthink it. Let me schedule the working session, we'll run your numbers, and if it makes sense we'll move forward. If not, no harm."

**Stakeholder Alignment:**
> "I want to make sure we involve the right people. Who should we include in the working session—you, the medical director, finance? That way everyone sees the same data and can weigh in on the decision."
