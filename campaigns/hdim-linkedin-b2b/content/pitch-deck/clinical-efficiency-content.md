## Section 1: "A Day in the Life" Narrative - Before HDIM

### Dr. Sarah Chen, Primary Care Physician - A Typical Day

**7:15 AM - Arrival at Clinic**
Dr. Chen arrives 45 minutes before her first patient to review charts. She needs to check results from three different systems:
- **Epic** for primary care records
- **Quest Diagnostics portal** for lab results (separate login)
- **Regional Hospital's Cerner system** for her patient's recent ED visit (another login)
- **Surescripts** for medication history verification

She spends 35 minutes just logging in and reconciling data across systems.

---

**8:00 AM - First Patient: Mr. Williams, Diabetes Management**

| Task | Time Spent | System | Pain Point |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Pull A1c history | 8 min | Quest + Lab Corp portals | Results in different formats |
| Check specialist notes | 12 min | Cardiology portal | Faxed records, manually entered |
| Verify medication adherence | 6 min | Surescripts | Incomplete data, phone call to pharmacy |
| Document visit | 15 min | Epic | Re-entering data already in other systems |
| **Total Admin Time** | **41 min** | 5 systems | For a 20-minute clinical encounter |

---

**9:30 AM - Prior Authorization Crisis**

Patient Mrs. Rodriguez needs an MRI for suspected multiple sclerosis. The prior authorization process begins:

1. **9:30** - Order placed in Epic
2. **9:35** - Print clinical documentation (7 pages)
3. **9:45** - Fax to United Healthcare (busy signal, retry x3)
4. **10:05** - Fax finally goes through
5. **10:30** - Call to confirm receipt (15 min hold time)
6. **10:45** - Missing information - need additional notes from neurologist
7. **11:15** - Call neurologist office, leave message
8. **2:30 PM** - Neurologist returns call
9. **2:45** - Re-fax with additional documentation
10. **Next Day 4:00 PM** - Denial received via fax
11. **Day 3** - Peer-to-peer review scheduled for Day 5
12. **Day 5** - Peer-to-peer call (30 min of physician time)
13. **Day 7** - Finally approved

**Total time invested: 4+ hours of staff time, 7 days delay to patient care**

---

**11:00 AM - Quality Measure Documentation**

The practice's quality reporting deadline approaches. Dr. Chen must document HEDIS measures for her diabetic population:

| Measure | Documentation Requirement | Data Location | Time to Find/Document |
|---------|---------------------------|---------------|----------------------|
| HbA1c Control (<8%) | Recent lab result | Quest, LabCorp, hospital labs | 4-6 min/patient |
| Retinal Eye Exam | Specialist report | Ophthalmology fax, patient-reported | 3-5 min/patient |
| Nephropathy Screening | Urine microalbumin | Multiple lab systems | 3-4 min/patient |
| Blood Pressure Control | Recent BP + documentation | EHR + manual BP logs | 2-3 min/patient |
| Statin Therapy | Medication reconciliation | Pharmacy records, Surescripts | 4-5 min/patient |

**For 400 diabetic patients: 80-100 hours of chart review annually**

---

**12:30 PM - Care Coordination Call**

Dr. Chen's patient Mr. Jackson was discharged from the hospital 3 days ago. She never received the discharge summary.

- **12:30** - Call hospital medical records (8 min hold)
- **12:38** - Told to call HIM department (transferred, 5 min hold)
- **12:43** - HIM says they faxed it - check fax machine
- **12:50** - Search through 47 faxes received this week
- **1:05** - Found it - 12 pages, much illegible
- **1:20** - Finally has information needed for follow-up call with patient

**50 minutes to get information that should have arrived automatically**

---

**4:30 PM - End of Day Documentation**

Dr. Chen saw 22 patients today. She has 3+ hours of documentation remaining:

- Chart notes to complete: 8 patients
- Referral letters to dictate: 4
- Prior auth follow-ups: 3
- Care gap documentation for quality measures: 6 patients
- Phone calls to return: 7

**She will leave the office at 7:45 PM. Tomorrow starts the same way.**
