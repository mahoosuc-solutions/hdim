## Designer Brief

**Objective**: Create a thought-leadership LinkedIn ad that challenges the status quo and positions HDIM as the innovative, vendor-agnostic alternative to traditional EHR integration approaches.

**Target Audience**:
- **Primary**: Forward-thinking CIOs who are frustrated with vendor lock-in
- **Secondary**: IT Directors who've been burned by expensive vendor upgrades
- **Psychographic**: Innovators and early adopters who value independence over "safe" choices

**Key Message**: The traditional approach (using Epic's or Cerner's integration tools) is expensive, restrictive, and outdated. HDIM offers freedom through FHIR standards.

**Emotional Tone**:
- 40% Challenging/Provocative ("Your vendor is ripping you off")
- 30% Educational/Thought Leadership ("Here's why")
- 30% Empowering/Solution ("There's a better way")
- NOT: Angry, bitter, or overly aggressive

**Design Requirements**:
1. **Visual Metaphor**: The "walled garden" or "lock" must be immediately clear
2. **Professional Contrarian**: Provocative but not unprofessional (healthcare IT is conservative)
3. **Thought Leadership Aesthetic**: More "Harvard Business Review" than "Disruptive Startup"
4. **Vendor Neutrality (Optional)**: If legal prefers, avoid naming "Epic" or "Cerner" explicitly in visual
5. **FHIR as Solution**: Position FHIR standard as the "open" alternative

**Brand Guidelines**:
- HDIM positioning: The independent, vendor-agnostic challenger
- Tone: Confident, data-driven, slightly rebellious (but respectful)
- Avoid: Attacking competitors personally (keep it about the business model)

**Deliverables**:
1. Primary ad: 1200×627px PNG (Walled Garden visual)
2. A/B variant: 1200×627px PNG (Cost Comparison visual)
3. Square variants: 1080×1080px PNG for both
4. Editable source files
5. Version with competitor names removed (for legal review)

**Timeline**: 7-10 business days (most complex of 3 variants, requires custom illustration)

**Budget**: $800-1,200 for custom illustration + design time

## Risk Assessment

**This is the HIGHEST RISK variant** of the 3 due to:
1. **Legal Risk**: Naming competitors (Epic, Cerner) could invite legal scrutiny
2. **Reputation Risk**: If tone is too aggressive, could alienate conservative healthcare IT buyers
3. **Verification Risk**: "Costing you millions" must be defensible with data

**Mitigation Strategies**:
1. **Legal Review**: Mandatory review by legal team before launch
2. **Data Backing**: Ensure cost claims are based on verifiable industry reports (KLAS, HIMSS, etc.)
3. **Tone Testing**: A/B test aggressive tone vs. softer "alternative approach" messaging
4. **Competitor Naming**: Prepare version without explicit names (use "EHR vendors" generically)
5. **Start Small**: Launch with 10% of budget, scale if performance + sentiment are positive

## Legal/Compliance Checklist

**CRITICAL**: This variant MUST be reviewed by legal before launch.

- [ ] **Comparative Advertising**: Confirm naming Epic/Cerner is legally defensible (likely OK if factual, but verify)
- [ ] **Cost Claims**: "Costing you millions" and "2-3x higher costs" must be backed by data
  - Source options: KLAS reports, HIMSS benchmarks, internal customer data (anonymized)
- [ ] **Trademark Usage**: Using "Epic App Orchard" and "Cerner" trademarks - verify fair use
  - Nominative fair use likely applies (using competitor name to compare)
  - Do NOT use their logos without permission
- [ ] **Defamation Risk**: Ensure no false statements about competitors
  - "Walled garden" and "vendor lock-in" are opinions/characterizations (likely protected)
  - Factual claims (costs, limitations) must be verifiable
- [ ] **FTC Endorsement Guidelines**: "50+ hospitals" claim must be accurate count

**Recommended Legal Disclaimer** (in ad or landing page):
"Cost comparisons based on industry benchmarks from KLAS 2024 Integration Report and HIMSS Analytics. Individual results may vary."
