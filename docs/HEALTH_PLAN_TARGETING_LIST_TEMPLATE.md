## 📊 Health Plan Selection Criteria

### Ideal First Customer Profile

**Size:**
- Covered lives: 1M-5M (medium to large regional plan)
- Why: Meaningful HEDIS improvement ROI, dedicated quality team, budget flexibility
- Too small (<500K): Limited budget, less HEDIS impact
- Too large (>15M): Long sales cycle, complex procurement, slower decision-making

**Geography:**
- Preference: California, Texas, Florida, New York, Pennsylvania (largest markets)
- Why: Concentration of customers, easier to win multiple deals in region
- Avoid: States with only 1-2 plan targets (low density)

**Market Position:**
- Public company preferred (more transparent financials, easier research)
- Strong regional player acceptable (if financially healthy)
- Non-profit acceptable (often more focused on quality)

**HEDIS Opportunity:**
- Current HEDIS score: <85% (high upside for improvement)
- At-risk plans preferred (struggling with quality scores = more motivated)
- Recent news mentioning quality challenges = good signal

**Recent Activity Signals (Good Fit):**
- Mentioned in news about quality improvement initiatives
- Recently hired new Chief Medical Officer or VP Quality
- Announced new technology initiatives
- Facing CMS financial penalties (under-performance)
- Has modern EHR systems (Epic, Cerner, Allscripts = easier integration)

### Tier 1 Targets (Must-Have, 5-10 companies)

**Characteristics:**
- 2M-5M covered lives
- Recent HEDIS scores: 75-83% (clear room for improvement)
- Quality officer actively hiring or recently hired
- Warm introduction available (board member, investor, customer knows them)
- Modern EHR systems (easier integration)

**Example Tier 1 prospects:**
- Blue Shield of California (3.9M lives)
- Cigna HealthSpring (2.5M lives)
- Anthem Blue Cross regional plans (varies by region)
- Aetna regional plans (various markets)
- Empire Blue Cross (2.8M lives, NY)

### Tier 2 Targets (Strong Fit, 10-15 companies)

**Characteristics:**
- 1.5M-2.5M covered lives
- HEDIS scores: 80-84% (some room for improvement)
- Quality team actively seeking new solutions
- Cold outreach required (no warm intro)
- Good EHR systems

**Example Tier 2 prospects:**
- Regional non-profit health plans (various markets)
- Medicaid-focused health plans (often under-resourced)
- Medicare Advantage plans (quality-focused)
- Self-insured groups with health plan administrators

### Tier 3 Targets (Good Fit, 10-15 companies)

**Characteristics:**
- 500K-1.5M covered lives
- HEDIS scores: 82%+ (smaller improvement opportunity but still valuable)
- Smaller quality teams (easier to reach, faster decision)
- Cold outreach required
- Emerging technology buyers

**Example Tier 3 prospects:**
- Small regional plans (multiple regions)
- ACO-owned health plans
- State-specific plans
- Specialty health plans (behavioral health, dental partnerships)

## 🔍 Research Sources for Filling Out List

### Company Information:
- **Crunchbase:** Company size, funding, leadership, recent news
- **CMS.gov:** Official health plan data (covered lives, ratings)
- **Healthcare Executive Magazine:** Quality officer profiles, industry news
- **LinkedIn:** Decision maker titles, background, connections
- **Company websites:** About pages, press releases, leadership bios
- **Healthcare dive, Modern Healthcare, Health IT News:** Recent news and initiatives

### HEDIS Scores:
- **NCQA (National Committee for Quality Assurance):** Official HEDIS scores
- **CMS Quality Reporting System:** Medicare quality scores
- **State insurance commissioner sites:** State-specific quality data
- **News articles:** "Health plan improves HEDIS scores", "Quality challenges", etc.

### Decision Maker Contact Info:
- **LinkedIn:** Search "Chief Medical Officer [Company]", "VP Quality [Company]"
- **Company website:** Leadership page, bios
- **LinkedIn 2nd connections:** Find who you know who knows them
- **Email finding tools:** Hunter.io, RocketReach, Apollo.io
- **Direct outreach:** Call main number, ask for quality team

### Warm Introductions:
- **Board members:** Do they know anyone at target health plans?
- **Customers/partners:** Any current customers that know target companies?
- **Investors:** Do investors have healthcare connections?
- **Industry events:** Conferences where you can network
- **Accelerators/incubators:** Any alumni connections?

## 🎤 Sample Discovery Call Talking Points

**Opening (5 min):**
- "Thanks for taking the time to talk about quality improvement."
- "We've developed HDIM specifically to help health plans improve HEDIS performance through real-time care gap detection."
- "I'd love to understand your current approach, then show you how HDIM might help."

**Discovery (15 min):**
- "Walk me through your current HEDIS process. How do you identify care gaps today?"
- "What are your biggest challenges with your current quality management approach?"
- "If you could improve one HEDIS measure, which would have the biggest impact?"
- "What's your current vendor landscape? Who do you use for quality management?"
- "What's your timeline for a new solution?"

**Brief Positioning (5 min):**
- "Most of our conversations with plans like [Company] highlight three gaps in legacy vendors:
  1. They're reactive (quarterly), not proactive (real-time)
  2. They're expensive and hard to customize
  3. They create vendor lock-in"
- "HDIM is different because [3-5 differentiators]"

**Close (5 min):**
- "Does a brief product demo next week make sense? 30 minutes should show you how this could work."
- "Who else from your team should be involved? (CMO, CQO, IT, Finance)"
- "What's your timeline for decision-making?"
