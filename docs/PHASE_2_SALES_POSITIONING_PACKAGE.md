## Part 2: Discovery Call Framework

### Pre-Call Research (Per Account)

**Gather:**
1. Current Star rating or quality measure performance
2. Recent investor presentations or earnings calls mentioning quality
3. Executive team backgrounds (look for CIO, CMO, or CFO LinkedIn profiles)
4. Recent healthcare news about the organization
5. Known pain points from industry reports or analyst coverage

**Find:** One signal of "innovation readiness":
- Recent AI/tech hiring
- Partnership with tech vendors
- Attendance at tech conferences (HIMSS, RISE)
- LinkedIn posts mentioning AI or digital transformation
- Recent awards for innovation

### Discovery Call Opening (First 2 Minutes)

**Goal:** Establish credibility, surface pain, trigger curiosity

**Script:**

*"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the call. I know you're busy, so I'll be direct. I'm calling because we work with health plans on a problem I'm guessing is top-of-mind for you: [INSERT SPECIFIC PAIN]*

*[PAIN OPTIONS - pick based on research:]*
- *"Your Star rating is [X], and you're forecasting to end the year at [lower]—that's millions in quality bonus risk"*
- *"Your quality team is probably spending 40+ hours a week on manual HEDIS tracking and gap management"*
- *"You've got a CMO/VP Quality who's frustrated that legacy HEDIS vendors aren't moving fast enough on AI"*

*The reason I'm calling: Most health plans attack this problem the old way—they hire more coordinators or upgrade their legacy HEDIS vendor. We took a different approach. We asked: 'If we built a healthcare quality platform from scratch today, knowing what modern AI can do, what would it look like?'*

*What we built doesn't exist anywhere else. We're predicting care gaps before they happen, generating clinical intelligence for doctors, and helping early pilots capture quality bonuses they were leaving on the table.*

*I'm not sure if this is relevant for you—but based on [SPECIFIC RESEARCH SIGNAL], it seems like you might be open to thinking differently about quality management. Would it make sense to have a 20-minute conversation to see if this could be useful?"*

**Key elements:**
- Opens with specific, credible pain (shows research)
- Contrasts old approach with new (differentiation)
- "Doesn't exist anywhere else" (scarcity/innovation)
- Mentions concrete outcomes (predictions, intelligence, bonuses)
- Soft close (low friction)

### Discovery Call Core (Minutes 3-15)

**If they say YES:**

**Question 1: Problem Probe**
*"Tell me about your biggest challenge with quality measures right now. What keeps you up at night?"*

**Listen for:**
- Manual processes, overwhelmed team, stuck Star rating, missed bonuses, frustrated leadership
- Take notes, let them vent for 1-2 minutes

**Question 2: Current Approach**
*"How are you currently tackling this? What's working? What's not?"*

**Listen for:**
- Legacy vendor frustration, manual processes, lack of predictive capability, reactive vs proactive

**Question 3: Value Realization**
*"If we could help you [specific benefit based on their pain], what would that be worth to you?"*

**Options based on role:**
- CMO: "Predict care gaps 60 days early so you're proactive instead of reactive?"
- VP Quality: "Move from 3.5 Stars to 4.0+ by AI-powered targeting instead of manual campaigns?"
- CFO: "Capture $X million in quality bonuses you're currently leaving on the table?"

**Question 4: Decision Process**
*"If this were as good as we think it is, how would you want to validate it before making a decision?"*

**Listen for:**
- Pilot interest, timeline, budget authority, stakeholders involved

### Discovery Call Close (Minute 16-20)

**If they're interested:**

*"Here's what I'm thinking—and tell me if this makes sense. We have a 90-day innovation pilot program. It's not just us dropping software and disappearing. We embed with your team, understand your specific quality challenges, and show you what's possible with AI-first thinking. The pilots are $25-50K, and we design them to prove ROI on quality bonus capture specifically."*

*"At the end of 90 days, you'll have real data on whether this works for your population. Some customers end up saying 'not for us right now'—that's fine. Others say 'this is changing our quality strategy'—and those become long-term partnerships."*

*"Here's the thing: We're being very selective about pilots right now. We want to work with organizations that are innovation-ready and actually want to transform their quality operations, not just another automation layer."*

*"Does that sound interesting to explore? And if it is—who else would need to be in a conversation to make this real?"*

**Key elements:**
- Frame as "partnership" not vendor sale (positioning)
- Mention selectivity (scarcity, higher perceived value)
- Focus on 90-day proof + ROI (lower risk)
- Qualify for innovation-readiness
- Identify stakeholders (multi-threaded)

### Objection Handling

#### "We like our current vendor"

**Response:** *"That makes sense. Most health plans have relationships with legacy HEDIS vendors—they're not bad, they're just built in 2015. Here's the difference: Their platform uses automation + basic analytics. Ours uses first-principles AI thinking. The gap shows up in predictive capability—they tell you who's behind. We tell you who will be behind 60 days from now, plus how to close the gap. Want me to show you in 10 minutes what that looks like?"*

#### "Is this proven?"

**Response:** *"Early pilots, yes. We've got health plans seeing 35% improvement in gap closure rates, cutting pre-visit planning time by 40%, and capturing quality bonuses faster. That said, these are pilots not full deployments. That's exactly why the 90-day innovation program exists—to prove it works in your specific population before bigger commitment. Does that answer it, or do you want to dig deeper?"*

#### "What's the price?"

**Response:** *"For a 90-day pilot covering your top 3-4 HEDIS measures, typically $25-50K depending on scope. That's an innovation partnership investment, not a vendor license. You get dedicated implementation, our team embedded with yours, and proof of ROI on quality bonus capture. After pilot, if it's working, we move to annual terms which are standard enterprise SaaS pricing. First step is understanding your specific needs to give you a solid number. Sound fair?"*

#### "We need to think about it"

**Response:** *"Totally understand. Here's what I'd suggest: I'll send you a one-pager on how pilot customers are using AI for predictive gaps—might trigger some ideas. And let's grab 20 minutes with [specific stakeholder they mentioned] in the next week to see if this is worth exploring. Fair?"*
