## PHASE 1: Opening & Credibility (5 Minutes)

### Opening Hook (First 30 Seconds)
**Goal:** Establish credibility, create curiosity, differentiate from typical vendor calls

**Script:**
> "Thanks for taking the time, [Name]. I know you're busy, so I'll make this worth your while.
>
> HDIM isn't another HEDIS vendor trying to automate the same old workflows. We built an AI-first platform that does something legacy vendors fundamentally can't: **predict care gaps 30 to 60 days before they happen**.
>
> Early pilots are seeing 35-40% improvement in gap closure rates, and we're tracking quality bonus capture in real-time—down to the dollar. Not estimates. Actual results.
>
> Before I tell you more, I'd love to understand what you're seeing in your quality program right now. Sound fair?"

**Tone:** Confident but consultative. You're not pitching yet—you're establishing that this conversation will be different.

**If They Ask "Who are you?":**
> "We're a healthcare AI company focused exclusively on quality measures. Small team, but we've built something the big vendors can't—predictive intelligence that gives your team 30-60 days to close gaps before they impact Star ratings or HEDIS submissions."

---

### Transition to Discovery
**Goal:** Shift control to them, make it conversational

**Script:**
> "I have three quick questions that'll help me understand if what we've built is relevant for you. First one: **What's your biggest challenge with quality measure performance right now?**"

**Pause. Let them talk. Listen for pain indicators.**

## PHASE 2: Pain Discovery (15 Minutes)

### Core Discovery Questions (Ask 5-7 of These)

**Question 1: Current State of Quality Program**
> "Walk me through how your quality team identifies and closes care gaps today. What does that process look like?"

**Listen for:**
- Manual workflows (weeks/months to prepare reports)
- Reactive approach (gaps identified after the fact)
- Data silos (claims vs. EHR vs. engagement platforms)
- Overwhelmed coordinators (high workload, burnout)

**Follow-up probe:**
> "How long does it typically take from 'we identified a gap' to 'we closed the gap'? What slows that down?"

---

**Question 2: Financial Impact & Visibility**
> "How much is quality measure performance costing you right now in missed bonuses or lost revenue? Do you have visibility into that?"

**Listen for:**
- No real-time visibility ("We find out after HEDIS submissions")
- CFO pressure ("Board wants to know ROI on quality investments")
- Star rating anxiety ("We're a 3.5-star plan trying to get to 4")
- Quality bonus at risk ("We left $500K-$1M on the table last year")

**Follow-up probe:**
> "If I showed you a dashboard that tracks quality bonus capture in real-time—down to the dollar—how valuable would that be for CFO/board reporting?"

---

**Question 3: Provider Engagement & Workflow**
> "When you send care gap lists to providers, what happens? Do they actually act on them?"

**Listen for:**
- Low engagement ("Providers don't have time to read generic lists")
- No prioritization ("They don't know which gaps matter most")
- Workflow friction ("It's a separate system, not in their workflow")
- Provider frustration ("They complain about irrelevant alerts")

**Follow-up probe:**
> "What if providers received AI-generated clinical summaries—narratives they could actually read in 30 seconds—instead of raw gap lists? Would that change engagement?"

---

**Question 4: Technology Stack & Vendor Satisfaction**
> "What tools are you using today for HEDIS reporting and care gap closure? How's that working out?"

**Listen for:**
- Legacy vendor dissatisfaction ("Annual updates, slow innovation")
- ECDS transition anxiety ("We're not ready for 2030 digital-only")
- Integration pain ("Doesn't work well with our EHR/data warehouse")
- Cost concerns ("We're paying $150K+/year and not seeing ROI")

**Follow-up probe:**
> "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your current vendor, what would it be?"

---

**Question 5: Predictive Capability Interest**
> "Right now, are you identifying gaps reactively—after they've already missed deadlines—or proactively, with time to intervene?"

**Listen for:**
- Reactive admission ("We're always playing catch-up")
- Interest in prediction ("We'd love to see gaps coming earlier")
- Lack of prioritization ("We don't know which gaps to focus on first")

**Follow-up probe:**
> "What if you could predict gaps 30-60 days in advance—before they impact HEDIS submissions or Star ratings? What would that enable your team to do differently?"

---

**Question 6: AI Readiness & Innovation Appetite**
> "Is your organization exploring AI for quality improvement, or are you waiting to see how others do it first?"

**Listen for:**
- Innovation-forward ("We're evaluating AI vendors actively")
- AI skepticism ("We want to see proof before we invest")
- Competitive pressure ("Our competitors are adopting AI; we need to catch up")

**Follow-up probe:**
> "Would you be open to being an early pilot customer if we could prove 35-40% gap closure improvement in 90 days?"

---

**Question 7: Decision Process & Timeline**
> "If we could show you a solution that solves these problems, what does the decision process look like on your end? Who else needs to be involved?"

**Listen for:**
- Decision stakeholders (CMO, CFO, VP Quality, IT, procurement)
- Budget availability ("We have budget approved for Q1/Q2")
- Timeline urgency ("We need something live before next HEDIS season")
- Approval complexity ("We'd need board approval" vs. "I can green-light a pilot")

**Follow-up probe:**
> "What would you need to see in a 90-day pilot to move forward with a full deployment?"

## Qualification Criteria: Green/Yellow/Red Light

### 🟢 GREEN LIGHT (High Priority - Pursue Aggressively)
- Health plan with 25K-100K members OR ACO with 50K+ attributed members
- Decision maker interested in AI innovation ("We're actively evaluating AI vendors")
- Pain points align with HDIM capabilities (workflow, financial ROI, ECDS, provider engagement)
- Budget available in Q1/Q2 2026
- Willing to be public reference customer
- Decision process clear (CMO/VP Quality can green-light pilot)

**Action:** Schedule pilot scoping call within 7 days, prioritize demo preparation

---

### 🟡 YELLOW LIGHT (Medium Priority - Nurture, Don't Force)
- Smaller health plan (<25K members) or early-stage ACO
- Decision maker interested but cautious ("We want to see proof before we invest")
- Budget uncertainty ("We might have budget in Q3/Q4")
- Complex approval process (requires board/procurement/IT approval)
- Pain points partially align with HDIM capabilities

**Action:** Nurture with thought leadership content, quarterly check-ins, invite to webinar

---

### 🔴 RED LIGHT (Low Priority - Polite Exit)
- Very small organization (<10K members) with limited budget
- No decision-making authority ("I'll pass this to my boss")
- No AI interest ("We're happy with manual processes")
- No budget ("We don't have budget for new vendors")
- No pain alignment ("We don't really have quality measure challenges")

**Action:** Thank them for their time, add to long-term nurture list, exit gracefully

## Discovery Call Best Practices

### DO:
- ✅ **Lead with curiosity, not pitch:** Ask questions first, present solution second
- ✅ **Listen for financial pain:** "How much is this costing you?" unlocks budget conversations
- ✅ **Quantify everything:** "35-40% improvement" beats "better gap closure"
- ✅ **Create urgency:** "ECDS transition by 2030" + "2026 HEDIS season" = timeline pressure
- ✅ **Ask for the pilot:** Make the ask clear: "90-day, $25-50K, clear success metrics"
- ✅ **Lock in next step:** Don't hang up without scheduling the scoping call

### DON'T:
- ❌ **Don't pitch before discovery:** Understand pain first, present solution second
- ❌ **Don't feature-dump:** Focus on outcomes (35% improvement) not features (FHIR API)
- ❌ **Don't compare on price alone:** Compare on value/ROI, not just cost
- ❌ **Don't leave it open-ended:** "Let me know if you're interested" = dead deal
- ❌ **Don't overpromise:** Be realistic about 90-day pilot expectations
- ❌ **Don't skip objection handling:** Address concerns directly, confidently
