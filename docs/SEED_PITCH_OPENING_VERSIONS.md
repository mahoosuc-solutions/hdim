# HDIM Seed Pitch: Three Opening Versions

**Purpose:** Three 60-second opening approaches for seed investor pitches ($750K raise)
**Created:** February 12, 2026
**Status:** Ready for practice and feedback

---

## Overview

Each version uses different positioning to appeal to different investor profiles. All three use identical facts but emphasize different angles:

- **Version 1 (Problem-First):** Lead with financial pain → best for healthcare VCs
- **Version 2 (Solution-First):** Lead with innovation → best for tech VCs
- **Version 3 (Traction-First):** Lead with proof → best for risk-averse investors

Target length: **60 seconds ±10 seconds** (adjust based on speaking pace)

---

## Version 1: Problem-First Opening

**Positioning:** Healthcare ROI angle
**Best for:** Healthcare-focused VCs, CFO/finance investors, healthcare-knowledgeable audiences
**Opening tone:** Specific pain point (money left on table)

### Full Script (60 seconds)

> "Thanks for taking the time.
>
> Health plans leave **$500K to $1M on the table every year** in quality bonuses. Here's why:
>
> They run HEDIS analytics in January, identify gaps in February, scramble to close them in March. But providers need 7-14 days just to respond, and complex interventions take time. So **60% of gaps don't close** when they should hit 85%.
>
> **We solve this by predicting gaps 30-60 days earlier.** Using AI on claims and EHR data, we identify which patients will have gaps before they're documented. That gives coordinators and providers actual runway to intervene.
>
> The result: **85% gap closure** instead of 60%. That's an extra **$1M in quality bonuses** per health plan annually.
>
> We've validated this with **5 health plans** who've told us they'd pilot. We're executing now—50-100 discovery calls this month, targeting 2-3 pilots by April.
>
> We're raising **$750K to scale the team** and hit $500K-$1M ARR by year-end. Let me walk you through the opportunity."

### Key Elements Breakdown

| Element | Content | Why It Works |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Hook** | $500K-$1M left on table | Specific dollar amount hits emotions + logic |
| **Problem setup** | Timeline + failure rate (60%) | Shows structural issue, not easy fix |
| **Solution** | Prediction 30-60 days earlier | Concrete differentiator, not vague |
| **Proof** | 85% vs 60%, $1M impact | Quantified outcome, verifiable |
| **Validation** | 5 health plans | Real proof, not theoretical |
| **Traction** | 50-100 calls this month | Executing now, not planning |
| **Ask** | $750K to scale | Clear capital request |

### Transition to Slide 2

After opening, pause and say:

> "Let me show you the problem in more detail—and why the timing is now."

*Then advance to market opportunity/problem deep-dive slide.*

### When This Works Best

**Investor Profile:**
- ✅ Healthcare VCs (Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, health system investors)
- ✅ Healthcare-focused funds (e.g., Khosla Impact, DBL Partners)
- ✅ Investors with healthcare ops experience
- ✅ CFO/finance-minded partners
- ✅ Risk-averse investors (clear problem = lower risk)

**Red Flags If This Doesn't Land:**
- Investor says: "Interesting, but what if HEDIS changes?"
- Response: "Good question. HEDIS benchmarks shift 5-10%, but the underlying gap closure deadline pressure is structural. Even ACOs and health systems have similar urgency. Let me show you the market."

## Version 2: Solution-First Opening

**Positioning:** Innovation + mechanism angle
**Best for:** Tech VCs, AI/ML-focused investors, general VCs, innovation-minded audiences
**Opening tone:** Provocative question (curiosity hook)

### Full Script (60 seconds)

> "Thanks for meeting. Quick question: what if you could predict healthcare gaps **30-60 days before they happen**, not identify them when it's too late?
>
> Right now, health plans run HEDIS analytics in February, find gaps, and have 3 weeks to close them. **60% don't close** because there's no time—providers need 7-14 days to respond, patients need appointments, interventions take time.
>
> We use AI to predict gaps early. Not waiting for claims to process. We analyze patterns—patient engagement, medication adherence, visit frequency—and flag which patients will likely have gaps **before the deadline crunch**.
>
> That 30-60 day head start changes everything. Coordinators can prioritize. Providers get AI-generated clinical narratives they actually read (not generic alerts). **Gap closure goes from 60% to 85%**—that's **$1M more in quality bonuses** per health plan.
>
> We've validated this with **5 health plans**. They told us they'd pilot. We're executing Phase 2 right now—50-100 calls this month, 2-3 pilots in April.
>
> We're raising **$750K to hire sales and hit $500K-$1M ARR** by year-end. Happy to show you the data."

### Key Elements Breakdown

| Element | Content | Why It Works |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Hook** | Provocative question (30-60 days early?) | Engages curiosity, makes investor lean in |
| **Problem context** | Timeline pressure + failure rate | Sets up why innovation matters |
| **Solution mechanism** | AI + specific inputs (engagement, adherence) | Shows how it works, not just that it works |
| **Differentiation** | Clinical narratives providers read | Makes innovation feel concrete and real |
| **Proof** | 60% → 85%, $1M impact | Same outcome, tied to innovation |
| **Validation** | 5 health plans piloting | Early customers, not just concept |
| **Ask** | $750K for sales team | Clear capital use |

### Transition to Slide 2

After opening, pause and say:

> "Let me show you how the AI works under the hood, and the customer validation we have."

*Then advance to solution architecture or customer validation slide.*

### When This Works Best

**Investor Profile:**
- ✅ Tech VCs (Sequoia, Accel, Greylock tech portfolios)
- ✅ AI/ML-focused investors
- ✅ General VCs who want innovation narrative
- ✅ Investors impressed by predictive/ML plays
- ✅ Founders/technical partners

**Red Flags If This Doesn't Land:**
- Investor says: "So it's just AI on claims data?"
- Response: "Good catch. A lot of payers have tried that. The difference is we're predicting **before claims settle**—we're using live patient engagement data, pharmacy fills, appointment patterns. That 30-60 day head start is what makes the ROI work. Here's the model..."

## Version 3: Traction-First Opening

**Positioning:** Momentum + execution angle
**Best for:** Risk-averse investors, late-seed investors, follow-up meetings, due diligence phase
**Opening tone:** Proof-focused (lead with what's real)

### Full Script (60 seconds)

> "Thanks for your time. I want to start with where we are today.
>
> We've validated HDIM with **5 health plans** who told us they'd pilot our predictive care gap platform. We're executing Phase 2 right now—**50-100 discovery calls this month**, targeting **2-3 pilot contracts by April** at $25-50K each.
>
> The problem we're solving: health plans leave **$500K-$1M on the table annually** because they identify care gaps too late. By the time they run HEDIS analytics and find gaps, there's only 3 weeks left to close them. **60% don't close.**
>
> We predict gaps **30-60 days early** using AI. That gives coordinators and providers runway to actually intervene. Gap closure goes to **85%**—that's **$1M more in quality bonuses** per plan.
>
> Our unit economics work: **$15-25K CAC**, **$100-150K annual contract value**, **5-7x LTV/CAC**. We're profitable per customer by month 3.
>
> We're raising **$750K to hire VP Sales and AEs**. With this capital, we hit **$500K-$1M ARR by December**, then raise Series A at **$2-3M ARR** in 2027.
>
> Let me show you the customer validation and financial model."

### Key Elements Breakdown

| Element | Content | Why It Works |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Opening** | 5 health plans validated (not theoretical) | Proof before pitch—lower perceived risk |
| **Momentum** | 50-100 calls this month, pilots by April | Executing now, not planning stage |
| **Problem** | Same as other versions | Establishes why revenue is possible |
| **Solution** | Same mechanism | Positioned as path to revenue |
| **Unit economics** | $15-25K CAC, $100-150K ACV, 5-7x LTV/CAC | Shows capital efficiency, path to profitability |
| **Runway clarity** | $750K → $1M ARR → Series A at $2-3M | Clear capital efficiency story |
| **Ask** | $750K for specific hiring | Resource request backed by plan |

### Transition to Slide 2

After opening, pause and say:

> "Let me walk through the customer feedback we've gotten and the financial model behind these numbers."

*Then advance to customer testimonials or financial model slide.*

### When This Works Best

**Investor Profile:**
- ✅ Risk-averse institutional investors
- ✅ Later-stage seed funds (want proof before betting)
- ✅ Follow-up meetings (they know problem already)
- ✅ Due diligence phase conversations
- ✅ Financially-disciplined funds (want unit economics)

**Red Flags If This Doesn't Land:**
- Investor says: "Your numbers look good, but are these 5 health plans really committed?"
- Response: "Excellent question. Yes—we have email confirmations from 4 of the 5 that they'll pilot at $25K. One is still in MSA phase. I can show you the LOI templates and email threads if that helps. This is why we're raising now—to close these pilots and prove the model works."

## How to Choose Your Primary Version

### Quick Decision Tree

**Question 1: Who are you pitching?**
- Healthcare VC or healthcare ops background? → **Version 1**
- General VC or tech investor? → **Version 2**
- Later-stage seed or risk-averse? → **Version 3**

**Question 2: What's your natural speaking style?**
- Data-driven / loves explaining ROI? → **Version 1**
- Technical / loves explaining how things work? → **Version 2**
- Momentum-focused / loves talking about traction? → **Version 3**

**Question 3: What stage is the conversation in?**
- Cold outreach / first meeting? → **Version 1 or 2**
- Warm intro / they know problem exists? → **Version 2 or 3**
- Follow-up meeting / they're interested? → **Version 3**

### Recommendation

**Start with Version 1 (Problem-First)** if:
- You're new to pitching
- Most of your outreach is to healthcare VCs
- You want maximum credibility in first 10 seconds

**Start with Version 2 (Solution-First)** if:
- You're pitching general VCs
- You enjoy explaining the technology
- You want to differentiate on innovation

**Start with Version 3 (Traction-First)** if:
- You're in follow-up meetings
- You have strong customer validation
- You want to emphasize execution over vision

## Practice Framework

### Week 1: Foundation

**Days 1-2: Learn all three versions**
- Read each script aloud 5 times
- Record yourself saying each one
- Time each version (aim for 55-65 seconds)
- Note which feels most natural

**Days 3-4: Pick primary + backup**
- Primary = version that feels most authentic
- Backup = version for different investor types

**Days 5-7: Internalize structure (not memorize words)**
- Don't memorize word-for-word (sounds robotic)
- Memorize key numbers and sequence
- Practice natural transitions between sections

### Week 2: Refinement

**Days 1-2: Record practice videos**
- Record 5+ takes of primary version
- Watch back and note:
  - Pacing (are you rushing through numbers?)
  - Eye contact (are you reading slides?)
  - Tone (confident? condescending? uncertain?)

**Days 3-4: Test with advisors**
- Deliver to 2-3 advisors
- Ask: "Which version makes you want to hear more?"
- Collect feedback:
  - Did opening land?
  - What questions came up?
  - What felt unclear?

**Days 5-7: Iterate and polish**
- Incorporate advisor feedback
- Adjust pacing/emphasis based on reactions
- Practice transitions to slide 2

### Week 3: Deployment

**Days 1-2: Record final version**
- Do 3-5 takes of your best version
- Select the one that feels most confident

**Days 3-7: Use it in real pitches**
- Deploy with 5-10 investors
- Track reactions
- Iterate based on what lands

## Delivery Techniques

### Pacing

**Slow down on:**
- Opening hook (let it land, pause 2 seconds)
- Key numbers ($500K-$1M, 60% vs 85%, $750K)
- Problem statement (this is the "why")
- Ask amount and timeline

**Speed up on:**
- Internal narrative details (problem context)
- Validation list (feels repetitive if slow)
- Historical context

**Pattern:** Vary pace to maintain interest. Don't speak at one speed.

### Tone

**What works:**
- Confident but not arrogant ("we know our market")
- Data-driven but not robotic (smile, let personality show)
- Passionate but not evangelical (you believe, not preaching)
- Specific but not defensive (own what you know and don't know)

**What doesn't work:**
- Overly casual ("yo, we're killing it")
- Overly formal (sounds like you're reading)
- Defensive (investor asks question, you say "great question" but sound annoyed)
- False modesty ("we're just a small team but...")

### Body Language

**Standing presentation:**
- Stand upright (posture = confidence)
- Make eye contact with the investor(s)
- Use hand gestures for emphasis (don't gesture constantly)
- Don't pace or fidget

**Sitting presentation:**
- Sit forward slightly (shows engagement)
- Make eye contact
- Hand gestures work in sit-down too
- Don't slouch or lean back

### Handling Interruptions

**If investor interrupts mid-opening:**
- Welcome it: "Great question, good timing."
- Answer briefly (20-30 seconds)
- Transition back: "But let me finish the opening so the context is clear."

**If investor asks about something coming in later slides:**
- Acknowledge: "I'll cover that in detail in a moment, but quick version..."
- Give 15-second answer
- Promise full detail later

**If investor seems confused:**
- Pause and ask: "Does this context make sense, or should I clarify?"
- Don't assume you need to keep going
- One confused investor = likely confused investors later too

## Success Metrics

### Good Signs Your Opening Is Working

- ✅ Investor leans forward
- ✅ Investor makes notes
- ✅ Investor asks specific follow-up question (not generic)
- ✅ Investor references specific number you mentioned ($500K, 60%, etc.)
- ✅ Investor says "tell me more about X"
- ✅ Investor stops checking phone/emails

### Bad Signs Your Opening Isn't Landing

- ❌ Investor says "so what do you do?" (opening didn't communicate it)
- ❌ Investor looks confused or bored
- ❌ Investor asks generic questions ("how big is the market?")
- ❌ Investor checks phone/email frequently
- ❌ Investor says "interesting" with no follow-up

### If Opening Isn't Landing

**Immediate action:**
- Don't keep going with same approach
- Pivot: "Let me reframe this a different way..."
- Switch to backup version if primary isn't resonating
- Ask directly: "Does this problem resonate with you?"

**Post-pitch action:**
- Ask advisor: "Did my opening land?"
- Review recording: What could've been clearer?
- Iterate: Adjust pacing, numbers, or version choice
