# Version 1 Practice Guide: Problem-First Opening

**Goal:** Record 5 quality takes of the Problem-First opening (60 seconds)
**Date:** February 12, 2026
**Status:** Ready to practice

---

## Before You Start

### What You'll Need

- **Recording device:** Phone, laptop, or computer with audio/video capability
- **Quiet space:** Minimal background noise
- **Timing tool:** Phone timer or watch (to stay within 60-65 seconds)
- **Notes:** Print this guide or have it nearby for reference

### Setup (2 minutes)

1. **Choose recording method:**
   - Option A: Video (better for body language/tone feedback)
   - Option B: Audio only (faster, easier to focus on delivery)
   - Recommend: Video (see yourself, identify nervous habits)

2. **Position camera/phone:**
   - If video: at eye level, showing head and shoulders
   - Frame: centered, good lighting (natural or lamp)

3. **Test audio:**
   - Record 10 seconds of silence (check for background noise)
   - Do a quick test sentence ("One two three, testing")

4. **Clear your space:**
   - Phone on silent
   - Pets/people warned not to interrupt
   - Door closed

## The Script (60 seconds)

**Print this and have it visible during practice:**

---

> "Thanks for taking the time.
>
> Health plans leave **$500K to $1M on the table every year** in quality bonuses. Here's why:
>
> They run HEDIS analytics in January, identify gaps in February, scramble to close them in March. But providers need 7-14 days just to respond, and complex interventions take time. So **60% of gaps don't close** when they should hit 85%.
>
> **We solve this by predicting gaps 30-60 days earlier.** Using AI on claims and EHR data, we identify which patients will have gaps before they're documented. That gives coordinators and providers actual runway to intervene.
>
> The result: **85% gap closure** instead of 60%. That's an extra **$1M in quality bonuses** per health plan annually.
>
> We've validated this with **5 health plans** who've told us they'd pilot. We're executing now—50-100 discovery calls this month, targeting 2-3 pilots by April.
>
> We're raising **$750K to scale the team** and hit $500K-$1M ARR by year-end. Let me walk you through the opportunity."

---

## Key Delivery Points

### Pacing Guide

| Section | Timing | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Opening ("Thanks for taking...") | 3-4 sec | Warm, genuine tone |
| Hook ($500K-$1M) | 4-5 sec | **SLOW DOWN** - let it land, pause 2 sec after |
| Problem setup (January → March) | 8-10 sec | Normal pace, explain timeline |
| Failure rate (60% vs 85%) | 3-4 sec | **SLOW DOWN** - emphasize contrast |
| Solution intro (predict 30-60 days) | 5-6 sec | **SLOW DOWN** - this is key differentiator |
| How it works (AI + data) | 6-7 sec | Normal pace, brief mechanism |
| Result (85% → $1M) | 4-5 sec | **SLOW DOWN** - emphasize outcome |
| Validation (5 health plans) | 4-5 sec | Normal pace |
| Traction (50-100 calls, pilots by April) | 4-5 sec | Normal pace, shows momentum |
| Ask ($750K, ARR target) | 4-5 sec | Clear, confident |
| Closing transition | 2-3 sec | Pause, invite engagement |
| **TOTAL** | **55-65 sec** | Adjust pacing to hit target |

### Emphasis Points

**Emphasize these with tone/pause/energy:**
1. "$500K to $1M on the table" - let it sink in
2. "60% of gaps don't close" - problem severity
3. "30-60 days earlier" - competitive advantage
4. "85% gap closure" - proof of solution
5. "5 health plans" - real validation (not theoretical)

**De-emphasize (normal pace, don't dwell on):**
- The detailed timeline (January → March) - context only
- Technical details (AI on claims + EHR) - not the focus
- Internal steps (coordinators + providers) - outcome matters more

## Eye Contact Practice

### Who to Look At

**Imagine one investor directly in front of you:**
- Look at their eyes/face for 5-7 seconds
- Then "look" at a different investor to your left/right
- Repeat pattern: center, left, right, center

**In video recording:**
- Look at camera 70% of the time (this is like eye contact)
- Look down at script 15% (perfectly normal)
- Look away/thinking 15% (shows you're processing, not robotic)

**What to avoid:**
- ❌ Reading entire script without looking up
- ❌ Staring at one spot (camera/wall)
- ❌ Looking down the entire time

## Body Language Checklist

**Record yourself and check after:**

- [ ] **Posture:** Standing upright or sitting upright? (Confidence = posture)
- [ ] **Hand gestures:** Using hands for emphasis? (2-3 gestures during 60 sec is good)
- [ ] **Fidgeting:** Touching face, hair, jewelry? (Minimize = confidence)
- [ ] **Feet/legs:** Standing still or pacing? (Stillness = control)
- [ ] **Facial expression:** Smiling at opening/closing? (Warmth matters)
- [ ] **Pace:** Stepping back, moving closer? (Movement = engagement)

## Practice Routine (30 minutes total)

### Warm-up (5 minutes)

Before recording, do this:

1. **Read script aloud twice** (full speed, don't worry about timing)
2. **Do tongue twisters:** "Red leather, yellow leather" (3 times) - loosens jaw
3. **Take 3 deep breaths** - calm your nervous system
4. **Stand and stretch** - release tension

---

### Recording Take 1 (5 minutes)

**Purpose:** Just get it on tape, don't worry about perfection

- Start recording
- Read script naturally
- If you mess up: **STOP and restart**, don't try to fix mid-sentence
- Review: Did you get a full take? OK to move on.

---

### Recording Takes 2-5 (20 minutes)

**Purpose:** Refine delivery, find the version that feels most natural

**Each take (4 minutes):**
1. Pause 10 seconds between takes (resets you mentally)
2. Record full 60-second take
3. Review: What was better? What could improve?
4. Make one small adjustment (pacing, emphasis, energy)
5. Record next take

**Adjustments to try across takes:**

| Take | Adjustment | Goal |
|------|-----------|------|
| 1 | Baseline | Just get comfortable with words |
| 2 | Slower on key numbers | Let "$500K-$1M" and "60% vs 85%" land |
| 3 | More energy | Add warmth/confidence to opening |
| 4 | Natural pauses | Pause 2 sec after hook and after problem |
| 5 | Smile during opening/close | Convey friendliness |

## Self-Evaluation Rubric (After Recording)

**Watch/listen to each take and rate 1-5:**

### Clarity (1-5)
- 5: Every word clear, key numbers emphasized, easy to follow
- 3: Mostly clear, some mumbling on details
- 1: Hard to understand, words run together

### Pacing (1-5)
- 5: Varied pace, slows on key numbers, no filler words
- 3: Mostly good, some sections too fast or too slow
- 1: Monotone, talks too fast or too slow throughout

### Confidence (1-5)
- 5: Sounds like you believe this, warm tone, clear ask
- 3: Sounds OK, some uncertainty in middle sections
- 1: Sounds tentative, unsure, asks permission

### Eye Contact (Video only) (1-5)
- 5: Looking at camera 70%+, natural eye movement
- 3: Looking at script frequently, some camera contact
- 1: Never looks at camera, reads entire thing

### Energy (1-5)
- 5: Enthusiastic but professional, smiling at open/close
- 3: Neutral tone, minimal expression
- 1: Flat, sounds bored or stressed

**Total Score:** Add up all 5 categories (25 points max)

- 20-25: Strong take, ready to use
- 15-19: Good take, could improve on 1-2 areas
- 10-14: OK take, needs work on delivery
- Below 10: Try again, focus on clarity and pacing

## What to Look/Listen For

### Signs of a Strong Take

✅ **Clear** - Every number is crisp ($500K, 60%, $1M, $750K)
✅ **Paced** - Varies speed, slows on key numbers, includes natural pauses
✅ **Confident** - Sounds like you own this problem, not seeking permission
✅ **Warm** - Opening/closing has genuine energy, not robotic
✅ **Specific** - Uses exact numbers and timeline, not vague ("about a million")

### Common Issues (and Fixes)

| Issue | What It Sounds Like | Fix |
|-------|-------------------|-----|
| **Too fast** | Rushes through, numbers blur together | Record slower version, use script as pacing guide |
| **Too slow** | Drags, sounds unsure | Add more energy, don't pause between thoughts |
| **Monotone** | All one volume/speed | Emphasize key numbers by slowing down |
| **Umms/Ahhs** | "Um, we predict, uh, gaps 30 days" | Slow down, pause instead of filler words |
| **Robotic** | Sounds like reading script | Take off glasses, practice with script away first |
| **Unsure** | Ends phrases as questions ("$500K to $1M?") | Practice ending sentences with period, not question mark |

## After You Record

### Step 1: Review (5 minutes)

- Watch/listen to all 5 takes
- Rate each using rubric above
- Pick your best 2-3 takes

### Step 2: Share (Optional - 15 minutes)

**Share best 2-3 takes with advisor:**
- Email file(s) or share recording link
- Ask: "Which version felt most convincing? Any feedback?"
- Common advisor feedback:
  - "Slow down on the $1M number"
  - "More energy at the opening"
  - "Sounds too rehearsed, try more natural"

### Step 3: Iterate (5-10 minutes)

Based on feedback:
- Make one small adjustment
- Record 2-3 more takes with that adjustment
- Pick your final version

## Quick Recording Tips

### Best Practices

1. **Record video if possible** (body language matters)
2. **Use natural lighting** (avoid harsh shadows on face)
3. **Quiet space** (minimize background noise)
4. **Phone at eye level** (not looking down/up at camera)
5. **Do multiple takes** (first take rarely best)

### Recording Apps

**On Phone:**
- Built-in camera/video app works fine
- Voice Memos (audio only, if preferred)

**On Computer:**
- QuickTime (Mac) - built in
- Windows Camera (Windows) - built in
- OBS Studio (free, advanced)

### File Format

- Video: MP4 or MOV (share on Google Drive)
- Audio: M4A or MP3 (email to advisor)
