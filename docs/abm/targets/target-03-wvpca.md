## Pain Hypothesis
WVPCA operates in one of the most medically underserved states in the nation, with 515+ CHC sites spanning rural Appalachian communities where broadband connectivity is unreliable and clinical staff are scarce. Their quality improvement challenges are compounded by: (1) chronic disease burden — WV has among the highest rates of diabetes, hypertension, obesity, and opioid use disorder in the country, making HEDIS/UDS measure improvement harder than peer states, (2) multi-EHR fragmentation across a geographically dispersed network with limited IT staff, (3) UDS+ FHIR transition requirements that strain already-thin technology resources. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) a cloud-hosted CQL measure evaluation engine that eliminates the need for on-premise infrastructure at each site, (2) FHIR R4 data normalization that works across heterogeneous EHRs via standard APIs, (3) automated care gap identification for chronic disease measures (diabetes, hypertension, CRC screening) that prioritizes the highest-impact patient outreach, (4) pre-built UDS+ FHIR export capabilities that reduce the technical burden of compliance. The population health management use case is acute — with ~580K patients and the highest chronic disease rates in the region, even incremental quality improvement drives significant health outcomes.
