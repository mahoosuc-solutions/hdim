## Pain Hypothesis
IPHCA is at an inflection point: the November 2024 launch of CINI means they are actively building value-based care infrastructure for the first time at network scale, with 5 MCO payer partnerships (Anthem, CareSource, MHS, MDwise, Humana) that each require distinct quality measure reporting. A brand-new CIN needs: (1) a quality measurement engine that can evaluate HEDIS measures across all 5 payer contracts simultaneously, (2) care gap identification at the patient level to drive VBC performance, (3) data integration across heterogeneous EHRs at member centers to create a unified clinical picture, (4) SDOH data capture aligned with whole-person care measurement goals. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) CQL-based HEDIS measure evaluation that maps to each payer's specific quality requirements, (2) automated care gap detection that enables proactive patient outreach, (3) FHIR R4 data normalization from multiple EHR sources, (4) a quality dashboard that shows CINI performance across all 5 MCO contracts. The timing is ideal — CINI is less than 2 years old and still building its technology stack, creating a greenfield opportunity for HDIM to become the quality measurement backbone.
