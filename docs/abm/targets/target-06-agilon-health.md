## Pain Hypothesis
Agilon's core business challenge is managing quality performance across hundreds of independent PCP groups, each with different EHR systems, clinical workflows, and quality reporting capabilities. The platform's financial model depends entirely on Star ratings — quality bonuses drive the medical margin that determines whether agilon is profitable or not. At 4.2 Stars composite, every tenth of a Star point across 430K+ MA members translates to tens of millions in bonus revenue. The pain is acute because: (1) each new partner group onboarded brings a different EHR and data format that must be normalized for quality measurement, (2) PCP groups need real-time care gap visibility to proactively close gaps before measurement periods end, (3) the company is in a financial turnaround (from -$296M EBITDA to breakeven) where quality improvement is the primary lever, and (4) the CEO departure creates organizational uncertainty that makes reliable, automated quality measurement infrastructure even more critical. HDIM solves this by providing: CQL-native HEDIS measure evaluation that works across any EHR via FHIR R4, real-time care gap identification at the patient level across all partner groups, and a quality dashboard that gives agilon and its PCP partners a unified view of Star rating trajectory. The Minerva/mphrX FHIR platform is a natural integration point — HDIM would consume the normalized FHIR data that Minerva aggregates and apply CQL-based quality measurement on top.
