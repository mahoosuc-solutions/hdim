## Pain Hypothesis
Greater Regional Health represents the classic rural ACO pain point: high VBC ambition constrained by limited technology infrastructure. Their $870K in MSSP shared savings (2019-2022) proves they can deliver quality outcomes, but the APP Plus transition (from CMS Web Interface to eCQM/CQM reporting) dramatically increases reporting complexity. Under the CMS Web Interface, they reported on ~3,300 patients with relatively simple data submission. Under APP Plus (mandatory 2025), they must report eCQMs across their entire attributed population using clinical data from their EHR — a capability most 25-bed rural hospitals simply do not have. The 6 measures in 2025 expand to 8 in 2026, 9 in 2027, and 11 by 2028, creating a growing compliance burden. Meanwhile, anticipated Medicaid cuts (projected impact by Jan 2028) threaten revenue, making shared savings from MSSP even more critical to financial sustainability. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) turnkey CQL-based HEDIS/eCQM evaluation that handles the APP Plus measure set without requiring a dedicated quality informatics team, (2) FHIR R4 integration that works with their existing EHR to extract clinical data for quality reporting, (3) care gap identification for their rural patient population — enabling proactive outreach to close gaps before measurement periods end, and (4) SDOH-aware analytics that account for the food insecurity and transportation barriers affecting their patient population. The small size means fast decision-making (high velocity score: 5) and a manageable pilot scope.
