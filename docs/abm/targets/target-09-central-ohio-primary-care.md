## Pain Hypothesis
COPC is a large, physician-owned primary care group that has committed to full-risk value-based care through its agilon health partnership and ACO REACH participation. Their pain is the gap between clinical excellence and quality measurement infrastructure: they have 300+ providers, 400K+ patients, and a newly implemented Epic EHR, but the transition to APP Plus (mandatory 2025) requires sophisticated eCQM/CQM reporting across their entire attributed population. Under the old CMS Web Interface, a sample-based approach was manageable. Now they must report on every attributed beneficiary — a massive data extraction, aggregation, and evaluation challenge even with Epic. The multi-site nature (70+ practices) means clinical data quality varies across locations, and measure compliance requires consistent care gap identification and closure across the entire network. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) CQL-native HEDIS/eCQM evaluation that operates on FHIR R4 data extracted from Epic — leveraging their recent EHR investment, (2) care gap identification at the patient level across all 70+ practice sites, giving each provider site visibility into their specific gaps, (3) quality dashboards that roll up from practice to group to ACO level — enabling Dr. Deep and the quality leadership to see exactly where interventions are needed, and (4) APP Plus measure set coverage that automatically expands as CMS adds measures (8 in 2026, 9 in 2027, 11 by 2028). The agilon partnership means quality improvement at COPC directly contributes to agilon's platform-level Star rating — creating aligned incentives.
