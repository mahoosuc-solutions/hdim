## Pain Hypothesis
Privia Health is the fastest-growing physician enablement platform in the US, with a $234M shared savings track record and a 9.3% savings rate that leads the industry. Their pain is scaling quality measurement infrastructure to match their explosive growth: the Evolent ACO acquisition (Dec 2025) added 120,000+ attributed lives across 11 new states with different provider networks, EHR systems, and clinical data sources — all of which must be integrated into quality reporting immediately. With 9 ACOs and 1.5M+ attributed lives, the APP Plus transition (mandatory 2025) requires eCQM reporting across every attributed beneficiary in every ACO — a massive data extraction, normalization, and evaluation challenge across hundreds of provider practices using different EHRs. The 11 new states from the Evolent acquisition compound this: each new market has distinct provider networks and clinical data integration requirements. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) CQL-native HEDIS/eCQM evaluation that works across any EHR via FHIR R4 — essential for Privia's multi-EHR environment, (2) scalable quality measurement that handles 1.5M+ attributed lives across 9 ACOs simultaneously, (3) care gap identification at the provider and practice level — enabling Privia's 5,100+ providers to close gaps proactively, (4) APP Plus compliance automation that scales as CMS adds measures (8 in 2026, 9 in 2027, 11 by 2028), and (5) rapid onboarding for new ACO populations like the Evolent acquisition. The financial case is direct: at $234M in shared savings with a 9.3% savings rate, even a 0.5% improvement in quality scores across 1.5M lives would drive $10M+ in incremental shared savings.
