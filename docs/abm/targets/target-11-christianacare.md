## Pain Hypothesis
ChristianaCare is at a critical inflection point: they are simultaneously migrating from Oracle Health to Epic (fall 2026 go-live), expanding into new geographies ($865M investment, PA and southern DE), and deepening their VBC commitments through their Medicaid ACO and Quality Blue programs. The Epic migration creates a once-in-a-decade opportunity for HDIM. During the EHR transition, ChristianaCare will need to rebuild their quality measurement infrastructure on top of the new Epic FHIR R4 data layer. The old Cerner-based quality reporting workflows will not carry over intact. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) a CQL-native HEDIS/eCQM evaluation engine that is EHR-agnostic and can be deployed alongside the Epic migration — giving ChristianaCare quality measurement continuity during the transition, (2) care gap identification across their expanding 3-state footprint (DE + PA + southern DE expansion sites), where clinical data consistency will be a challenge during go-live, (3) Medicaid quality measure coverage that supports their ACO's demonstrated performance improvement trajectory, and (4) APP Plus measure set readiness as CMS expands reporting requirements. The addition of David Nash (population health founder) to the board signals strategic intent to invest in quality measurement infrastructure. Randy Gaboriault — a nationally recognized CIO with deep digital health expertise — is exactly the type of technology leader who would evaluate a platform like HDIM as part of the Epic implementation ecosystem.
