## Pain Hypothesis
Emplify Health (Gundersen + Bellin) is in the most complex moment of its organizational history: integrating two legacy health systems — each with their own Epic builds, clinical workflows, quality measurement processes, and ACO participation — into a single unified entity. The July-August 2025 unified Epic Foundation go-live is a watershed event, but it also means their quality measurement infrastructure is being rebuilt from scratch. Their pain is multi-dimensional: (1) post-merger quality standardization across 11 hospitals and 100+ clinics that previously had different quality measurement baselines, (2) a unified Epic instance that is new to both legacy workforces (staff described significant change management challenges and workflow disruption), (3) MSSP ACO reporting obligations that now span a larger, more geographically dispersed attributed population, and (4) the APP Plus transition that adds complexity to an already stressed quality reporting infrastructure. The heart failure admissions exceeding the national average is a visible quality gap that demonstrates the need for systematic care gap identification. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) a CQL-native quality measurement layer that operates on the new unified Epic FHIR R4 data — giving Emplify a single source of truth for quality across both legacy systems, (2) care gap identification that works consistently across all 100+ clinic locations regardless of which legacy workflows a site used previously, (3) HEDIS/eCQM evaluation that scales from the rural tri-state footprint (where data quality varies) to the urban Green Bay and La Crosse hubs, and (4) APP Plus measure set coverage that grows with CMS requirements while Emplify focuses on operational integration. The physician-led culture (CEO Dr. Rathgaber is a practicing physician) means quality measurement resonates at the leadership level — this is not a system where quality is delegated to a back-office team.
