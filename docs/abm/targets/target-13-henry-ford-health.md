## Pain Hypothesis
Henry Ford Health has a unique pain profile among health systems: they own their own health plan (HAP, 476K members), which means quality performance directly impacts their bottom line through CMS Star rating bonuses. HAP is at 4.0 Stars — solid but not elite. The gap to 4.5 Stars (and maximum MA quality bonuses) represents tens of millions in annual revenue. The drug plan quality score of 3.5 Stars (below Michigan's 3.7 average) is a visible weakness that pulls down overall ratings. For an integrated system that controls both the delivery and insurance sides, every half-star improvement in HEDIS-driven quality measures translates directly to financial return — unlike other health systems that share VBC savings with external payers. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) CQL-native HEDIS evaluation across HAP's 476K member population — identifying the specific care gaps that are depressing their Stars from 4.0 to below 4.5, (2) patient-level care gap identification across 13 hospitals and 250+ outpatient sites — the sheer scale of Henry Ford's delivery network means care gaps are distributed across hundreds of locations, (3) quality dashboards that connect delivery-side performance (HEDIS measures at the clinic level) with plan-side outcomes (Stars ratings at the HAP level), enabling the closed-loop optimization that only an integrated system can achieve, and (4) APP Plus measure set readiness as CMS expands reporting requirements for both their ACO and MA plan. The Henry Ford Innovations arm signals a culture that evaluates and adopts technology — this is not a system that is resistant to platform investments.
