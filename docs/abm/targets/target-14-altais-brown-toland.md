## Pain Hypothesis
Altais / Brown & Toland faces the classic IPA quality measurement challenge at massive scale: 10,000+ providers using heterogeneous EHR systems across California, with quality performance tied directly to capitation rates, shared savings, and IHA performance rankings. Unlike an integrated health system with a single Epic instance, an IPA must aggregate clinical data from hundreds of different physician practice EHRs (Epic, athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, Greenway, etc.) and normalize it for quality measurement. The Pioneer ACO and MSSP ACO participation means they are contractually committed to quality reporting, but the data aggregation challenge grows with every new provider added to the network. The APP Plus transition (mandatory 2025) makes this harder — the shift from CMS Web Interface to eCQM/CQM reporting across the entire attributed population requires consistent data extraction from every practice site. The Abridge AI deployment signals technology appetite and investment capacity, but AI documentation tools address the physician experience problem, not the quality measurement problem. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) a FHIR R4-native quality measurement engine that can ingest data from heterogeneous EHR sources — exactly the challenge an IPA faces, (2) CQL-native HEDIS/eCQM evaluation that standardizes quality measurement regardless of which EHR a provider uses, (3) care gap identification at the individual physician practice level — enabling Altais to show each of their 10,000+ providers exactly where their patients have gaps, which drives IHA performance and capitation rates, and (4) scalability that grows with the network (from Brown & Toland's 2,700 to Altais's 10,000+) without requiring each practice to implement its own quality measurement infrastructure. The Blue Shield founding investment means Altais has payer alignment — Blue Shield's quality expectations flow directly into Altais's network management obligations.
