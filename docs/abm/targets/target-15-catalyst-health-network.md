## Pain Hypothesis
Catalyst Health Network is the largest independent primary care network in Texas (1,000+ providers, 1.5M+ patients), and their full-risk agilon health partnership means quality performance directly determines financial outcomes — under agilon's Total Care Model, the network assumes full capitation risk for Medicare Advantage patients, making every HEDIS measure gap a direct financial loss. Their pain is the classic independent network quality measurement challenge amplified by Texas's scale: 1,000+ practices using different EHRs across a geographic footprint spanning the entire state, with quality data that must be aggregated, normalized, and evaluated consistently to drive care gap closure. Unlike an integrated health system with a single Epic instance, Catalyst must pull clinical data from hundreds of independent practice EHRs and produce reliable quality measurement — and they must do this while the APP Plus transition (mandatory 2025) shifts reporting from sample-based to population-based, dramatically increasing data volume. The agilon partnership intensifies the urgency: agilon's platform economics depend on network-level quality performance, and agilon's reported $132M in shared savings (2022) demonstrates the financial magnitude at stake. HDIM solves this by providing: (1) a FHIR R4-native quality measurement engine that ingests data from any EHR — essential for a heterogeneous independent practice network, (2) CQL-native HEDIS/eCQM evaluation that produces consistent quality measurement across 1,000+ practices regardless of their underlying EHR, (3) patient-level care gap identification that Catalyst can push back to each independent practice — enabling targeted interventions at the point of care, and (4) APP Plus measure set coverage that scales with CMS requirements while Catalyst focuses on network expansion. The Texas market is strategic: it is the second-largest state for MA enrollment, and winning Catalyst would establish HDIM in the fastest-growing VBC market in the country.
