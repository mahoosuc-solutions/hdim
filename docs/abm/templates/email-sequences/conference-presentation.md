# Email Sequence: Conference Presentation

**Trigger:** Decision maker at target organization presents at a healthcare conference (HIMSS, AHIP, RISE, NAACOS, NACHC, state PCA conferences)
**Urgency:** Low — first touch within 30 days of presentation
**Best for archetypes:** All (universal trigger)
**Sequence duration:** 18 days, 3 touches

---

## Personalization Checklist

Before sending, verify each merge field is populated:

- [ ] `[ORG_NAME]` — organization name
- [ ] `[DECISION_MAKER_FIRST]` — recipient first name
- [ ] `[TRIGGER_DETAIL]` — specific presentation (e.g., "your HIMSS session on population health data infrastructure")
- [ ] `[PAIN_HYPOTHESIS]` — from research dossier
- [ ] `[ARCHETYPE_STAT]` — data point from matching archetype
- [ ] `[SENDER_NAME]` — Aaron
- [ ] `[CONFERENCE_NAME]` — name of the conference
- [ ] `[TALK_TOPIC]` — their session title or topic
- [ ] Watch/read the presentation if available (slides, recording, write-up)
- [ ] Note 1-2 specific points they made that connect to HDIM's value prop
- [ ] Check their LinkedIn for post-conference posts or commentary

---

## Touch 1 — Day 0

**Timing:** Within 1 week of presentation (ideally same week while the topic is fresh)

### Subject Line Variants

- **A:** Your [CONFERENCE_NAME] session — one point really resonated
- **B:** [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST], your talk on [TALK_TOPIC] hit on something important
- **C:** Saw your [CONFERENCE_NAME] presentation — a related perspective

### Email Body

> [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST],
>
> I caught your session at [CONFERENCE_NAME] on [TALK_TOPIC]. One thing you said really resonated: [SPECIFIC_POINT_FROM_TALK]. That matches what I am seeing across the organizations I work with.
>
> The challenge you described — [PAIN_HYPOTHESIS] — is one I have been building infrastructure to solve. Not as a concept, but as working technology that quality teams use daily.
>
> I am reaching out because your perspective on this is clearly informed by real operational experience, and I think you would find it interesting to see what we have built. Not a sales conversation — a "here is what is working" conversation.
>
> Would 15 minutes be useful? I can show you a live demo relevant to the challenges you described.
>
> [SENDER_NAME]

**Word count:** ~140

### LinkedIn Action

Same day — like and comment on their conference post or presentation share. Reference a specific point from their talk. Do not pitch.

## Touch 2 — Day 5-7

**Timing:** 5-7 days after Touch 1

### Subject Line Variants

- **A:** Building on your [TALK_TOPIC] theme
- **B:** A data point related to your [CONFERENCE_NAME] session
- **C:** The infrastructure behind what you described

### Email Body

> [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST],
>
> Quick follow-up on my note about your [CONFERENCE_NAME] session.
>
> The challenge you described — the gap between quality aspiration and quality infrastructure — shows up in the numbers consistently.
>
> [ARCHETYPE_STAT]. The organizations closing that gap fastest are the ones investing in real-time quality visibility rather than faster retrospective reporting. It is a different infrastructure, not just a faster version of what exists.
>
> You clearly understand the problem from the operational side. I can show you what the solution side looks like — specific to an organization [ORG_NAME]'s size and complexity.
>
> Fifteen minutes whenever works for you.
>
> [SENDER_NAME]

**Word count:** ~120

### LinkedIn Action

Send connection request: "Hi [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST] — really enjoyed your [CONFERENCE_NAME] session on [TALK_TOPIC]. I work on the infrastructure side of what you described and thought it might be worth connecting."

## Touch 3 — Day 14-18

**Timing:** 14-18 days after Touch 1

### Subject Line Variants

- **A:** 60-second video — what you described, in practice
- **B:** Made this walkthrough — thought of your [CONFERENCE_NAME] talk
- **C:** The infrastructure side of [TALK_TOPIC]

### Email Body

> [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST],
>
> Last note. I recorded a 60-second Loom walkthrough showing working infrastructure related to what you described at [CONFERENCE_NAME]. No slides — just a live demo of real-time quality visibility at [ORG_NAME]'s scale.
>
> Here is the link: [LOOM_LINK]
>
> Your session resonated because you described real problems, not theoretical ones. This walkthrough shows a real solution — the kind of daily quality views that replace the quarterly reporting cycle you were describing.
>
> If it connects, I am happy to do a live deeper dive. If not — I genuinely appreciated the talk.
>
> [SENDER_NAME]
>
> P.S. — If someone on your team is closer to the day-to-day quality infrastructure decisions, feel free to forward this.

**Word count:** ~130

### LinkedIn Action

Share relevant content and tag [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST] if connected. If not, engage with their post-conference content.

---

## Sequence Notes

- **If they reply at any touch:** Stop the sequence immediately.
- **Conference-specific timing:** Touch 1 should land within the week of the conference while the conversation is still active. Waiting 2+ weeks loses the connection to their talk.
- **Specificity matters:** Generic "great talk" emails get deleted. Reference a SPECIFIC point from their presentation. If you did not watch/read it, do not fake it — find the abstract or write-up first.
- **Conference networking context:** Speakers expect to be contacted after conferences. This is the least "cold" of all trigger types. Use that advantage — frame the email as a continuation of the conference conversation, not a cold outreach.
