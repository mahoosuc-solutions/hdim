# Email Sequence: Leadership Change

**Trigger:** New CMO, VP Quality, CIO, or Quality Director announced at target organization
**Urgency:** High — first touch within 2 weeks of announcement
**Best for archetypes:** All (universal trigger — 01 FQHC, 02 MA Plan, 03 ACO, 04 Health System)
**Sequence duration:** 18 days, 3 touches

---

## Personalization Checklist

Before sending, verify each merge field is populated:

- [ ] `[ORG_NAME]` — organization name (confirm exact legal/brand name they use publicly)
- [ ] `[DECISION_MAKER_FIRST]` — recipient first name (verify spelling from LinkedIn)
- [ ] `[TRIGGER_DETAIL]` — specific role and context (e.g., "your appointment as VP of Quality at [ORG_NAME]")
- [ ] `[PAIN_HYPOTHESIS]` — from research dossier (e.g., "quality infrastructure at [ORG_NAME] has been built around manual chart review and quarterly reporting")
- [ ] `[ARCHETYPE_STAT]` — data point from matching archetype (e.g., "health systems your size typically have 15-20% of care gaps that are closed but uncaptured in current systems")
- [ ] `[SENDER_NAME]` — Aaron
- [ ] `[TITLE]` — their new title (exact wording from announcement)
- [ ] Research their background — previous role, published work, conference talks
- [ ] Check if they posted about the new role on LinkedIn
- [ ] Identify what the org's quality posture looked like before their arrival (recent reports, ratings)
- [ ] Note any public statements they have made about priorities in the new role

## Touch 1 — Day 0

**Timing:** Within 1-5 business days of announcement (do not rush — let them settle in)

### Subject Line Variants

- **A:** Congrats on the new role — a resource for your first 90 days
- **B:** [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST], welcome to [ORG_NAME]
- **C:** Quality infrastructure landscape — thought this might help

### Email Body

> [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST],
>
> Congratulations on [TRIGGER_DETAIL]. That is an important role, and the timing is interesting — quality measurement in healthcare is shifting faster than most infrastructure can keep up with.
>
> New leaders in quality and clinical roles often audit their quality infrastructure in the first 90 days. What is our data latency? Where are the manual bottlenecks? How quickly can we identify and close care gaps? Those answers usually shape the improvement roadmap for the next two years.
>
> I work with quality teams at organizations similar to [ORG_NAME] — helping them move from retrospective quality reporting to real-time gap visibility. I am not reaching out to sell anything. I am reaching out because I have context on what is working at similar organizations and I am happy to share it.
>
> If a 15-minute conversation would be useful as you get oriented, I am easy to reach. If the timing is not right, no problem at all — my door stays open.
>
> Welcome aboard, and best of luck in the new role.
>
> [SENDER_NAME]

**Word count:** ~170

### LinkedIn Action

Same day — like their new role announcement post. Leave a brief, genuine congratulatory comment. Do not pitch in the comment. Example: "Congratulations, [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST] — exciting move. [ORG_NAME] is getting a strong leader."

## Touch 2 — Day 5-7

**Timing:** 5-7 days after Touch 1

### Subject Line Variants

- **A:** The first-quarter quality infrastructure question
- **B:** What quality leaders wish they knew on day one
- **C:** Dashboard vs. reality — a common first-quarter discovery

### Email Body

> [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST],
>
> Quick follow-up on my note last week. One thing I consistently hear from quality leaders in their first quarter: the gap between what the dashboard says and what is actually happening clinically.
>
> The dashboard says 72% compliant on a given measure. The clinical reality is that some of those members already had the service but the data has not caught up, some are unreachable, and some just need a phone call this week. But the dashboard does not tell you which is which — and by the time the next data refresh lands, the intervention window has moved.
>
> [PAIN_HYPOTHESIS]. That is a common pattern, and it is solvable — but it requires infrastructure that most organizations have not built yet.
>
> [ARCHETYPE_STAT]. The opportunity is usually larger than expected, and most of it comes down to data latency and gap categorization.
>
> If it would be helpful, I can walk you through what we are seeing at organizations your size — 15 minutes, no strings.
>
> [SENDER_NAME]

**Word count:** ~175

## Sequence Notes

- **If they reply at any touch:** Stop the sequence immediately. Move to direct conversation.
- **If they open but do not reply:** Proceed to the next touch on schedule.
- **If no opens after Touch 3:** Move to nurture track (monthly industry insights, no direct outreach for 60 days).
- **Timing sensitivity:** New leaders are overwhelmed in weeks 1-2. Touch 1 should feel like a resource offer, not a demand on their time. If they just started, consider delaying Touch 1 by a few extra days.
- **Background research matters:** The more you know about their previous role and public priorities, the more credible Touch 1 feels. Reference their background if relevant (e.g., "Given your work at [PREVIOUS_ORG] on population health...").
- **Title matching:** Adjust language based on their specific role. A CIO cares about infrastructure and integration. A CMO cares about clinical outcomes. A VP Quality cares about measure performance and gap closure rates. A Quality Director cares about day-to-day operations and team capacity.
