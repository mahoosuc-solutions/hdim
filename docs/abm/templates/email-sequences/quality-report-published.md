# Email Sequence: Quality Report Published

**Trigger:** Organization publishes annual quality report, HEDIS results, or UDS data showing gaps
**Urgency:** High — first touch within 2 weeks of publication
**Best for archetypes:** 01 (FQHC), 03 (ACO), 04 (Health System)
**Sequence duration:** 18 days, 3 touches

---

## Personalization Checklist

Before sending, verify each merge field is populated:

- [ ] `[ORG_NAME]` — organization name (confirm exact legal/brand name they use publicly)
- [ ] `[DECISION_MAKER_FIRST]` — recipient first name (verify spelling from LinkedIn)
- [ ] `[TRIGGER_DETAIL]` — specific published metric (e.g., "your colorectal cancer screening rate at 58%")
- [ ] `[PAIN_HYPOTHESIS]` — from research dossier (e.g., "your 12 clinic sites report quality data on different cadences")
- [ ] `[ARCHETYPE_STAT]` — data point from matching archetype (e.g., "FQHCs with 8+ sites typically discover 40% of gaps are already addressed but uncaptured")
- [ ] `[SENDER_NAME]` — Aaron
- [ ] Read the actual report — identify 1-2 specific measures to reference
- [ ] Note any measures where they improved (use for positive framing)
- [ ] Check if there is a press release or board presentation accompanying the report
- [ ] Identify the reporting framework (UDS, HEDIS, MSSP, custom)

## Touch 1 — Day 0

**Timing:** Within 1-3 business days of report publication

### Subject Line Variants

- **A:** Read [ORG_NAME]'s quality report — one number stood out
- **B:** Your [SPECIFIC_MEASURE] rate and what it does not show
- **C:** A thought on [ORG_NAME]'s quality numbers

### Email Body

> [DECISION_MAKER_FIRST],
>
> I read [ORG_NAME]'s quality report — [TRIGGER_DETAIL] caught my attention. Not because the number is bad, but because of what it represents: the difference between what your team knows and what the data can actually show at any given point.
>
> The hardest part of quality improvement is not identifying the gaps. It is the lag between when a gap opens and when your team can see it and act. Most organizations are working with data that is 60-90 days old by the time it reaches a quality dashboard. That means your current numbers reflect decisions made months ago, not your team's actual capability.
>
> I have been building infrastructure that closes that lag — giving quality teams real-time visibility into which gaps are open, which are already resolved but uncaptured, and which members need outreach this week.
>
> Would a 15-minute demo be useful? I can show you what [ORG_NAME]-scale quality monitoring looks like when the data is live instead of quarterly.
>
> [SENDER_NAME]

**Word count:** ~165

## Sequence Notes

- **If they reply at any touch:** Stop the sequence immediately. Move to direct conversation.
- **If they open but do not reply:** Proceed to the next touch on schedule.
- **If no opens after Touch 3:** Move to nurture track (monthly industry insights, no direct outreach for 60 days).
- **Report-specific tip:** Reference specific measures from their report, not generic quality language. The more specific, the more credible.
- **UDS vs. HEDIS framing:** For FQHCs, reference UDS measures and HRSA benchmarks. For MA plans, reference HEDIS and Star Ratings. For ACOs, reference MSSP quality gates. Match their language.
