## Script

### Opening Hook (0:00-0:08)
"If your specialty network has 40 different payer contracts and every one of them defines quality differently, you're probably managing 40 separate quality programs with one quality manager. Let me show you why that's actually 5 programs."

### Dashboard Overview (0:08-0:25)
"Here's the contract alignment matrix. Forty payer contracts as rows, quality measures as columns. And look at this — Anthem, Aetna, United, Humana, and your largest Medicaid MCO all measure statin therapy for cardiovascular disease. Different contract language, same underlying HEDIS measure. Your network-wide rate is 71%. That's one number, calculated once, applicable to 32 of your 40 contracts. Five measures cover 80% of your contracts."

### The Key Insight (0:25-0:45)
"Now here's your biggest gap. Cardiac rehab referral rate — 34%. The target across your payer contracts is 60%. And 28 of your 40 payers include it in their quality incentive calculations. But HDIM found 890 patients who had a qualifying cardiac event in the last 12 months with no rehab referral on file. 340 of them have follow-up appointments in the next 30 days. If your cardiologists add a referral at those visits, your rate jumps from 34% to 53% — zero additional patient outreach."

### Action View (0:45-0:60)
"And here's the view your referring PCPs need to see. Your network versus the hospital-owned cardiology group, side by side. Your statin therapy rate is 71% versus their 68%. Cost per CHF admission — you're at $18,400, they're at $21,200. You're winning on quality and cost. You just couldn't prove it. This is the PDF that goes to every referring PCP in your network."

### Closing CTA (0:60-0:75)
"Want to see how your contracts map? I can normalize your payer agreements into common measures and show you which 5 matter most. Takes about 15 minutes. Just reply and we'll set it up."

## Personalization Notes
- If customizing for a specific target, replace [ORG_NAME] with their network name
- Adjust [POPULATION_SIZE] to match their patient base (5K-30K range); gap counts scale proportionally
- For non-cardiology networks (oncology, behavioral health, nephrology), swap the specialty-specific measures:
  - **Behavioral health:** Depression screening, follow-up visit rates, medication adherence, SUD treatment engagement
  - **Oncology:** Screening rates, time-to-treatment, clinical trial enrollment, survivorship care plans
  - **Nephrology:** CKD monitoring, vascular access, dialysis adequacy, transplant referral
- If their trigger is referral volume loss, lead with the Network Value Dashboard comparison instead of the contract matrix
- For IPA structures, emphasize the cross-practice benchmarking (Dr. Patel 89 open loops vs Dr. Chen 12)
