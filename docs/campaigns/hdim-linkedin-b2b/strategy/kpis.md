# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): HDIM LinkedIn Campaign
**Campaign Duration**: 90 days
**Platform**: LinkedIn
**Primary Objective**: Generate qualified leads for sales team

---

## KPI Hierarchy

```
PRIMARY KPIs (What We're Optimizing For)
├─ Cost Per Lead (CPL)
├─ Lead Quality Score
└─ Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

SECONDARY KPIs (How We're Performing)
├─ Click-Through Rate (CTR)
├─ Lead Gen Form Completion Rate
├─ Demo Booking Rate
└─ Demo-to-Close Rate

TERTIARY KPIs (Supporting Metrics)
├─ Impressions
├─ Cost Per Click (CPC)
├─ Frequency
└─ Engagement Rate
```

---

## Primary KPIs (Campaign Success Metrics)

### 1. Cost Per Lead (CPL)

**Definition**: Total ad spend ÷ Total leads generated

**Target Benchmarks**:
| Phase | Target CPL | Max Acceptable | Excellent Performance |
|-------|-----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Month 1 (Learning) | $200-250 | $300 | <$180 |
| Month 2 (Scaling) | $180-220 | $280 | <$160 |
| Month 3 (Optimization) | $165-200 | $250 | <$150 |
| **90-Day Average** | **$165-$200** | **$250** | **<$150** |

**Why This Matters**: CPL directly impacts ROI. At $50K average deal size and 10% lead-to-close rate, each lead is worth $5,000. A $200 CPL = 25x ROI.

**Weekly Tracking**:
```
Week 1: Expected $300-400 (small sample size)
Week 2: Expected $250-300 (optimization starting)
Week 3-4: Expected $200-250 (getting efficient)
Week 5-8: Expected $180-220 (scaled efficiency)
Week 9-12: Expected $165-200 (fully optimized)
```

**Action Triggers**:
- ✅ **CPL <$180**: Increase budget 20%, scale aggressively
- ⚠️ **CPL $180-250**: Monitor closely, optimize targeting
- ❌ **CPL >$250 for 7+ days**: Pause campaign, diagnose issues
- 🚨 **CPL >$350 for 3+ days**: Emergency stop, full review

---

### 2. Lead Quality Score

**Definition**: % of leads that are Sales Accepted Leads (SAL)

**Qualification Criteria** (Must meet 3 of 4):
1. ✅ Works at hospital/health system with 200+ employees
2. ✅ Job title: Director level or above (Director, VP, C-suite)
3. ✅ Manages IT systems, clinical systems, or EHR
4. ✅ Has budget authority or strong influence

**Target Benchmarks**:
| Phase | Target Quality | Min Acceptable | Excellent |
|-------|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| Month 1 | 65-75% | 60% | >80% |
| Month 2 | 70-80% | 65% | >85% |
| Month 3 | 70-85% | 70% | >90% |

**Weekly Tracking**: Sales team tags each lead as:
- **A-Tier** (Perfect fit): CIO/CMIO at 500+ employee hospital
- **B-Tier** (Good fit): Director/VP at 200-500 employee hospital
- **C-Tier** (Marginal): IT Manager or non-decision maker
- **D-Tier** (Unqualified): Wrong industry, too small, wrong role

**Target Distribution**:
- A-Tier: 30-40%
- B-Tier: 30-40%
- C-Tier: 15-25%
- D-Tier: <10%

**Quality Score** = (A-Tier + B-Tier) ÷ Total Leads

**Action Triggers**:
- ✅ **Quality >80%**: Targeting is excellent, consider expanding
- ⚠️ **Quality 60-70%**: Acceptable, minor targeting tweaks
- ❌ **Quality <60%**: Tighten targeting immediately
  - Exclude job titles: Manager, Coordinator, Analyst
  - Increase company size minimum to 500 employees
  - Add seniority filter: Director+ only

---

### 3. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Definition**: Total campaign spend ÷ Total customers closed

**Calculation**:
```
CAC = Total Spend ÷ Closed Deals
    = $10,000 ÷ 5-8 closed deals
    = $1,250 - $2,000 per customer
```

**Target Benchmarks**:
| Metric | Target | Max Acceptable | Excellent |
|--------|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **CAC** | $1,250-$2,000 | $2,500 | <$1,200 |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | 25:1 to 40:1 | 20:1 | >40:1 |
| **Months to Payback** | 0.5-1 month | 2 months | <0.5 months |

**Why This Matters**: At $50K average deal size:
- $1,250 CAC = **40x ROI** (excellent)
- $2,000 CAC = **25x ROI** (good)
- $2,500 CAC = **20x ROI** (acceptable)
- $3,000+ CAC = **<17x ROI** (concerning)

**Tracking**:
- **Real-time CAC estimate**: Total Spend ÷ (Leads × 30% Demo Rate × 35% Close Rate)
- **Actual CAC**: Only calculable after sales cycle completes (30-60 days)

**Action Triggers**:
- ✅ **CAC <$1,500**: Proof of excellent channel fit, scale aggressively
- ⚠️ **CAC $1,500-2,500**: Monitor, optimize where possible
- ❌ **CAC >$2,500**: Review entire funnel (lead quality, sales process, product-market fit)

## Secondary KPIs (Performance Indicators)

### 4. Click-Through Rate (CTR)

**Definition**: (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100

**Target Benchmarks by Ad Type**:
| Ad Type | Target CTR | Min Acceptable | Excellent |
|---------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| Lead Gen Forms | 0.60-0.80% | 0.45% | >0.90% |
| Sponsored Content | 0.50-0.70% | 0.35% | >0.80% |
| Sponsored InMail | 3.0-5.0% | 2.5% | >6.0% |
| Document Ads | 0.40-0.60% | 0.30% | >0.70% |

**Industry Context**: LinkedIn B2B healthcare IT average CTR is 0.45-0.60%, so we're targeting above-average performance.

**Action Triggers**:
- ✅ **CTR >0.70%**: Winning ad, scale budget
- ⚠️ **CTR 0.40-0.60%**: Acceptable, test headline variants
- ❌ **CTR <0.40% after 5,000 impressions**: Pause ad, replace creative
  - Test new headline
  - Test new visual
  - Test different value proposition

**CTR Optimization Levers**:
1. **Headline**: Test benefit-driven vs. curiosity-driven
2. **Visual**: Test people vs. data visualization
3. **First line of body**: Hook attention in first 50 characters
4. **Social proof**: Add "Join 50+ hospitals using HDIM"

---

### 5. Lead Gen Form Completion Rate

**Definition**: (Form Submissions ÷ Form Opens) × 100

**Target Benchmarks**:
| Phase | Target Rate | Min Acceptable | Excellent |
|-------|------------|----------------|-----------|
| Month 1 | 35-45% | 30% | >50% |
| Month 2 | 40-50% | 35% | >55% |
| Month 3 | 40-55% | 40% | >60% |

**Why This Matters**: LinkedIn pre-fills name, title, company, so completion rate should be high. Low completion = form is asking too many questions or CTA is weak.

**Current Form Fields** (7 total):
1. First Name (pre-filled)
2. Last Name (pre-filled)
3. Work Email (pre-filled)
4. Company (pre-filled)
5. Job Title (pre-filled)
6. Current EHR System (dropdown: Epic, Cerner, AllScripts, Other)
7. Biggest Integration Challenge (optional text)

**Action Triggers**:
- ✅ **Completion >50%**: Form is optimized
- ⚠️ **Completion 30-50%**: Acceptable, test removing optional field
- ❌ **Completion <30%**: Too much friction
  - Remove custom questions (#6 and #7)
  - Test thank-you page copy (is offer compelling?)
  - Check mobile experience (50%+ traffic is mobile)

---

### 6. Demo Booking Rate

**Definition**: (Demos Booked ÷ Total Leads) × 100

**Target Benchmarks**:
| Phase | Target Rate | Min Acceptable | Excellent |
|-------|------------|----------------|-----------|
| Month 1 | 25-30% | 20% | >35% |
| Month 2 | 30-35% | 25% | >40% |
| Month 3 | 30-40% | 28% | >45% |

**Why This Matters**: This is a sales team KPI but directly impacts campaign ROI. Low demo rate = poor lead quality OR poor sales follow-up.

**Diagnostic Questions (if <25%)**:
1. **Lead Quality**: Are leads actually qualified? (Check Quality Score)
2. **Sales Follow-Up**: How fast is sales team responding?
   - Target: Within 24 hours
   - Ideal: Within 4 hours (3x higher conversion)
3. **Demo Friction**: Is scheduling easy?
   - Use Calendly link in first email
   - Offer multiple time slots
4. **Value Proposition**: Is demo compelling?
   - Position as "15-min solution overview" not "60-min sales pitch"

**Action Triggers**:
- ✅ **Demo Rate >35%**: Excellent lead quality + sales execution
- ⚠️ **Demo Rate 20-30%**: Standard B2B SaaS, room for improvement
- ❌ **Demo Rate <20%**: Investigate root cause:
  - If lead quality <70% → Tighten targeting
  - If sales response time >24 hrs → Improve SLA
  - If both are good → Re-evaluate demo offer/positioning

---

### 7. Demo-to-Close Rate

**Definition**: (Closed Deals ÷ Demos Completed) × 100

**Target Benchmarks**:
| Metric | Target | Min Acceptable | Excellent |
|--------|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Demo-to-Close | 30-40% | 25% | >45% |
| Sales Cycle | 30-60 days | 90 days | <30 days |

**Why This Matters**: This validates product-market fit. If demo-to-close <25%, the problem isn't the ad campaign—it's product, pricing, or sales execution.

**Industry Benchmarks** (B2B healthcare IT):
- Best-in-class: 40-50% demo-to-close
- Above average: 30-40%
- Average: 20-30%
- Below average: <20%

**Action Triggers**:
- ✅ **Close Rate >35%**: Strong product-market fit, scale campaign
- ⚠️ **Close Rate 25-35%**: Acceptable, focus on sales enablement
- ❌ **Close Rate <25%**: Product or pricing issue (not ad campaign problem)
  - Review demo script
  - Analyze lost deal reasons
  - Re-evaluate pricing vs. competitors

## Tertiary KPIs (Supporting Metrics)

### 8. Impressions

**Target**: 175,000 - 225,000 over 90 days (for $10K budget)

**Weekly Target**: ~6,000 - 7,500 per week

**Why We Track It**: Ensures sufficient reach. Too few impressions = targeting too narrow. Too many impressions = poor CTR.

---

### 9. Cost Per Click (CPC)

**Target**: $8-15 per click

**Why We Track It**: CPC × Conversion Rate = CPL. If CPC is high but conversion rate is also high, that's acceptable.

**Benchmarks**:
- Lead Gen Forms: $10-15 CPC
- Sponsored Content: $8-12 CPC
- InMail: N/A (cost per send, not per click)

---

### 10. Frequency

**Target**: <3.0 impressions per user

**Why We Track It**: Frequency >3.5 = ad fatigue (CTR declines, CPL increases). Pause and rotate creative when frequency >4.

---

### 11. Engagement Rate (Sponsored Content Only)

**Definition**: (Likes + Comments + Shares + Clicks) ÷ Impressions

**Target**: 1.5-3.0%

**Why We Track It**: High engagement = resonating with audience. Bonus: organic reach from shares.

## Red Flags / Warning Signs

**Immediate Action Required** if you see:

| Warning Sign | Likely Cause | Immediate Action |
|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| CPL >$400 for 5+ days | Poor targeting or weak creative | Pause underperforming ads, tighten targeting |
| CTR <0.30% for 3+ days | Creative not resonating | Test new headline/visual |
| Form Completion <25% | Form too long or offer weak | Remove custom questions |
| Lead Quality <50% | Targeting too broad | Exclude Managers, increase company size |
| Demo Rate <15% | Sales follow-up slow | Implement 4-hour response SLA |
| Frequency >4 | Ad fatigue | Pause creative, rotate new ads |
| Zero leads after $500 spend | Fundamental issue | Emergency stop, full diagnostic |
