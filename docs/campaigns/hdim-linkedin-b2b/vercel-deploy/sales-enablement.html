Objection Handling

Common objections and suggested responses

We already have an integration engine

Response

Your integration engine moves data, but does it understand healthcare? HDIM isn't just another interface engine - it's a healthcare intelligence layer that normalizes data to FHIR, automatically identifies care gaps, and calculates quality measures. Your Rhapsody/MuleSoft moves HL7 messages; HDIM makes that data actionable for clinical and quality outcomes.

Our EHR vendor offers similar features

Response

EHR-native tools only see data in that EHR. Your patients receive care across your network, external specialists, labs, pharmacies, and other facilities. HDIM aggregates ALL that data into a unified view. Plus, EHR vendor roadmaps are driven by their priorities, not yours - with HDIM, you control your data strategy.

This seems expensive for what it does

Response

Let's look at what you're spending today: $910K average on integration maintenance, $5-7M in missed quality incentives, $340K-780K in prior auth delays. HDIM typically delivers 327% ROI over 3 years. The question isn't whether you can afford HDIM - it's whether you can afford to keep losing that revenue.

We don't have IT bandwidth for another project

Response

That's exactly why you need HDIM. Your IT team is spending 40% of their time on integration maintenance. HDIM's pre-built connectors and managed service model mean we handle the heavy lifting. Most customers deploy in 4-8 weeks with minimal IT involvement. And once live, your team's maintenance burden drops dramatically.

What about security and HIPAA compliance?

Response

Security is built into our architecture, not bolted on. We're SOC 2 Type II ready with zero security bypasses - no demo modes, no backdoors. Full HIPAA compliance with 6-year audit logs, AES-256 encryption, and multi-tenant isolation. We'll sign a BAA today. Check our security case study for the technical details.

Can you really deploy in 4-8 weeks?

Response

Yes, and here's why: pre-built connectors for Epic, Cerner, and other major EHRs; standardized FHIR-based integration patterns; cloud-native deployment; and a proven implementation methodology. We're not building from scratch - we're configuring a platform that's already built for healthcare.
