# Commercial License Terms Outline (BSL Conversion Model)

Status: Draft for counsel review.  
Date: 2026-03-07  
Product: HealthData-in-Motion (HDIM)

## 1. Purpose

This outline defines commercial terms for production use rights not granted under
the repository BSL 1.1 license.

## 2. Scope of Commercial Grant

Commercial license grants customer rights to:

- Use HDIM in production for internal business operations.
- Operate HDIM as hosted or managed service for approved affiliates or clients
  if the applicable order form includes service-provider rights.
- Receive updates, patches, and support according to purchased tier.

Commercial license does not grant:

- Ownership of HDIM intellectual property.
- Redistribution of source code outside customer organization except approved
  contractors under confidentiality.
- Rights to use Grateful House trademarks except as allowed in agreement.

## 3. Licensing Model

Recommended structure:

- Annual subscription, per legal entity, with environment limits.
- Optional usage add-ons (tenants, users, transactions, or covered lives).
- Optional hosted/SaaS rider if customer provides service to third parties.

## 4. Commercial Options Matrix (Planning)

Use these option groups to build deal-specific order forms.

### A) Rights Model Options

- `Option A1: Internal Use Only`
  - Production rights for customer's internal operations.
  - No rights to provide service to third parties.
- `Option A2: Affiliate Use`
  - Internal use plus named affiliate entities.
  - Requires affiliate schedule in order form.
- `Option A3: Service Provider Rights`
  - Customer may provide HDIM-enabled services to third parties.
  - Requires explicit service-provider rider and higher support tier.

### B) Deployment Responsibility Options

- `Option B1: Customer-Hosted`
  - Customer runs infrastructure and operations.
  - Licensor provides software/support only.
- `Option B2: Shared Responsibility`
  - Customer-hosted with licensor implementation/operational support.
- `Option B3: Licensor-Hosted` (future/optional)
  - Requires SLA, uptime credits, and data-processing terms.

### C) Support Options

- `Option C1: Standard`
  - Business-hours support and standard response targets.
- `Option C2: Priority`
  - Faster response targets and named escalation.
- `Option C3: Enterprise`
  - Priority + governance cadence + incident review support.

## 5. Packaging (Initial)

- `Starter`: Internal production use, business-hours support.
- `Growth`: Internal production use, priority support, higher scale limits.
- `Enterprise`: Internal + approved external service rights, SLA terms, security
  and procurement support.

All package names and limits are commercial placeholders pending sales feedback.

## 6. Pricing Grouping Strategy (Commercial)

Define pricing by one primary metric plus optional add-ons.

### Recommended Primary Grouping (Healthcare Fit)

- `Covered Lives Banding` (recommended default)
  - Band 1: up to 25K lives
  - Band 2: 25K-100K lives
  - Band 3: 100K-250K lives
  - Band 4: 250K+ lives

### Alternate Groupings (use when needed)

- `Tenant/Entity Count`
  - Useful for multi-tenant service providers.
- `Environment/Instance Count`
  - Useful for enterprise IT procurement and segmentation.
- `Event/Transaction Volume`
  - Useful for high-throughput event processing use cases.

### Add-On Groupings

- Compliance add-ons (BAA workflow support, audit support package)
- Advanced support add-ons (24x7, incident commander access)
- Implementation bundles (migration, integration, onboarding)
- Commercial rights add-ons (affiliate expansion, service-provider rider)

## 7. Support and Service Levels

Base support policy:

- Critical incidents: target initial response within 4 business hours.
- High severity: next business day response target.
- Medium/low: standard queue response targets.

Optional enterprise SLA addendum:

- Uptime commitment (if hosted by licensor).
- Credits/remedies structure.
- Named escalation contacts.

## 8. Security and Compliance Terms

Include:

- Security incident notification obligations.
- Vulnerability disclosure channel and patch policy alignment.
- Data handling and confidentiality obligations.
- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) availability when required by deployment
  model and customer responsibilities.

## 9. Option-to-Package Mapping (Recommended)

- `Starter`
  - Rights: A1
  - Deployment: B1 or B2
  - Support: C1
- `Growth`
  - Rights: A1 or A2
  - Deployment: B1 or B2
  - Support: C2
- `Enterprise`
  - Rights: A2 or A3
  - Deployment: B1/B2/B3
  - Support: C3

## 10. Commercial Restrictions and Guardrails

- No sublicensing except explicit reseller/OEM terms.
- No removal of copyright or attribution notices.
- No use in violation of law or regulated data rules.
- No benchmark publication without written consent (optional policy choice).

## 11. Renewal and Termination

- Initial term: 12 months unless otherwise agreed.
- Auto-renewal option with notice window.
- Suspension rights for material non-payment or material breach.
- Termination for cause with cure period.
- Post-termination wind-down and data export rights.

## 12. Legal Terms Baseline

- Governing law and venue.
- Limitation of liability and warranty disclaimers.
- Indemnity structure (IP + third-party claims).
- Export controls and sanctions compliance.
- Assignment/change-of-control language.

## 13. Order of Documents

Recommended precedence:

3. Security/BAA Addenda
