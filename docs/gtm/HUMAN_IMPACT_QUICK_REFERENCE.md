## The Three Patients (30-Second Summaries)

### Maria (Diabetes)
**Setup:** 67-year-old grandmother. Last A1C test: 14 months ago. No one noticed.
**Without HDIM:** ICU admission. $43,000 bill. Permanent kidney damage. Can't read to grandchildren.
**With HDIM:** $120 lab test. Medication adjustment. Back in the garden.
**Quotable:** "The gap between a $45 lab test and a $43,000 hospitalization is 14 months of silence."

### James (Depression)
**Setup:** 42-year-old widowed father. Two ER visits for "chest pain." Zero mental health screens.
**Without HDIM:** Suicide attempt. Psychiatric hospitalization. $85,000 bill. Custody battle.
**With HDIM:** 30-second PHQ-2 in ER. Depression treated. Running 5Ks with his kids.
**Quotable:** "A PHQ-2 takes 30 seconds. James spent 20 months suffering because nobody had 30 seconds."

### Sarah (Breast Cancer)
**Setup:** 55-year-old triathlete. "Too busy" for mammograms. Three years overdue.
**Without HDIM:** Stage 2B diagnosis. Mastectomy. Chemotherapy. $239,000. Life changed forever.
**With HDIM:** Mobile mammogram in her office parking lot. Stage 0. Lumpectomy. Still racing.
**Quotable:** "The difference between Stage 0 and Stage 2B isn't months. It's millimeters -- and a system that doesn't give up."

## The 5-Minute Story (Elevator Pitch)

**30 Seconds:**
"On Christmas night 2025, we discovered patient data was sitting in our cache for 24 hours after access. We could have left it -- faster performance, lower costs. Instead, we reduced it to 5 minutes. We documented exactly why. That commit defines who we are."

**60 Seconds:**
"Most healthcare platforms cache patient data for hours or days. Faster responses, better demos, easier sales. We cache for 5 minutes maximum. It costs us performance. It complicates our architecture. It increases our infrastructure expenses. We do it anyway because somewhere there's a domestic abuse survivor whose ex-partner works in healthcare IT. Somewhere there's a teenager whose depression diagnosis could affect their future. Their data leaves our system in 5 minutes because their privacy matters more than our latency metrics."

**The Line:**
"5 minutes is our cache TTL. It's also our answer to 'can I trust these people with my patients?'"

## Story Hooks by Objection

### "We already have care gap detection."
"Do you detect care gaps, or do you close them? Maria's health system had her in a database. They just didn't have anyone calling her for 14 months."

### "Our members don't respond to outreach."
"Sarah ignored three emails and two text messages. The fourth call came at 7 AM and offered a mammogram van in her office parking lot. She said yes. The question isn't whether members respond. It's whether we're creative enough in how we reach them."

### "We can't afford another platform."
"James had two ER visits for chest pain before his suicide attempt. Total cost: $93,100. A PHQ-2 screening costs $25. What can you afford NOT to do?"

### "Implementation is too complex."
"The 5-minute cache decision was made on Christmas night. By December 29, it was in production with 750 lines of documentation. We move fast when patient safety is at stake."

### "How is this different from [competitor]?"
"Ask them about their cache TTL for PHI. Ask them for the documentation. Ask them to show you the commit history. We'll show you ours."
