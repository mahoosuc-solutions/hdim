# Battle Card: HDIM vs Epic Healthy Planet

## Quick Reference

| Aspect | HDIM | Epic Healthy Planet |
|--------|------|---------------------|
| **Focus** | Multi-EHR Quality | Epic-only PHM |
| **Pricing** | $80-150/mo (Docker) | $500K-$1M+ add-on |
| **Implementation** | 90 days | 18-24 months |
| **EHR Support** | Any EHR | Epic only |

---

## Win Themes

### 1. Open Platform
"Healthy Planet only works within Epic's walls. What about your affiliated clinics on Athena, Greenway, or Cerner? HDIM unifies quality across ALL EHRs."

### 2. Network-Wide Quality
"ACOs and health systems have multi-vendor environments. HDIM provides a single quality view across your entire network."

### 3. Faster, Cheaper
"Healthy Planet adds 18-24 months to Epic implementations at $500K+. HDIM deploys in 90 days, independently."

## Competitive Weaknesses (Epic)

| Weakness | Impact | HDIM Advantage |
|----------|--------|----------------|

| 18-24 month implementation | Slow time-to-value | 90-day deployment |

## Objection Handling

### "We're an Epic shop - makes sense to use Healthy Planet"
**Response:** "For facilities on Epic, Healthy Planet has value. But what about your network? Most ACOs have 30-50% of providers on non-Epic systems. HDIM aggregates across all EHRs - giving you complete quality visibility."

### "Epic integration is seamless"
**Response:** "Within Epic, yes. But HDIM integrates with Epic via FHIR AND brings in data from every other EHR. You get Epic integration plus network-wide coverage."

### "We've already invested in Epic"
**Response:** "HDIM complements Epic - it doesn't replace it. Use Epic for clinical workflows, HDIM for network-wide quality aggregation. Many Epic customers use HDIM for their multi-vendor quality reporting."

## Proof Points

- Works with 50+ EHR systems via FHIR
- 90-day deployment vs 18-24 months
- No Epic license required
- Multi-EHR quality dashboards
- Lower cost than Epic add-on modules

## Ideal Customer Profile

**Best fit for HDIM over Epic:**
- ACOs with multi-EHR provider networks
- Health systems with non-Epic affiliates
- Organizations with capacity constraints for Epic projects
- Those needing quality reporting before Epic is fully deployed
- Regional HIEs with diverse EHR landscapes
