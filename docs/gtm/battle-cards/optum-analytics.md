# Battle Card: HDIM vs Optum Analytics

## Quick Reference

| Aspect | HDIM | Optum Analytics |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| **Focus** | Provider-friendly Quality | Payer-owned Analytics |
| **Pricing** | $80-150/mo (Docker) | $500K-$2M+/year |
| **Implementation** | 90 days | 12-18 months |
| **Ownership** | Independent | UnitedHealth Group |

---

## Win Themes

### 1. Provider Neutrality
"Optum is owned by UnitedHealth Group - the largest payer in the country. Do you want your quality data flowing to a competitor?"

### 2. 60-80% Lower Cost
"Optum's enterprise pricing starts at $500K+. HDIM delivers comparable quality automation at a fraction of the cost."

### 3. No Conflict of Interest
"HDIM is provider-friendly. We're not using your data to inform payer strategies."

| Weakness | Impact | HDIM Advantage |

## Discovery Questions

1. "How do you feel about your quality data going through a payer-owned platform?"
2. "What concerns do you have about data being used for payer strategies?"
3. "What's your budget for quality analytics? (probe for <$500K)"
4. "How important is vendor independence to your organization?"
5. "Do you work with multiple payers beyond UHC?"

## Landmines to Plant

- "Ask Optum who owns the insights generated from your data."
- "Request clarification on data sharing between Optum and UnitedHealthcare."
- "Inquire about their pricing for organizations under 50K members."
- "Ask about non-UHC payer integrations."

---

## Objection Handling

### "Optum is the industry standard"
**Response:** "Optum has scale, but they're payer-owned. For providers and ACOs, there's growing concern about data flowing to a payer subsidiary. HDIM offers comparable capabilities with complete independence."

### "We already work with UnitedHealthcare"
**Response:** "That's exactly why you need an independent quality platform. Having your quality data controlled by your payer creates dependencies and potential conflicts. HDIM gives you leverage in negotiations."

### "Optum has the most comprehensive data"
**Response:** "Their data advantage comes from payer claims. HDIM focuses on clinical quality from your EHRs - data you control. Plus, HDIM integrates claims data from any payer, not just UHC."

---

## Proof Points

- Independent platform with no payer ownership
- FHIR-native for true interoperability
- Multi-payer claims integration
- 60-80% lower TCO than Optum
- Mental health screening (unique feature)

## Ideal Customer Profile

**Best fit for HDIM over Optum:**
- Provider organizations (ACOs, health systems)
- Organizations working with multiple payers
- Privacy/data sovereignty concerns
- Mid-market organizations (<100K members)
- Organizations seeking payer negotiation leverage
