## Part 1: Understanding Star Ratings

### The Star Ratings System

Medicare Advantage Star ratings determine quality bonus payments worth billions annually across the industry.

**Rating Distribution (2025):**
| Star Rating | Plans (%) | Bonus Status |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| 5.0 Stars | 6% | Maximum bonus (5%) |
| 4.5 Stars | 11% | Maximum bonus (5%) |
| 4.0 Stars | 31% | Partial bonus (3%) |
| 3.5 Stars | 28% | No bonus |
| 3.0 Stars | 15% | No bonus |
| < 3.0 Stars | 9% | Penalty risk |

### Financial Impact by Rating

For a 100,000-member Medicare Advantage plan:

| Rating Change | Annual Bonus Impact |
|---------------|---------------------|
| 3.5 → 4.0 | +$25-35M |
| 4.0 → 4.5 | +$15-25M |
| 4.5 → 5.0 | +$5-10M |
| 3.0 → 3.5 | Avoids sanction risk |

**The math is stark:** A single half-star improvement can fund your entire quality program many times over.

### Star Rating Categories

**Part C Measures (60% weight):**
- Staying healthy (screenings, vaccinations)
- Managing chronic conditions
- Member experience (CAHPS)
- Member complaints
- Health plan responsiveness

**Part D Measures (40% weight):**
- Drug plan member experience
- Drug pricing and patient safety
- Medication adherence

### 2025 Star Rating Changes

**Key Updates:**
- Health Equity Index (HEI) incorporated
- Medication adherence measures triple-weighted
- CAHPS weighting increased
- New behavioral health measures
- Guardrails on measure dropping

## Part 7: Medication Adherence Deep Dive

### Why Medication Adherence Matters Most

**Triple-weighted in Star ratings** - These three measures can account for 15-20% of total Star score.

**The PDC Challenge:**
- Members often don't fill prescriptions
- Gaps occur throughout the year
- Early intervention is critical
- Year-end "cramming" doesn't work

### PDC Calculation

**Proportion of Days Covered (PDC):**
```
PDC = Days Covered by Medication / Days in Measurement Period × 100
```

**Threshold:** PDC ≥ 80% = Compliant

**Example:**
- Measurement period: 365 days
- Days covered: 290 days
- PDC: 290/365 = 79.5% → Non-compliant

### Adherence Intervention Strategy

**Monthly PDC Tracking:**
| Month | Target PDC | Intervention Trigger |
|-------|------------|---------------------|
| Month 1-3 | 80%+ | None if on track |
| Month 4-6 | 75%+ | Reminder outreach |
| Month 7-9 | 70%+ | Pharmacist MTM |
| Month 10-12 | 65%+ | Intensive outreach |

**Intervention Waterfall:**
1. **Automated refill reminders** - All members
2. **90-day supply conversion** - Members on 30-day fills
3. **Mail-order promotion** - Members using retail
4. **Pharmacist outreach** - PDC < 75% at month 6
5. **Care manager engagement** - PDC < 70% at month 9
6. **Provider notification** - Non-responsive members

### HDIM Medication Adherence Features

- Real-time PDC calculation from pharmacy claims
- Member-level adherence dashboards
- Automated intervention triggering
- Pharmacist worklist generation
- Provider alerts for non-adherent patients
- Outcome tracking and reporting

## Part 8: CAHPS Strategy

### The CAHPS Challenge

CAHPS measures member experience through surveys. Unlike clinical measures, you can't directly control outcomes—but you can influence them.

**Key Insight:** CAHPS surveys go to members in late winter for prior year. Experiences from January-December drive responses in March.

### CAHPS Risk Identification

**Members at risk of negative survey response:**
- Recent claim denial
- Long prior authorization wait
- Multiple customer service contacts
- Recent complaint or grievance
- Negative care experience indicators

**HDIM Approach:**
1. Aggregate experience indicators from multiple sources
2. Score members on CAHPS risk
3. Proactive outreach to at-risk members
4. Resolve issues before survey season

## Part 10: Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Year-End Focus

**Problem:** Quality push starts in Q4.

**Reality:** Most measures require 12 months of opportunity. Year-end sprints can't close gaps from Q1.

**Solution:** Year-round quality management with monthly progress reviews.

### Pitfall 2: Provider Data Lag

**Problem:** Providers get gap lists quarterly.

**Reality:** By the time gaps are identified and distributed, opportunities are missed.

**Solution:** Real-time gap lists accessible to providers daily.

### Pitfall 3: Ignoring Medication Adherence

**Problem:** Focus on clinical measures, ignore pharmacy.

**Reality:** Medication adherence is triple-weighted and highly improvable.

**Solution:** Dedicated adherence program with real-time PDC tracking.

### Pitfall 4: Member Experience Afterthought

**Problem:** CAHPS isn't measurable until survey results.

**Reality:** Experience issues are visible in operational data long before surveys.

**Solution:** Proactive experience monitoring and intervention.
