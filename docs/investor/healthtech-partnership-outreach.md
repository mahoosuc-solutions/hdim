### The Core Problem HDIM Solves (Frame This for Clay)

Healthcare quality measurement is fundamentally broken by design — and it's been broken for decades. The entire industry runs on **batch processing**:

- Encounters happen throughout the year
- Data is collected, cleaned, and adjudicated months later
- HEDIS scores are calculated in Q1–Q2 of the *following* year
- Star ratings are published 12–18 months after the care was (or wasn't) delivered

**By the time a health plan knows their stars dropped, the patients who needed care gap closure are already in the next measurement year.** By the time a provider knows a diabetic patient missed their HbA1c, they've missed the measure. The feedback loop is so delayed it's effectively useless for real clinical decisions.

This is the same patient whose records Verato has perfectly matched. Verato solved identity — the hardest data foundation problem in healthcare. But that investment yields a complete, trusted, *static* record. Nobody has built the engine to evaluate clinical quality against it in real time, at the point of care, when it can actually change outcomes.

HDIM does exactly that. Care gaps in under 2 seconds. FHIR R4 native. CQL-compliant. The legacy batch world becomes real-time.

**For Verato, this is the difference between being the data foundation and being the platform that drives results.** Their customers today use Verato to know *who* their patients are. With HDIM, they can immediately know *what those patients need* — and act on it.
