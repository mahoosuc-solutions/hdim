## Post 1: The Quality Measurement Lag Problem

**Theme:** Industry insight
**Best day to post:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 8-9am

```
Health systems are making quality decisions with 6-month-old data.

I've talked to a dozen Chief Quality Officers this year. Every one of them said the same thing:

"By the time we see our HEDIS gaps, it's too late to close them."

The typical workflow:
1. Care happens in January
2. Claims process in February-March
3. Data warehouse loads in April
4. Analytics team runs reports in May
5. Quality team sees gaps in June
6. Outreach happens in July

That's 6 months of preventable gaps.

The technology to fix this exists. FHIR R4 enables real-time clinical events. CQL can evaluate quality measures in milliseconds.

The question isn't whether real-time quality measurement is possible.

It's why we're still waiting 6 months for data that should take 6 seconds.

What's been your experience with quality data lag?

#HealthcareQuality #HEDIS
```

## Post 2: Why HEDIS Still Matters

**Theme:** Technical depth / market validation
**Best day to post:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 8-9am

```
"HEDIS is outdated."

I hear this a lot. Here's why I disagree:

Yes, HEDIS measures are imperfect. They're claims-based, retrospective, and miss nuance.

But they're also:
- The basis for $50B+ in value-based care payments
- Required for Medicare Advantage star ratings
- The benchmark health plans use to evaluate networks

Ignoring HEDIS doesn't make it go away. It just means you're flying blind on the metrics that determine your reimbursement.

The real problem isn't the measures themselves.

It's that most organizations can only evaluate them once a year.

Imagine if your bank only showed your balance annually. You'd have no idea if you were on track.

That's how most health systems manage quality today.

The fix isn't abandoning HEDIS. It's measuring it continuously.

Real-time HEDIS evaluation → Actionable gaps → Closed loops → Better outcomes.

What would change in your organization if you could see quality gaps the day they happened?

#ValueBasedCare #HealthcareAnalytics
```

## Post 3: The Hidden Cost of Quality Reporting

**Theme:** Market validation / vision
**Best day to post:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 8-9am

```
A CMO told me their quality reporting costs $200K/year.

I asked what that included:
- $80K: Analytics vendor license
- $60K: 1.5 FTEs doing manual chart review
- $40K: Data integration and maintenance
- $20K: Audit and compliance

Then I asked: "What's the ROI?"

Long pause.

"Honestly? We don't know. We just know we have to do it."

This is the dirty secret of healthcare quality:

Most organizations treat it as a compliance exercise, not a strategic asset.

They pay to produce reports. Not to improve outcomes.

But the data locked in those reports could:
- Identify high-risk patients before they're admitted
- Close care gaps before the measurement year ends
- Prioritize outreach based on actual impact

The organizations winning at value-based care aren't just reporting quality.

They're operationalizing it.

What if your $200K quality investment actually prevented readmissions?

#HealthcareROI #PopulationHealth
```

## Post 4: What FHIR Actually Enables

**Theme:** Technical depth
**Best day to post:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 8-9am

```
FHIR isn't just another healthcare standard. It's an architecture shift.

Here's what changed:

Before FHIR:
- Batch exports (nightly, weekly)
- Custom integrations per system
- Data lives in silos
- Analysis is retrospective

With FHIR R4:
- Real-time subscriptions to clinical events
- Standard API across vendors
- Data flows where it's needed
- Analysis can be proactive

The technology leap is like going from batch processing to event streaming.

But most healthcare organizations are still using FHIR like it's HL7 v2 - just another way to move files around.

The opportunity:

Every A1C result, every diagnosis code, every medication order - these are events that can trigger immediate action.

A patient's diabetes is uncontrolled? Know today, not in 6 months.

A patient missed their mammogram? Reach out this week, not next quarter.

The infrastructure is there. Epic, Cerner, and most EHRs support FHIR R4.

The question is: what are you building on top of it?

#FHIR #HealthIT #Interoperability
```

## DOs and DON'Ts

**DO:**

- Take a point of view (mild controversy drives engagement)

- Show expertise without being arrogant
- Engage with comments thoughtfully

**DON'T:**

- Post walls of text without line breaks
- Spam hashtags
- Ignore comments
