### RECITALS

A. Controller has entered into a Service Agreement with Processor for healthcare quality measurement and analytics services (the "Services").

B. In connection with the provision of Services, Processor will Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

C. The Parties wish to ensure that such Processing complies with applicable Data Protection Laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and any applicable national implementing legislation.

D. This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") sets out the terms and conditions governing the Processing of Personal Data by Processor on behalf of Controller.

---

### 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** "Data Protection Laws" means all applicable laws and regulations relating to the processing of Personal Data, including the GDPR, the UK GDPR, and any applicable national data protection laws.

**1.2** "Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is Processed under this DPA.

**1.3** "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that is Processed by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the Services.

**1.4** "Personal Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

**1.5** "Processing" (and "Process") means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

**1.6** "Standard Contractual Clauses" or "SCCs" means the standard contractual clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries as approved by the European Commission.

**1.7** "Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Processor to Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

---

### 2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE OF PROCESSING

**2.1 Purpose.** Processor shall Process Personal Data only for the purposes of providing the Services as described in the Service Agreement.

**2.2 Categories of Data Subjects.** Personal Data processed under this DPA relates to the following categories of Data Subjects:
- Patients of Controller
- Healthcare providers/staff of Controller
- Controller's employees accessing the Services

**2.3 Types of Personal Data.** The following types of Personal Data may be Processed:
- Name, date of birth, contact information
- Medical record numbers and patient identifiers
- Health and medical information (diagnoses, medications, lab results)
- Healthcare provider information
- User account information

**2.4 Special Categories of Data.** Processor will Process special categories of Personal Data, including health data, as necessary to provide the Services. Processor will implement additional safeguards as required by Article 9 of the GDPR.

---

### 3. CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

**3.1** Controller warrants that:
(a) It has obtained all necessary consents or has another lawful basis for the Processing of Personal Data;
(b) It has provided appropriate privacy notices to Data Subjects;
(c) Its instructions to Processor are lawful and comply with Data Protection Laws;
(d) It has conducted any required Data Protection Impact Assessments.

**3.2** Controller shall promptly notify Processor of any changes to Data Protection Laws that may affect Processing.

---

### 4. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

**4.1 Processing Instructions.** Processor shall:
(a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, unless required to do so by applicable law;
(b) Immediately inform Controller if, in Processor's opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws;
(c) Ensure that persons authorized to Process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

**4.2 Security.** Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:
(a) Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data;
(b) Ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems;
(c) Ability to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner following an incident;
(d) Regular testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of security measures.

**4.3 Sub-processing.** Processor shall:
(a) Not engage a Sub-processor without prior written authorization from Controller;
(b) Impose data protection obligations on Sub-processors by way of contract;
(c) Remain fully liable for Sub-processor actions;
(d) Maintain an up-to-date list of Sub-processors and notify Controller of any changes.

**4.4 Data Subject Rights.** Processor shall:
(a) Assist Controller in responding to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Data Protection Laws;
(b) Notify Controller promptly upon receiving any request from a Data Subject;
(c) Not respond to Data Subject requests directly unless authorized by Controller.

**4.5 Personal Data Breach.** Processor shall:
(a) Notify Controller without undue delay, and in any event within 24 hours, upon becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach;
(b) Provide Controller with sufficient information to enable Controller to meet its notification obligations;
(c) Cooperate with Controller in investigating and remediating the breach;
(d) Document all Personal Data Breaches including the facts, effects, and remedial action taken.

**4.6 Assistance.** Processor shall:
(a) Assist Controller in ensuring compliance with its obligations regarding security, breach notification, impact assessments, and prior consultation;
(b) Make available to Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA;
(c) Allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by Controller or an auditor mandated by Controller.

**4.7 Deletion and Return.** Upon termination of the Services, Processor shall, at Controller's choice:
(a) Delete all Personal Data and certify such deletion; or
(b) Return all Personal Data to Controller and delete existing copies.

---

### 5. SUB-PROCESSORS

**5.1 Authorized Sub-processors.** Controller hereby provides general authorization for Processor to engage the Sub-processors listed in Annex B.

**5.2 Changes to Sub-processors.** Processor shall:
(a) Provide at least 30 days' prior written notice of any intended addition or replacement of Sub-processors;
(b) Provide Controller with the opportunity to object to such changes;
(c) If Controller objects, work in good faith to address the objection or provide alternative solutions.

**5.3 Sub-processor Agreements.** Processor shall enter into a written agreement with each Sub-processor imposing data protection obligations substantially similar to those set out in this DPA.

---

### 6. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

**6.1** Processor shall not transfer Personal Data to a country outside the European Economic Area ("EEA") unless:
(a) The transfer is to a country deemed adequate by the European Commission;
(b) Appropriate safeguards are in place as per Article 46 of the GDPR;
(c) A derogation under Article 49 of the GDPR applies.

**6.2 Standard Contractual Clauses.** For transfers to the United States or other non-adequate countries, the Parties agree to execute the Standard Contractual Clauses as set out in Annex C. The SCCs are incorporated by reference into this DPA.

**6.3 Supplementary Measures.** Processor shall implement supplementary technical and organizational measures as necessary to ensure an adequate level of protection for transferred Personal Data.

---

### 7. AUDIT RIGHTS

**7.1** Controller may audit Processor's compliance with this DPA by:
(a) Reviewing Processor's certifications, audit reports, and compliance documentation;
(b) Submitting reasonable written questions to Processor;
(c) Conducting on-site inspections with reasonable advance notice (at least 30 days).

**7.2** Processor shall make available:
(a) SOC 2 Type II reports (when available);
(b) Security questionnaire responses;
(c) Penetration test summaries;
(d) Other relevant compliance documentation.

**7.3** On-site audits shall be:
(a) Conducted during normal business hours;
(b) Subject to reasonable confidentiality obligations;
(c) At Controller's expense (unless audit reveals material non-compliance).

---

### 8. LIABILITY

**8.1** Each Party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations of liability set out in the Service Agreement.

**8.2** Notwithstanding the above, neither Party excludes or limits liability for:
(a) Fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
(b) Gross negligence or willful misconduct;
(c) Any liability that cannot be limited by law.

---

### 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

**9.1 Term.** This DPA shall remain in effect for as long as Processor Processes Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

**9.2 Termination.** This DPA shall terminate automatically upon termination or expiration of the Service Agreement.

**9.3 Survival.** Sections 4.7 (Deletion and Return), 7 (Audit Rights), and 8 (Liability) shall survive termination.

---

### 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**10.1 Conflicts.** In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Service Agreement, this DPA shall prevail with respect to the Processing of Personal Data.

**10.2 Amendments.** This DPA may only be amended by written agreement signed by both Parties.

**10.3 Governing Law.** This DPA shall be governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction], without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

**10.4 Entire Agreement.** This DPA, together with its Annexes and the Service Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof.
