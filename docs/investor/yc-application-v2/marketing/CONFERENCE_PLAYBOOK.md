## Conference Participation Strategies

### Strategy 1: Exhibitor Booth

**Best For:** HIMSS, HLTH, MGMA Annual
**Investment:** $10,000-$40,000

**What's Included:**
- Booth space (10x10 minimum)
- Signage and displays
- Demo stations
- Lead capture system
- Staffing (2-4 people)

**Success Metrics:**
- Booth visitors: 200-500
- Qualified leads: 50-150
- Demo appointments set: 20-50

---

### Strategy 2: Speaking/Presenting

**Best For:** NACHC, NAACOS, AMGA
**Investment:** $2,000-$5,000

**Approach:**
1. Submit session proposals 6+ months in advance
2. Focus on educational content, not product pitch
3. Include customer co-presenter when possible
4. Offer resource/follow-up for attendees

**Session Topic Ideas:**
- "Real-Time Quality Measurement: Moving Beyond Batch Reporting"
- "Closing 10,000 Care Gaps: A Community Health Center Case Study"
- "Solo Practice Quality Strategy: Competing with Health Systems"
- "The Economics of Quality: Calculating Your True ROI"
- "FHIR for Quality: Practical Implementation Lessons"

**Success Metrics:**
- Session attendance: 50-200
- Business cards/scans: 20-50
- Follow-up meetings: 10-20

---

### Strategy 3: Sponsored Session/Workshop

**Best For:** HIMSS, HLTH
**Investment:** $5,000-$15,000

**Approach:**
1. Sponsor breakfast/lunch session
2. Educational content with soft product tie-in
3. Interactive workshop format
4. Capture all attendee info

**Success Metrics:**
- Attendance: 30-100
- Qualified leads: 20-50
- Demo appointments: 10-25

---

### Strategy 4: Networking Only

**Best For:** Regional events, first-time attendance
**Investment:** $500-$2,000

**Approach:**
1. Attend as participant
2. Focus on relationship building
3. Host informal meetups
4. Assess future investment potential

**Success Metrics:**
- Meaningful conversations: 20-30
- Follow-up meetings: 5-10
- Intelligence gathered: Competitor info, market feedback

## At-Conference Execution

### Team Roles

| Role | Responsibilities | Skills Needed |
|------|------------------|---------------|
| **Booth Lead** | Overall coordination, VIP conversations | Sales experience, product knowledge |
| **Demo Specialist** | Product demonstrations | Deep product knowledge, presentation skills |
| **Lead Capture** | Badge scanning, conversation starters | Outgoing personality, qualification skills |
| **Floater** | Session attendance, networking | Relationship building, note-taking |

### Booth Schedule

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 7:00 AM | Team breakfast, daily briefing |
| 8:00 AM | Booth opens, early arrivals |
| 10:00-11:00 AM | Peak traffic window |
| 12:00-1:00 PM | Lunch coverage (rotate) |
| 2:00-3:00 PM | Post-lunch traffic |
| 4:00-5:00 PM | End-of-day conversations |
| 5:00-6:00 PM | Lead review, booth cleanup |
| Evening | Networking events, dinners |

### Conversation Framework

**Step 1: Qualify (30 seconds)**
```
"What brings you to the conference?"
"What's your role at [Organization]?"
"Are you currently dealing with quality measurement challenges?"
```

**Step 2: Discover (2-3 minutes)**
```
"What's your current approach to quality tracking?"
"What's working well? What's frustrating?"
"How much time does your team spend on quality reporting?"
```

**Step 3: Demo (5-7 minutes)**
```
"Let me show you what real-time quality measurement looks like..."
[Dashboard walkthrough]
"This is how [similar organization] improved their score by 40%..."
```

**Step 4: Close (1-2 minutes)**
```
"Based on what you've shared, I think we should set up a deeper dive."
"Can I get your info to schedule a follow-up?"
"What's the best time for a 30-minute call next week?"
```

### Lead Qualification Criteria

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **Hot (A)** | Has budget, decision-maker, expressed strong interest, timeline <3 months |
| **Warm (B)** | Interested, not decision-maker OR timeline 3-6 months |
| **Cool (C)** | General interest, no clear need, gathering information |
| **Cold (D)** | Not a fit, competitor, wrong segment |

### Lead Capture Form

**Required Fields:**
- Name
- Email
- Organization
- Role/Title
- Phone (optional)

**Qualification Fields:**
- Current quality solution
- Primary pain point
- Decision timeline
- Budget authority
- Organization size (providers)

**Notes Field:**
- Conversation summary
- Follow-up action
- Lead score (A/B/C/D)

## Speaking Proposal Templates

### Educational Session Proposal

**Title:** Real-Time Quality Measurement: Moving from Reactive to Proactive Care

**Session Type:** Breakout Session (45-60 minutes)

**Abstract:**

Quality measurement in healthcare has traditionally been a retrospective exercise—reviewing data days or weeks after patient encounters to identify missed opportunities. But in an era of value-based care, practices need real-time visibility to close care gaps at the point of care.

This session explores the technology and workflow changes required to implement real-time quality measurement, including:

- The clinical and financial impact of data latency
- FHIR-based approaches to real-time quality evaluation
- Case studies from practices that achieved 40%+ quality score improvements
- Practical steps for transitioning from batch to real-time measurement

Attendees will leave with a framework for evaluating their own quality measurement approach and actionable strategies for improvement.

**Learning Objectives:**

1. Explain the difference between batch and real-time quality measurement
2. Identify opportunities to close care gaps at the point of care
3. Evaluate technology requirements for real-time quality measurement
4. Apply lessons from case studies to their own organizations

**Target Audience:** Quality directors, CMOs, practice managers, health IT leaders

**Speaker Bio:**

[Speaker Name] is [Title] at HDIM, a healthcare technology company focused on real-time quality measurement. With experience in [relevant background], [he/she/they] has helped [number] healthcare organizations improve quality outcomes through technology-enabled measurement. [Previous speaking engagements, publications, or relevant credentials.]

### Panel Discussion Proposal

**Title:** The Future of Quality Measurement: Technology, Policy, and Practice

**Session Type:** Panel Discussion (60 minutes)

**Abstract:**

As value-based care models mature, quality measurement is evolving from a reporting requirement to a strategic imperative. This panel brings together perspectives from technology, clinical practice, and policy to discuss where quality measurement is heading and how organizations can prepare.

**Discussion Topics:**

1. How is real-time data changing quality improvement workflows?
2. What policy changes are shaping quality measurement requirements?
3. How are small practices keeping up with quality demands?
4. What role will AI play in quality measurement?
5. How can we balance measure burden with clinical value?

**Suggested Panelists:**

- Quality director from health system or large practice
- CMS or NCQA representative
- Health IT vendor perspective (HDIM)
- Solo/small practice physician

---

### Workshop Proposal

**Title:** Hands-On Quality Measurement ROI Calculation

**Session Type:** Interactive Workshop (90 minutes)

**Abstract:**

Many healthcare organizations struggle to quantify the return on investment from quality measurement initiatives. This hands-on workshop provides attendees with practical tools and frameworks for calculating the true cost of quality measurement—and the potential savings from improvement.

**Workshop Agenda:**

1. Current state assessment: What are you spending today? (15 min)
2. Hidden costs: Staff time, penalties, missed bonuses (20 min)
3. ROI framework: Calculating potential savings (25 min)
4. Build your business case: Worksheet exercise (20 min)
5. Q&A and peer discussion (10 min)

**Attendee Takeaways:**

- Quality measurement ROI calculator (spreadsheet)
- Business case template
- Benchmarks for similar organizations
- Action plan worksheet

### Conversion Funnel

```
Booth Visitors: 300
↓ (50% leave contact info)
Leads Captured: 150
↓ (20% qualified as Hot/Warm)
Qualified Leads: 30
↓ (50% agree to demo)
Demos Scheduled: 15
↓ (60% attend demo)
Demos Completed: 9
↓ (33% convert to customer)

```

### ROI Calculation

**Example: $25,000 Event Investment**

- Leads captured: 100
- Hot leads: 20 (20%)
- Demos scheduled: 10 (50%)
- Demos completed: 6 (60%)
- Customers: 2 (33%)
- Average contract value: $5,000/year
- Customer lifetime: 3 years
- Total LTV: 2 × $5,000 × 3 = $30,000

**ROI: ($30,000 - $25,000) / $25,000 = 20%**

*Note: This calculation is conservative and doesn't include:*
- Pipeline value from warm leads
- Brand awareness impact
- Partnership opportunities
- Competitive intelligence gathered
