## Battle Card #1: vs. Arcadia Analytics

### Quick Facts

| Attribute | Arcadia | HDIM |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Monthly Cost | $50K-$150K | $80-$2,000 |
| Implementation | 12-18 months | 2-4 weeks |
| Data Refresh | Daily batch | Real-time (<200ms) |
| Contract Term | 3 years minimum | Month-to-month |
| Setup Fee | $100K+ | $0 |

**Price Advantage: 625x cheaper**

---

### Their Strengths (Acknowledge)
- 20+ years in market, established brand
- Comprehensive data aggregation from multiple sources
- Strong payer relationships and network
- Deep analytics capabilities for large systems

### Their Weaknesses (Exploit)
- **Price:** Prohibitive for organizations under 50,000 lives
- **Speed:** Batch processing = stale data
- **Implementation:** 18 months before value
- **Complexity:** Requires dedicated staff to operate
- **Lock-in:** Multi-year contracts, high switching costs

---

### Talk Tracks

**When they mention Arcadia:**
> "Arcadia is great for large health systems with 18 months and $500K to spend. For organizations like yours, HDIM delivers the same quality measurement in 2 weeks for less than their monthly fee. And your data updates in 200 milliseconds, not overnight."

**When they say "We're talking to Arcadia":**
> "I'd encourage that conversation—it'll help you see the contrast. When they quote you $50K/month and an 18-month implementation, come back and we'll have you measuring quality in 2 weeks for $299/month."

**When they say "We need enterprise features":**
> "Which features specifically? We have 61 HEDIS measures, real-time care gaps, provider dashboards, and regulatory reporting. Often 'enterprise' means 'complexity'—let me show you how HDIM does the same thing simply."

**When they say "Arcadia has more analytics":**
> "Arcadia has more reports because they've been adding to legacy architecture for 20 years. We have the analytics that matter for quality improvement—and you can actually use them because the data is real-time, not 48 hours old."

---

### Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Arcadia is more comprehensive" | "For $600K/year, I'd hope so. What specifically do you need that we don't offer? Most of Arcadia's features are complexity for complexity's sake." |
| "We need their payer integrations" | "We integrate with payer data too—via FHIR and file feeds. What payers do you work with? Let me show you how we handle that." |
| "Arcadia is the industry standard" | "Standard for who? Large health systems with $600K budgets. For 14,000 organizations like yours, we're building the new standard." |
| "Our board knows Arcadia" | "Brand recognition doesn't improve quality scores. Would your board prefer an 18-month wait and $600K cost, or results in 2 weeks for $3,500/year?" |

---

### Proof Points
- [Reference customer] implemented HDIM in 2 weeks, improved diabetes control by 15% in 90 days
- Average HDIM customer sees ROI in first quarter; Arcadia customers often wait 2+ years
- HDIM's real-time data enables point-of-care interventions; Arcadia's batch data is for retrospective reporting only

- **Lock-in:** Only works if you stay on Epic forever
- **Reporting:** Basic compared to purpose-built tools

### Talk Tracks

**When they say "We already have Healthy Planet":**
> "Is it configured and working? Most organizations have Healthy Planet sitting unused because it takes months to configure. We can be live in 2 weeks, showing you data from Epic plus all your other sources."

**When they say "It's already included in our Epic license":**
> "Included but not free—you're paying in consulting costs and staff time to configure it. More importantly, Healthy Planet only sees Epic data. Where do your patients get labs? Where do they see specialists? That data matters for quality."

**When they say "We want to stay in Epic":**
> "We embed in Epic via SMART on FHIR. Your providers never leave the EHR—they see care gaps right in the patient chart. But the care gaps include data from everywhere, not just Epic."

---

## Using Battle Cards in Sales Calls

### Pre-Call Prep
1. Research prospect's current solution (if any)
2. Review relevant battle card
3. Note 2-3 key talk tracks to use
4. Prepare 1-2 proof points

### During the Call
1. **Listen first:** Let them reveal pain points
2. **Acknowledge competitor strengths:** Build credibility
3. **Focus on their priorities:** Don't dump all objections
4. **Use talk tracks naturally:** Not as scripts

### Post-Call Follow-Up
1. Send comparison grid (simple, visual)
2. Include relevant proof point
3. Offer trial/demo
4. Log competitive intel in CRM
