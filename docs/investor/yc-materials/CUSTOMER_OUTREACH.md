## Discovery Call Framework

### Opening (2 min)
"Thanks for taking the time. Before I show you anything, I'd love to understand your current situation. Mind if I ask a few questions?"

### Discovery Questions (10 min)

1. **Current State**
   - "How are you currently calculating quality measures?"
   - "What platform are you using? How long have you had it?"
   - "Walk me through what happens when a care gap is identified."

2. **Pain Points**
   - "What's your biggest frustration with quality reporting today?"
   - "How long does it take from when a gap exists to when someone acts on it?"
   - "How much time does your team spend on manual quality work?"

3. **Impact**
   - "What would it mean if you could identify gaps during the visit instead of after?"
   - "If you could automate 80% of your reporting work, what would your team do instead?"
   - "How would real-time quality data change your Star Rating trajectory?"

4. **Decision Process**
   - "What's your timeline for quality platform decisions?"
   - "Who else would need to be involved in evaluating a new solution?"
   - "What would success look like for a pilot?"

### Demo (15 min)
Show Patient 360 → Real-time gaps → AI recommendations → One-click actions

### Close (3 min)
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I think would make sense..."

## Objection Handling

**"We use Epic/Cerner"**
"Perfect—that's exactly who we're built for. HDIM connects to your existing FHIR endpoints. We're not replacing your EHR, we're adding real-time intelligence on top. Think of it as upgrading from batch processing to real-time. Would you be open to seeing how it works alongside your current stack?"

**"We don't have budget right now"**
"I hear that a lot—it's exactly why I built HDIM. Our starting tier is $80/month. That's probably less than your team spends on coffee. What if we did a 30-day pilot with no commitment? You'd know pretty quickly if it's valuable."

**"We tried something similar and it failed"**
"That's helpful context—what went wrong? [Listen carefully]

If it was implementation complexity: We deploy in days, not months. Standard FHIR APIs, no proprietary integration.

If it was adoption: Our AI agents make it so staff don't have to change workflows. The insights come to them.

If it was accuracy: We use the same CQL standard that Epic and CMS use. Measures are validated against HEDIS specs.

Would it be useful to see a working demo before ruling it out?"

**"We're locked into a contract"**
"Understood. When does that come up for renewal?

Here's what I'd suggest: Let's do a technical evaluation now so you have real data when that conversation happens. No cost, no commitment—just proof of what's possible. That way you'll know exactly what to ask for in your next RFP."

**"This seems too good to be true / too cheap"**
"Fair question. Here's how I did it:

I built the entire platform myself using AI-assisted development. What would have taken a team of 20 engineers, I built solo. That's not a compromise on quality—it's a bet on modern development tools.

The result is a platform that's enterprise-grade but doesn't have enterprise overhead. I pass those savings to customers because I want this technology in every healthcare organization, not just the ones that can afford Epic.

The best way to validate is to try it. 30-day pilot, no commitment."

**"We need to see references"**
"I'm a solo founder raising funding to scale—so I don't have the typical customer logos yet. What I can offer:

1. A live demo with your actual measures
2. A 30-day pilot with your data
3. Full technical documentation for your team to validate

Would any of those help you evaluate?"
