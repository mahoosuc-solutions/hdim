# Market Research Findings - Healthcare Quality Measurement
**Research Date:** December 14, 2024
**Purpose:** Validate HDIM value proposition for YC application

---

## Executive Summary

Research confirms significant pain points in healthcare quality measurement that HDIM directly addresses. Key findings support our positioning as a faster, cheaper, standards-based alternative to legacy enterprise solutions.

---

## 1. Implementation Time (6-8 months is standard)

**Finding:** "For most clients, implementation takes an estimated 6–8 months before organizations start to see any meaningful data." - [ACO Health Solutions](https://www.acohealthsolutions.com/quality-and-performance-management)

**HDIM Differentiator:** Deploy in days, not months. FHIR-native integration eliminates custom data mapping.

---

## 2. Real-Time vs Batch Processing Gap

**Finding:** Industry recognizes real-time is superior but most systems still batch:
- "Without proper flexibility, systems may experience downtime, performance issues, and latency" - [Innovaccer](https://innovaccer.com/resources/blogs/top-5-challenges-implementing-population-health-management-software-and-how-to-overcome-them)
- Data synchronization gaps create "operational inefficiencies and hamper interoperability"

**HDIM Differentiator:** <200ms measure calculation vs. 24-72 hour batch. Quality data available at point of care.

---

## 3. Cost Barriers for Small Practices & FQHCs

**Key Findings:**
- "Specialized IT applications required for population health management are generally too expensive and complex for small, independent practices" - [Medical Economics](https://www.medicaleconomics.com/view/making-population-health-work-small-practices)
- "Most third-party software is designed and priced for large organizations" - David Nash, MD, Dean of Population Health, Thomas Jefferson University
- FQHCs "operate on thin, often unpredictable financial margins" - [Commonwealth Fund](https://www.commonwealthfund.org/publications/issue-briefs/2024/aug/community-health-centers-meeting-primary-care-needs-2024-FQHC-survey)

**HDIM Differentiator:** Starts at $80/month vs. $50K+/month for Epic. Democratizes access for resource-constrained organizations.

---

## 4. The "Crowded Desktop" Problem

**Finding:** "Many healthcare providers juggle 6 to 20 clinical support tools in addition to their EHRs" - [Veradigm](https://veradigm.com/crowded-desktop-problem-point-of-care-gap-alerts/)

- 74% of physicians change diagnoses based on external tools
- BUT most avoid using them because they're "external to existing EHR workflow"
- "Only 8% of patients receive all recommended preventive care" - AHRQ

**HDIM Differentiator:** FHIR-native means seamless EHR integration without separate login or workflow disruption.

---

## 5. Care Gap Alerts Must Be Point-of-Care

**Key Research:**
- EHR-integrated alerts make providers "41% more likely to prescribe" guideline-directed therapy - PROMPT-HF trial
- "A successful solution needs to be easy to use—automatic setup, no install, EHR-agnostic, free for providers" - [Fierce Healthcare](https://www.fiercehealthcare.com/sponsored/closing-care-gaps-point-care-smarter-approach)

**HDIM Differentiator:** Real-time gap identification when chart opens, not days later in batch reports.

---

## 6. Epic Healthy Planet Specific Complaints

**User Feedback (KLAS Research):**
- "Healthy Planet was overpromoted as the solution that was going to make population health work"
- "Healthy Planet is not my favorite Epic module. It never has been"
- "Since Epic is such a large program, sometimes it is hard to get customer assistance"
- Usability complaints: "If it takes five extra clicks to discretely document a declined vaccine, it won't be documented"

**Source:** [KLAS Research](https://klasresearch.com/comments/epic-healthy-planet/78116)

**HDIM Differentiator:** Purpose-built for quality, not bolted-on. Direct support from founders.

---

## 7. CQL/Standards Adoption is the Future

**Key Findings:**
- "CQL reduced the time needed to specify, implement and test new quality measures by as much as 90%" - [NCQA](https://www.ncqa.org/resources/clinical-quality-language-and-cql-engines-the-basics/)
- Major bodies (CMS, NCQA, WHO) now distribute measures in CQL
- "Only when all programs use one standard for execution logic and one standard for data will we realize full benefits"

**HDIM Differentiator:** 100% CQL-native. Template-driven means new measures in hours, not weeks.

---

## 8. Data Integration Nightmare

**Findings:**
- "Data collection is inconsistent across practices"
- "Data sent in numerous formats including fax, secure FTP, EMR, traditional mail"
- "Many EHRs meet CEHRT requirements but practices lack technical expertise to work with QRDA files"
- "Studies have called into question the validity and reliability of quality measures calculated by EHR reporting systems" - [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5999523/)

**HDIM Differentiator:** FHIR R4 standard means consistent data model regardless of source EHR.

---

## 9. HEDIS Reporting Burden Growing

**2024 Changes:**
- NCQA expanding Race/Ethnicity Stratification to 18 measures
- Moving more measures to ECDS-only (electronic clinical data systems)
- "NCQA plans to make HEDIS reporting completely digital by 2030"
- Stakeholders citing "heavy administrative burden"

**Source:** [Cotiviti](https://resources.cotiviti.com/quality-measurement-and-reporting/ncqa-confirms-hedis-updates-for-measurement-year-my-2024)

**HDIM Differentiator:** Digital-first architecture already aligned with NCQA's 2030 vision.

---

## 10. Value-Based Payment Transition

**Finding:** "Value-based payment models could help FQHCs... but successfully implementing VBP takes time, culture change, and technical expertise in data reporting" - [Commonwealth Fund](https://www.commonwealthfund.org/blog/2024/federally-qualified-health-centers-can-make-switch-value-based-payment-need-assistance)

**HDIM Differentiator:** Purpose-built for VBC quality reporting from day one.

## Competitive Intelligence

### Innovaccer (well-funded competitor)
- Strengths: #1 in Black Book 2024, strong platform
- Weaknesses: "Falls short in population health overview", "throwing out new capabilities too fast"
- Pricing: Enterprise-focused

### Epic Healthy Planet
- Strengths: Market leader, integrated with Epic EHR
- Weaknesses: "Overpromoted", usability issues, Epic-only, expensive
- Pricing: $50K+/month

### Health Catalyst
- Strengths: Strong analytics, good employer reviews
- Weaknesses: Long implementations, expensive
- Pricing: Enterprise-focused

## Recommendations for YC Application

1. **Lead with the 8% statistic** - Only 8% of patients receive all preventive care. This is the problem.

2. **Use real implementation timelines** - "6-8 months is standard" makes "days to deploy" compelling

3. **Quote David Nash** - Credible academic source on pricing barriers

4. **Emphasize point-of-care** - 41% improvement with EHR-integrated alerts is powerful

5. **Position CQL as moat** - 90% reduction in measure development time

6. **Target FQHCs/small practices** - Underserved market with clear pain, regulatory requirement to report
