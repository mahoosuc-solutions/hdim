# Source-Available Business Rationale (ROBS Support Document)

**Entity:** Grateful House Incorporated  
**Product:** HealthData-in-Motion (HDIM)  
**Date:** 2026-03-07  
**Purpose:** Document legitimate business purpose for source-available strategy in a
ROBS-funded C-corporation context.

This document is for business records and planning only, not legal or tax advice.

## 1. Executive Summary

Grateful House Incorporated is releasing HDIM as source-available under Business Source License
1.1 (BSL 1.1) to accelerate adoption while preserving commercial licensing
rights for production and hosted use. This strategy is intended to:

BSL 1.1 is source-available and not an OSI-approved open-source license.

- Increase qualified market demand
- Shorten enterprise sales cycles
- Strengthen defensible company value through distribution and ecosystem growth
- Support revenue generation through commercial licenses and services

## 2. Legitimate Business Purpose

Source-available distribution under BSL is a revenue and valuation strategy, not a hobby activity.
Business objectives include:

- Expanding top-of-funnel through developer-led evaluation
- Reducing customer proof-of-value friction
- Converting production use cases into commercial licensing opportunities
- Building trust and technical credibility in regulated healthcare environments

## 3. Industry Precedent

Established companies have built multi-billion-dollar businesses using BSL and
open-core licensing models:

- **HashiCorp** -- Adopted BSL 1.1 in 2023; acquired by IBM for $5.4B in 2024
- **GitLab** -- Open-core model; IPO at $11B valuation (2021)
- **Confluent** -- Built on open-source Apache Kafka; $2.5B+ market cap
- **Elastic** -- Server Side Public License; $1.5B+ market cap
- **MongoDB** -- Server Side Public License; $15B+ market cap
- **Sentry** -- BSL 1.1 adopter; $3B+ valuation
- **CockroachDB** -- BSL 1.1 adopter; $5B valuation (2022)

These precedents demonstrate that source-available licensing is a proven,
mainstream strategy for enterprise software companies.

## 5. Why BSL 1.1 (Instead of Permissive OSS)

BSL allows public code access and non-production use while reserving commercial
production rights. This aligns with corporate objectives:

- Prevents uncompensated production competition
- Enables paid commercial terms for production deployments
- Preserves long-term optionality via change date and change license

## 6. Revenue Model Alignment

Expected revenue streams tied to this licensing model:

- Commercial production licenses
- Enterprise support and SLAs
- Implementation and integration services
- Compliance and deployment advisory offerings

## 7. Corporate Governance Alignment

This strategy supports C-corp fiduciary duties by pursuing measurable enterprise
value creation:

- Pipeline growth from public technical visibility
- Increased customer confidence through transparent architecture and controls
- Stronger recruiting and partnership leverage
- Improved due-diligence posture for strategic financing

## 8. ROBS Risk Controls

To support audit readiness and good governance, the company will maintain:

- Executed IP assignment from founder/individual to corporation
- Formal board approval and meeting minutes for licensing decision
- Consistent accounting for licensing and service revenue
- Documentation of pricing, contracts, and customer conversions
- Separation of corporate assets from personal assets

## 9. Measurable Success Criteria

Within 12 months of launch, target metrics include:

- Qualified inbound leads attributable to public repository
- Number of technical evaluations converted to paid agreements
- Annual recurring revenue linked to BSL conversion funnel
- Evidence of external adoption (forks, issues, integrations, references)

## 10. Conclusion

The HDIM source-available launch under BSL 1.1 is a deliberate commercial strategy
designed to increase corporate value and revenue potential while protecting core
production rights. The strategy is consistent with the company’s profit motive
and supports the long-term interests of the corporation and its shareholders.
