# Structured Interview Rubric: Medical Cofounder Candidate

## Interview Process Overview

Stage sequence:

1. Stage 1: 30-minute screening call.
2. Stage 2: 60-minute structured interview.
3. Stage 3: 60-minute operator scenario workshop.
4. Stage 4: references and diligence.

No candidate skips stages.

## Stage 1: Screening (30 minutes)

Objective: Validate baseline fit and rule out non-operators.

Questions:

1. What clinical deployments have you personally led in the last 3 years?
2. What is your current weekly availability for operator work?
3. What role do you prefer: advisor, operator, or executive owner?
4. What does cofounder-level accountability mean to you?

Pass criteria:

- Demonstrates deployment ownership, not just sponsorship.
- Can commit weekly execution time in next 60 days.
- Accepts staged `Chair -> Cofounder` pathway.

## Stage 2: Structured Interview (60 minutes)

### Section A: Founder Alignment (15 min)

Evaluate:

- Mission alignment
- risk tolerance
- decision-making style
- conflict handling

Scoring (1-5): `Alignment`, `Trust`, `Decision Quality`

### Section B: Commercial Clinical Leadership (20 min)

Prompt:

"Describe how you would influence a CMO, CFO, and IT leader to approve a 90-day MA pilot with measurable outcomes."

Evaluate:

- executive communication clarity
- understanding of MA economics
- ability to manage multi-stakeholder dynamics

Scoring (1-5): `Commercial Fluency`, `Executive Influence`, `Cross-Functional Leadership`

### Section C: Execution Operating Cadence (25 min)

Prompt:

"Design your first 6 weeks if you started as Chair of Clinical Strategy next Monday."

Evaluate:

- concrete actions
- prioritization
- weekly operating cadence
- metrics orientation

Scoring (1-5): `Execution Depth`, `Pace`, `Accountability`

## Stage 3: Operator Scenario Workshop (60 minutes)

Scenario:

"A national MA prospect wants a pilot, but quality leaders are interested, finance is skeptical, and IT is concerned about integration timelines. Build the deployment plan and stakeholder strategy."

Required outputs from candidate:

1. 30/60/90-day pilot plan.
2. Stakeholder map and escalation path.
3. Success metrics and review cadence.
4. Risk register with mitigations.

Evaluate:

- practical deployment design
- quality/financial metric clarity
- risk management maturity
- ownership posture

Scoring (1-5): `Pilot Design`, `Metrics Discipline`, `Risk Management`, `Operator Ownership`

## Stage 4: References and Diligence

Reference domains:

1. Former CEO/GM or business peer.
2. Clinical operations partner.
3. Implementation/IT partner.

Reference questions:

1. Did this person lead or just advise?
2. How did they perform under ambiguity and pressure?
3. Would you trust them with high-stakes deployment ownership?
4. What failed under their leadership and how did they respond?
