## Post Text

CMS is quietly converging two quality measurement systems that most practices treat as completely separate problems.

The APP Plus quality measure set started with 6 measures in 2025. It grows to 8 this year, 9 next year, and 11 by 2028. The explicit goal is alignment: MIPS reporting for ACOs and provider groups will use the same measures that drive Medicare Advantage Star ratings on the payer side.

This sounds like simplification. It isn't. It means provider organizations will be measured on HEDIS-derived clinical quality metrics that were designed for health plans with dedicated quality departments, claims data warehouses, and teams of nurses chasing charts.

Most independent practices and small ACOs don't have that infrastructure. They report MIPS annually, often through a registry, often in the final weeks before the deadline. They've never had to track whether a diabetic patient's HbA1c was captured within a measurement year in real time.

The practices that adapted to meaningful use by buying an EHR survived. The practices that adapt to APP Plus by building real-time quality measurement into their workflows will outperform. The ones that don't will face penalties that compound every year.

The timeline isn't 2030. It's already started.
