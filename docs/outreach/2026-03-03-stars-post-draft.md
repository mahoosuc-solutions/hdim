## Post Text

2026 Star ratings stabilized. That's been the headline. But stabilization isn't improvement, and the reason most plans couldn't move the needle is the same reason they couldn't last year: they can't measure fast enough to act.

HEDIS measures account for roughly 40% of a plan's Star rating. The measures themselves aren't the problem. Most plans know which gaps exist. The problem is when they find out.

A care gap identified in Q4 from claims data that's 3-6 months old isn't actionable. The member's open enrollment window may have already closed. The intervention opportunity passed months ago. The gap shows up in the denominator, and nobody had a chance to close it.

The 2025 Star ratings hit a nadir. 2026 recovered slightly. But the recovery came from CMS methodology adjustments and disaster provisions, not from plans fundamentally changing how fast they detect and close gaps.

The plans that will actually move Stars in 2027 won't do it by adding more nurses to call lists. They'll do it by shortening the time between a clinical event and a quality signal from months to hours.

That's an infrastructure problem, not a staffing problem.
