# HDIM Investor Launch Plan

**Created:** February 2, 2026
**Status:** Ready for execution
**Timeline:** 12 weeks
**Goal:** Close Series A term sheet with 2-3 customer pilots as validation

---

## Executive Summary

This plan addresses launching HDIM to investors as a technical founder with:
- No existing VC relationships (cold start)
- Under 6 months runway (urgent timeline)
- No customer traction yet (product-market fit unvalidated)

**Strategy:** Parallel track approach prioritizing customer traction (70% effort weeks 1-4) while warming investor relationships (30% effort). Full investor push begins week 5 armed with pilot validation.

---

## Phase 1: Customer Traction Sprint (Weeks 1-4)

### Goal
Sign 2-3 LOIs or pilot agreements with hospitals/health systems within 30 days.

### Target Customer Profile

**Primary:** Mid-size health systems (5-20 hospitals) with value-based care contracts
- Large enough to validate the market
- Small enough to move fast (enterprise health systems have 6-12 month procurement cycles)

**Secondary:** ACOs (Accountable Care Organizations) and health plans with HEDIS reporting requirements

### Outreach Channels (Priority Order)

1. **LinkedIn direct outreach to:**
   - Chief Quality Officers (CQO)
   - VP of Population Health
   - CMIO (Chief Medical Information Officer)
   - Directors of Quality Improvement

2. **Healthcare conferences (if any in next 30 days):**
   - HIMSS regional events
   - State hospital association meetings
   - Quality improvement conferences

3. **Cold email campaigns** to quality/population health leaders

### The Offer

- **Free 30-day pilot** with defined success criteria
- **Full implementation support** (leverage your 30-min deployment capability)
- **No commitment required** - but ask for LOI stating intent to purchase if pilot succeeds
- **Clear value prop:** "See your HEDIS gaps in real-time instead of waiting for annual reports"

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Outreach sent | 20 | 50 | 30 | 20 |
| Discovery calls | 0 | 2 | 5 | 5 |
| Pilots/LOIs | 0 | 0 | 1 | 2-3 |

## Phase 2: Investor Warm-Up (Weeks 1-4, 30% Effort)

### Goal
Build a pipeline of 40-50 qualified investors ready for full push when customer traction materializes.

### Investor Target List

**Tier 1: Healthcare-focused VCs**
- Rock Health
- General Catalyst (healthcare team)
- Andreessen Horowitz Bio + Health
- Oak HC/FT
- Flare Capital
- HealthX Ventures
- Lux Capital (healthcare)

**Tier 2: Enterprise SaaS VCs with healthcare portfolio**
- Bessemer Venture Partners
- Insight Partners
- Battery Ventures
- Scale Venture Partners

**Tier 3: Angels (can move fast)**
- Former health system CIOs/CMIOs
- Healthcare startup founders who've exited
- AngelList healthcare investors

### Warm Intro Strategy

1. **Mine network for second-degree connections:**
   - LinkedIn search each VC partner → find mutual connections → request intros
   - Alumni networks (university, past employers)
   - Former colleagues at health systems or health tech

2. **Build in public:**
   - Post 2-3x/week on LinkedIn about healthcare quality measurement
   - Share technical insights (not product pitches)
   - Goal: Attract inbound investor interest

3. **Attend healthcare startup events:**
   - Rock Health events
   - StartUp Health Festival
   - Local healthcare innovation meetups

### What to Do Before You Have Traction

- **Don't pitch** - you'll burn the lead
- **Do:** Request advice meetings ("building X, would love your perspective on Y")
- **Do:** Add to monthly update list
- **Do:** Ask "who else should I talk to?" to expand network

## Phase 3: Full Investor Push (Weeks 5-12)

### Prerequisites

- [ ] At least 1 signed LOI or active pilot (ideally 2-3)
- [ ] Monthly update sent to warm contacts for 4+ weeks
- [ ] Target list of 40-50 investors with intro paths identified

### The Pitch Narrative

**Weak (without traction):**
> "We built a 51-microservice platform for real-time HEDIS evaluation"

**Strong (with traction):**
> "Hospital X and Y are piloting our platform because they were spending $200K/year on quality reporting that was always 6 months stale. We're showing them care gaps in real-time."

### Fundraise Mechanics

**Week 5-6: Launch**
- Send 15-20 outreach emails in a single week (creates urgency)
- Use warm intros where possible
- Book meetings 1-2 weeks out

**Week 7-9: First Meetings**
- Run partner meetings in batches (creates FOMO)
- Send deck + data room within 24 hours of each meeting
- Follow up at 48 hours if no response

**Week 10-12: Push to Close**
- Focus on 3-5 serious prospects
- Provide diligence materials
- Push for term sheet

### Required Assets

| Asset | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Pitch deck (25 slides) | ✅ Ready | Review, trim to 15 for first meetings |
| Due diligence package | ✅ Ready | Available in docs/ |
| Demo environment | ✅ Ready | docker-compose.demo.yml |
| Financial model | ❓ Check | Need 3-year projections |
| Cap table | ❓ Check | Need clean document |
| Customer references | 🔄 Building | Pilot contacts for VC calls |

### Backup Options

If institutional VCs move too slow:
- **Angels:** Close in 2-4 weeks vs. 8-12 for VCs
- **Strategic investors:** Providence Ventures, Kaiser Permanente Ventures, UPMC Enterprises
- **Revenue-based financing:** Lighter Capital, Clearco (if any revenue)

## Outreach Templates

### LinkedIn Connection Request (300 char)

```
Hi [Name] - I'm working on real-time HEDIS gap detection for health systems.
Saw [hospital] is focused on value-based care. Would love to hear how you're
handling quality measure reporting today. Open to connecting?
```

### LinkedIn Follow-up After Connect

```
Thanks for connecting, [Name].

Quick context: I built a platform that calculates HEDIS measures in real-time
instead of the typical 6-12 month lag. One CQO told me they were "flying blind"
on quality gaps until annual reports came in.

Curious - is that resonating with what you see at [hospital]?

If helpful, I could show you a 15-min demo of how it works. No pitch, just
want to validate we're solving a real problem.

Either way, appreciate the connection.

[Your name]
```

### Cold Email to Prospects

**Subject:** Real-time HEDIS gaps at [Hospital Name]?

```
Hi [Name],

Quick question: How long does it take [Hospital] to identify HEDIS care gaps
after a patient visit?

I ask because most health systems I've talked to are working with data that's
3-12 months stale. By the time they find gaps, the measurement year is closing.

I built a platform that calculates gaps in real-time (<2 seconds after
a FHIR event). Two health systems are piloting it now.

Worth a 15-minute call to see if this matches a pain point for you?

[Your name]
[One-line credential]
```

### Discovery Call Script

**Opening (2 min)**
> "Thanks for taking the time. I'm trying to understand how health systems
> handle quality measurement - not pitching today, just learning. Mind if
> I ask a few questions?"

**Discovery Questions (10 min)**
1. "How do you currently track HEDIS measures throughout the year?"
2. "What's the typical lag between a care event and seeing it in dashboards?"
3. "When you find gaps, how do you close the loop with care teams?"
4. "What does quality reporting cost annually - vendors, FTEs, manual work?"
5. "If you could change one thing about quality measurement, what would it be?"

**Transition (2 min)**
> "That's helpful. Based on what you're describing, what we've built might
> be relevant. Would you be open to a 15-minute demo next week?"

**If Yes:**
> "Great. We're looking for 2-3 design partners for a 30-day free pilot.
> If the demo resonates, would that be something [hospital] could consider?"

## Tracking and Decision Points

### Pipeline Tracking

**Customer Pipeline Stages:**
Lead → Contacted → Discovery Call → Demo → Pilot Proposed → LOI Sent → LOI Signed → Pilot Active

**Investor Pipeline Stages:**
Research → Intro Requested → Intro Sent → Meeting Scheduled → First Meeting → Partner Meeting → Due Diligence → Term Sheet

### Weekly Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Customer outreach sent | 20-30/week |
| Customer response rate | 20-30% |
| Discovery calls | 3-5/week |
| Investor intros requested | 5/week |
| Investor meetings (weeks 5+) | 2-3/week |

### Decision Points

**End of Week 2: Outreach Working?**
- If <10% response rate → rewrite messaging
- If no calls booking → try different titles or stronger offer

**End of Week 4: Customer Traction Check**
- ✅ 2+ LOIs → proceed to investor push
- ⚠️ 1 LOI → proceed but note weaker position
- ❌ 0 LOIs → extend customer sprint 2 weeks

**End of Week 8: Investor Traction Check**
- ✅ 2+ in diligence → focus on closing
- ⚠️ Slow-moving → add angels and strategics
- ❌ No interest → pivot to angel-only round

**End of Week 12: Decision Time**
- ✅ Term sheet → negotiate and close
- ⚠️ Close but no term sheet → push for decision
- ❌ No term sheet likely → activate backup plan

### Backup Plans

**Scenario A: Great customer traction, slow investors**
- Convert pilots to paid contracts
- Pursue revenue-based financing
- Seek strategic investment from health systems

**Scenario B: Investor interest, no customer traction**
- Offer more aggressive pilot terms (90 days free)
- Target smaller orgs (large clinics, small ACOs)
- Frame as "design partner" opportunity

**Scenario C: Neither working by week 8**
- Cut burn rate immediately
- Look for consulting/services revenue
- Consider acqui-hire conversations
- Seek bridge loan from friends/family

---

## Key Success Factors

1. **Customer traction solves everything.** A signed pilot fixes weak network, mediocre deck, and most investor objections.

2. **Speed beats perfection.** A good email today beats a perfect one next week.

3. **Follow up relentlessly.** Most deals close on 5th-7th touch.

4. **Parallel process creates leverage.** Investors move faster when others are interested.

5. **Rejection is data.** Track why people say no - it sharpens the pitch.
