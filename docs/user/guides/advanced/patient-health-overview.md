# Patient Health Overview

The patient health overview provides a comprehensive, 360-degree view of patient's health status and care history.

## Accessing Patient Health Overview

### From Patient Detail Page
1. Click patient name to open detail
2. Click **Health Overview** tab
3. Comprehensive view loads with all sections

### Quick Access
- From patient panel, click **View Health Overview**
- From search results, click patient name + **Overview**
- System shows latest data

## Health Overview Sections

### Patient Summary Card
**Quick snapshot**:
- Name, age, demographics
- Primary conditions
- Current medications
- Allergies/sensitivities
- Emergency contacts
- Primary care provider
- Care team members

### Health Score
**0-100 overall health assessment**:
- Green (80-100): Stable health
- Yellow (50-79): Manageable but needs monitoring
- Red (0-49): Significant health challenges
- Score based on:
  - Condition control
  - Recent events
  - Medication adherence
  - Risk factors
  - Health engagement

### Conditions and Problems
**Active chronic/acute conditions**:
- List of all active diagnoses
- Onset date
- Current status
- Management approach
- Related medications
- Recent relevant labs

### Current Medications
**All active medications**:
- Drug name and dose
- Indication
- Prescribing provider
- Start date
- Adherence status
- Refill due date
- Interactions (if any)

### Recent Vital Signs and Labs
**Latest measurements**:
- Blood pressure (trend)
- Weight (trend)
- Glucose (for diabetics)
- A1C (for diabetics)
- Lipids
- Kidney function
- Other relevant labs
- Comparison to prior values

### Care Plan Summary
**Active care plan overview**:
- Plan name
- Active goals
- Progress on each goal
- Care team members
- Next review date
- Key interventions

### Open Care Gaps
**Clinical opportunities**:
- Gap type
- Patient status
- Days since opened
- Recommended action
- Assigned to (provider, care manager)
- Follow-up date

### Health Timeline
**Chronological health history**:
- Recent events (hospitalizations, ED visits, office visits)
- Key milestones (diagnoses, surgeries)
- Medication changes
- Care plan updates
- Can scroll back months/years
- Click event for details

### Risk Factors
**What puts patient at risk**:
- Medical risk factors (conditions, labs)
- Behavioral risk factors (smoking, obesity)
- Social risk factors (housing, food security)
- Environmental factors
- Contributing to overall risk score

### Preventive Care Status
**Screening and preventive services**:
- Vaccines due/completed
- Cancer screenings (mammogram, colonoscopy, etc.)
- Health maintenance visits
- Preventive services status

### Social Determinants
**Social factors affecting health**:
- Housing status
- Food security
- Transportation
- Social support
- Employment status
- Education level
- Healthcare literacy

### Engagement Metrics
**How engaged is patient**:
- Appointment compliance (keeps appointments?)
- Medication adherence (takes medications as prescribed?)
- Self-management (exercises, diet, etc.)
- Communication frequency (responds to outreach?)
- Care plan participation

## Using the Overview

### Comprehensive Understanding
Before seeing patient or making decisions:
1. Open health overview
2. Review all sections
3. Understand medical complexity
4. Note social challenges
5. Identify priority issues
6. Plan appropriate intervention

### Quick Clinical Review
For office visit or phone call:
1. Scan summary and health score
2. Review current medications
3. Check recent labs
4. Note open gaps
5. Plan visit focused on priorities

### Care Coordination
Sharing patient understanding:
1. Care team all see same overview
2. Common understanding of patient
3. Coordinated care planning
4. No surprises or contradictions
5. Efficient communication

### Quality Improvement
Understanding gaps and opportunities:
1. Identify which gaps most prevalent
2. Understand barriers (medical vs. behavioral vs. social)
3. Plan targeted interventions
4. Track improvement over time

## Advanced Features

### Timeline Filters
Show only specific event types:
- Hospitalizations
- ED visits
- Office visits
- Medication changes
- Lab results
- Care plan updates

### Exporting Information
1. Click **Export Overview**
2. Choose format (PDF, summary)
3. Download for sharing (with appropriate consent)
4. Use for referral information

### Comparing to Cohort
See how patient compares to:
- Peers with same condition
- Peers in same age group
- Peers in same risk category
- National averages

## Mobile Access

### On Mobile Device
- Health overview accessible but simplified
- Shows key information only
- Limited scrolling/detail
- Full version on desktop better for comprehensive review

## Privacy and Sharing

### Patient Portal Access
Patient can see (usually):
- Health conditions (with clinical terminology)
- Current medications
- Recent visits
- Lab results
- Care instructions
- May not see: sensitive notes, internal assessments

### Sharing with Patient
Use overview to:
- Educate patient about their health
- Show trends and progress
- Demonstrate care coordination
- Engage patient in care planning

## Best Practices

### Effective Overview Use
1. ✅ Review before patient encounter
2. ✅ Use to inform care decisions
3. ✅ Share with care team
4. ✅ Update regularly with new information
5. ✅ Use trends not just snapshots
6. ✅ Address identified gaps
7. ✅ Involve patient in review
8. ✅ Document care decisions

### Avoiding Issues
❌ Relying on outdated information
❌ Not reviewing social determinants
❌ Ignoring engagement metrics
❌ Making decisions based on one data point
❌ Not coordinating with team

## Scenario: Using Health Overview

**Situation**: Patient has office visit for follow-up

**Pre-Visit**:
1. Open health overview
2. Note patient is Yellow (moderate risk)
3. See A1C trending down (good!)
4. Note 2 open care gaps
5. See patient struggles with medication adherence

**During Visit**:
1. Acknowledge A1C improvement (positive reinforcement)
2. Discuss barriers to medication adherence
3. Address one of the gaps (can do at visit)
4. Make plan for second gap
5. Reinforce self-management

**Post-Visit**:
1. Update overview with visit documentation
2. Note medication adherence discussion
3. Update care plan
4. Mark gap as addressed
5. Schedule follow-up
