# Best Practices Guide

Guidelines for optimal platform use and care delivery.

## Data Entry Best Practices

### Accuracy
- **Verify information**: Confirm with patient or chart before entering
- **Use correct formats**: Follow standard field formats consistently
- **Complete required fields**: Don't skip mandatory information
- **Double-check before saving**: Review entry before committing
- **Correct errors promptly**: Fix mistakes immediately when discovered

### Completeness
- **Document thoroughly**: Include all relevant clinical information
- **Use complete sentences**: Avoid abbreviations in documentation
- **Provide context**: Explain reasoning for clinical decisions
- **Record dates/times**: Include when actions occurred
- **Note missing information**: Flag if data unavailable

### Consistency
- **Use standard abbreviations**: System recognizes approved abbreviations
- **Follow templates**: Use provided templates for consistency
- **Use dropdown selections**: Select from provided options when available
- **Follow organizational standards**: Adopt established conventions
- **Document same way always**: Consistency aids searching and quality

### Timeliness
- **Document immediately**: Record information when fresh
- **Don't batch for later**: Enter data promptly
- **Update as information changes**: Keep current with new information
- **Flag urgent issues immediately**: Don't delay critical entries
- **Review and finalize**: Complete documentation before leaving patient

## Patient Care Best Practices

### Care Coordination
- **Review complete record**: Check all patient history before deciding
- **Consult with team**: Discuss complex cases with colleagues
- **Document decisions**: Explain why made specific clinical decisions
- **Communicate changes**: Inform team of important updates
- **Verify medication reconciliation**: Ensure accurate medication list

### Quality Focus
- **Monitor quality gaps**: Regularly check patient care gaps
- **Address gaps promptly**: Close gaps as soon as clinically appropriate
- **Document care provided**: Ensure care appears in patient record
- **Follow guidelines**: Adhere to clinical guidelines and standards
- **Measure outcomes**: Track results of interventions

### Patient Engagement
- **Communicate clearly**: Explain condition and treatment in plain language
- **Include patient in decisions**: Shared decision-making when appropriate
- **Provide education**: Help patient understand condition and self-care
- **Follow up appropriately**: Ensure patient compliance and safety
- **Respect preferences**: Document and honor patient preferences

### Safety
- **Check allergies first**: Verify before prescribing any medication
- **Watch for interactions**: Check drug interactions before ordering
- **Verify patient identity**: Confirm correct patient before actions
- **Double-check critical data**: Especially for high-risk patients
- **Report safety concerns**: Escalate immediately if safety issue

## Workflow Best Practices

### Efficiency
- **Use saved filters**: Save and reuse common search filters
- **Set patient panel**: Organize panel by priority or team
- **Use dashboard customization**: Arrange widgets for your workflow
- **Block time strategically**: Schedule focused work time
- **Minimize context switching**: Focus on one task type at a time

### Organization
- **Categorize patients**: Group by status or priority
- **Set priorities daily**: Identify high-priority patients first
- **Track action items**: Use task system to manage to-do items
- **Schedule follow-ups**: Use calendar for necessary follow-ups
- **Review assignments regularly**: Ensure proper distribution

### Time Management
- **Batch similar tasks**: Do all searches together, all orders together
- **Use templates**: Pre-built templates save time
- **Automate routine items**: Set up automation for recurring tasks
- **Delegate appropriately**: Assign to team members based on skills
- **Build in review time**: Allow time for quality checks

## Communication Best Practices

### Messaging
- **Be clear and concise**: Get to point quickly
- **Use professional tone**: Maintain appropriate professionalism
- **Provide context**: Include relevant background information
- **Follow up appropriately**: Don't rely on missed communications
- **Respect HIPAA**: Never include unnecessary PHI in messages

### Collaboration
- **Involve right people**: Include all necessary team members
- **Document decisions**: Record what was decided and why
- **Escalate appropriately**: Know when to involve supervisors
- **Ask for help**: Don't hesitate when uncertain
- **Give feedback**: Help colleagues improve

### Notifications
- **Review alerts promptly**: Act quickly on critical alerts
- **Don't ignore alerts**: Address even low-priority alerts eventually
- **Tune settings**: Adjust threshold to reduce alert fatigue
- **Communicate if missed**: Let team know if alert missed
- **Verify alert accuracy**: Report false positives

## Quality and Compliance Best Practices

### Documentation
- **Document contemporaneously**: Record information timely
- **Be objective**: Stick to facts, avoid interpretation
- **Follow templates**: Use structured documentation formats
- **Use approved abbreviations**: Standard abbreviations only
- **Keep secure**: Don't discuss PHI in unsecured locations

### Audit Trail
- **Know it's recorded**: All actions logged for audit
- **Follow proper procedure**: Procedures exist for reason
- **Never falsify data**: Document only true, accurate information
- **Correct properly**: Use amendment process for errors
- **Explain unusual activity**: Be prepared to explain access

### Compliance
- **Follow policies**: Understand and follow organizational policies
- **Maintain confidentiality**: Protect patient privacy always
- **Report violations**: Report any compliance concerns
- **Stay current**: Attend required compliance training
- **Ask questions**: Clarify when unsure about compliance

## System Optimization Best Practices

### Performance
- **Clear cache weekly**: Keep browser running smoothly
- **Refresh regularly**: Get latest data from system
- **Close unused tabs**: Free up browser memory
- **Restart daily**: Fresh computer state improves performance
- **Use stable internet**: Wired better than WiFi

### Technical
- **Keep browser updated**: Always use latest version
- **Report errors**: Help IT fix bugs by reporting issues
- **Don't workaround bugs**: Use proper process even if slow
- **Test carefully**: Verify changes work before relying
- **Request help for technical**: Contact IT for technical issues

### Data Management
- **Back up important work**: Export critical reports locally
- **Monitor data quality**: Watch for inconsistencies
- **Report discrepancies**: Let IT know of data issues
- **Don't edit improperly**: Always use correct process
- **Escalate data concerns**: Get help with complex issues

## Role-Specific Best Practices

### Physicians
- **Review panel daily**: Check for new alerts and priorities
- **Verify before prescribing**: Allergies, interactions, contraindications
- **Close gaps promptly**: Address care gaps timely
- **Document decision rationale**: Explain clinical thinking
- **Mentor team members**: Help less experienced providers

### Care Managers
- **Maintain current panel**: Keep accurate list of assigned patients
- **Schedule outreach**: Plan patient contacts systematically
- **Document calls**: Record all patient interactions
- **Track care plans**: Monitor patient progress toward goals
- **Collaborate with clinical team**: Coordinate care with providers

### Administrators
- **Monitor system health**: Keep eye on data quality and access
- **Train staff**: Help users understand system effectively
- **Report issues promptly**: Escalate problems to IT
- **Manage access**: Keep permissions current
- **Maintain compliance**: Ensure organization meets requirements

## Prevention-Focused Best Practices

### Error Prevention
- **Slow down**: Take time to avoid mistakes
- **Use checklists**: Follow procedures consistently
- **Double-check critical items**: Especially safety-related
- **Get second opinion**: Verify important decisions
- **Learn from errors**: Understand what went wrong

### Safety Prevention
- **Verify identity**: Always confirm patient identity
- **Check all information**: Verify data before using
- **Don't assume**: Clarify when uncertain
- **Ask questions**: Better to ask than assume
- **Escalate immediately**: Don't wait on safety issues

### Data Loss Prevention
- **Save frequently**: Don't lose work to browser issues
- **Verify save**: Confirm changes saved
- **Follow process**: Use proper save/submit methods
- **Don't close without saving**: Pay attention to warnings
- **Back up reports**: Export important reports locally
