# Compliance & Audit Guide

Meeting regulatory requirements and maintaining audit readiness.

## Regulatory Framework

### HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
**What it is**: Federal privacy and security law protecting patient health information

**Key Requirements**:
- Privacy: Patients control their health information
- Security: Protect information from unauthorized access
- Breach notification: Report any breaches to patients
- Business associates: Vendors must also comply

**Your Responsibilities**:
- ✅ Know what is protected (all PHI)
- ✅ Only use minimum necessary information
- ✅ Secure information against unauthorized access
- ✅ Report suspicious activity
- ✅ Complete required training

### State Privacy Laws
**Variations by state**:
- Different notification requirements
- Patient rights vary
- Specific security measures mandated
- Some states stricter than HIPAA

**What to do**:
- Know your state requirements
- Ask supervisors if unsure
- Follow most restrictive rule
- Report discrepancies to compliance

### Meaningful Use / Certification
**Electronic Health Record (EHR) Standards**:
- System must meet federal interoperability standards
- Patient access to records required
- Data exchange with other systems required
- Reporting to government programs required

**Your role**:
- Use system as designed
- Complete required documentation
- Support audits when requested
- Report system issues to IT

## Audit Process

### Audit Overview
**What is an audit?**: Systematic review of compliance with regulations

**Types**:
- **Internal audit**: Organization reviews own compliance
- **External audit**: Third party reviews compliance
- **Regulatory audit**: Government reviews compliance
- **System audit**: IT reviews data access and changes

**Frequency**: Typically annual, sometimes more frequent

### What Gets Audited

**Access**:
- Who accessed what patient records
- When they accessed records
- What they did (view, modify, export)
- Was access appropriate

**Documentation**:
- Completeness of patient records
- Accuracy of documentation
- Timeliness of entries
- Compliance with templates

**Security**:
- Proper use of passwords and MFA
- Encryption of data at rest and in transit
- Audit logs maintained and reviewed
- Security incidents reported

**Policies**:
- Following organizational policies
- Compliance with privacy practices
- Proper consent obtained
- Handling of patient requests

### Your Role During Audit
- **Cooperate fully**: Auditors need access to information
- **Respond promptly**: Answer questions quickly
- **Be honest**: Don't try to hide issues
- **Provide documentation**: Support claims with records
- **Ask questions**: Clarify if unsure what's being asked
- **Don't modify records**: Never alter records after audit request

## Audit Trail and Logging

### What Gets Logged
- **Login**: When you login and logout
- **Access**: Every patient record accessed
- **Modifications**: All changes to patient data
- **Exports**: All data exports
- **Messaging**: Message to/from
- **Administrative**: User additions/deletions/changes

### Review Your Activity
**Check your audit trail**:
1. Go to Settings > Activity Log
2. Review recent activity
3. Flag anything suspicious
4. Report concerns to IT

**What you should see**:
- ✅ Your own logins/logouts
- ✅ Patients you accessed for care
- ✅ Records you modified
- ✅ Reports you generated

**What concerns you**:
- ❌ Access to patients not assigned
- ❌ Access outside work hours without reason
- ❌ Large data exports
- ❌ Multiple failed login attempts

### Access Controls
**How to interpret logs**:
- System tracks every access
- You're accountable for access under your ID
- Access must be authorized and documented
- Unauthorized access is violation

**Prevention**:
- Never share login credentials
- Lock screen when away
- Logout at end of day
- Report unauthorized access

## Handling Patient Requests

### Access Requests
**Patient right**: Access their own health information

**Your role**:
- Direct patient to proper channel
- Don't handle directly
- Route to medical records department
- Provide timeline (usually 30 days)
- Don't charge excessive fee

### Amendment Requests
**Patient right**: Request corrections to records

**Your role**:
- Don't directly edit records
- Document patient request
- Route to supervisor/compliance
- Proper amendment added with notation
- Original entry retained

### Deletion Requests
**Patient right**: Request deletion (limited)

**Important**: HIPAA doesn't guarantee deletion right

**Your role**:
- Don't delete records
- Route to legal/compliance
- Follow guidance for deletion (rare)
- Verify authorization
- Document decision

### Breach Notification
**Your responsibility if breach**:
- Report immediately to supervisor
- Don't try to contain alone
- Provide details of what/who/when
- Follow organization breach protocol
- May need to notify patients

**Timeline**: Generally must notify within 60 days

## Quality Measures and Compliance

### Measure Validation
**Auditors verify**:
- Patients in numerator actually meet criteria
- Denominator correctly identifies eligible patients
- Exclusions properly applied
- Calculations accurate
- Data sources reliable

**Your role**:
- Accurate documentation supports measures
- Complete records enable audits
- Don't falsify information
- Correct errors promptly
- Verify measure accuracy

### Performance Standards
**Organizations must meet**:
- Minimum quality standards (HEDIS, etc.)
- Benchmarking against peers
- Improvement targets
- Reporting requirements

**Your contribution**:
- Deliver quality care
- Document thoroughly
- Close care gaps promptly
- Follow clinical guidelines
- Contribute to team goals

## Documentation Standards

### Complete Documentation
**Auditors check**:
- All required fields completed
- Narrative describes patient situation
- Rationale for clinical decisions
- Signed and dated entries
- Corrected errors properly

### Legal Requirements
**Records must**:
- Be timely (documenting as care occurs)
- Be accurate (verify information)
- Be complete (all relevant info)
- Comply with regulatory standards
- Support clinical decision-making

**Your documentation**:
- ✅ Document contemporaneously
- ✅ Be objective and factual
- ✅ Spell out abbreviations or use approved only
- ✅ Correct errors with proper amendment
- ✅ Never alter or falsify records
