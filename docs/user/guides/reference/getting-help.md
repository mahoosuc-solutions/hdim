**Consult documentation first if**:

- Feature explanation needed
- Troubleshooting simple issue

## Submitting a Support Ticket

### Effective Ticket Submission

**Subject**: Brief, clear title
- ✅ "Cannot login after password reset"
- ✅ "Report generation times out"
- ❌ "System down"
- ❌ "Help!"

**Description**: Provide context
1. What were you trying to do?
2. What happened instead?
3. When did this start?
4. How often does it occur?
5. Has anything changed recently?

**Details to include**:
- Exact error message (copy/paste)
- Device type (laptop, phone, etc.)
- Browser and version
- Time issue occurred
- Patient name/MRN (if applicable)
- Steps you tried
- Screenshots (helpful but optional)

**Severity level**:
- **Critical**: Patient safety impact, system unavailable
- **High**: Major feature broken, blocking multiple users
- **Medium**: Feature not working, workaround exists
- **Low**: Minor issue, doesn't impact work

**Example ticket**:
```
Subject: Report generation fails for large date ranges

Description:
When I try to generate a quarterly report (3 months of data),
the report fails with "Error 500" after about 5 minutes of
processing. Monthly reports work fine. This started yesterday.

Steps to reproduce:
1. Go to Reports
2. Create new report
3. Select last 3 months date range
4. Click Generate
5. Wait 5 minutes → Error 500

Browser: Chrome 120.0.6099.129
Device: Windows 10 laptop
Time: Occurred around 2pm yesterday and today
```

### Ticket Priorities and Response Times

| Severity | Description | Response | Resolution |
|----------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Critical | Patient safety, system down | 15 min | 1 hour |
| High | Major feature down | 1 hour | 4 hours |
| Medium | Feature broken, workaround | 4 hours | 24 hours |
| Low | Minor issue, cosmetic | 24 hours | 1 week |

## Escalation Process

### When to Escalate
- Issue not resolved within response time
- Solution doesn't work
- New issue discovered while troubleshooting
- Potential security concern
- Potential patient safety issue
- Policy violation involved

### How to Escalate
1. **Reply to ticket**: Explain issue not resolved
2. **Request escalation**: Specifically ask to escalate
3. **Call if urgent**: Phone faster than email for critical
4. **Notify supervisor**: Inform your manager
5. **Follow up**: Call if no response in expected time

### Escalation Path
**Standard escalation**:
IT Help Desk → IT Supervisor → IT Director → CIO

**Clinical escalation**:
Supervisor → Department Head → Medical Director → Chief Quality Officer

**Privacy escalation**:
Supervisor → Compliance Officer → Privacy Officer → Legal

**Patient safety escalation**:
Your supervisor → Clinical director → Chief Medical Officer → CEO (if critical)

## Emergency Support

### Patient Safety Emergency
**Call immediately**: Don't email or submit ticket
- **Emergency number**: [phone number]
- **Explain**: Patient safety impact
- **Detail**: What's happening with patient
- **Your info**: Your name and role

### System Down Emergency
**Call immediately**: System unavailable
- **Help desk emergency line**: [phone number]
- **Incident**: System down/unavailable
- **Impact**: Number of users affected
- **Your info**: Your name and location

### Data Breach/Security Incident
**Report immediately**: Don't wait
- **Security hotline**: [phone number]
- **Incident**: What happened
- **Details**: When, where, who may be affected
- **Your info**: Your name, contact number
- **Follow-up**: Follow organization breach protocol

- [ ] Cleared browser cache
- [ ] Tried different browser
- [ ] Restarted browser
- [ ] Restarted computer
- [ ] Checked internet connection
- [ ] Consulted documentation/knowledge base
- [ ] Checked FAQ and troubleshooting guides
- [ ] Tried workaround if available

**Most issues resolved** by steps above
