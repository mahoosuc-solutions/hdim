# Performance Scorecards

Performance scorecards provide comprehensive measurement of clinical and operational performance against established targets.

## Scorecard Components

### Quality Metrics Section
- HEDIS measures (diabetes, hypertension, preventive, etc.)
- Current performance percentage
- Target/benchmark percentage
- Gap to target
- Trend (improving/declining)
- Action required (if below target)

### Financial Metrics Section
- Revenue metrics
- Cost per patient
- Utilization costs
- ROI on initiatives
- Financial efficiency

### Operational Metrics Section
- Staff productivity
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Appointment compliance
- Documentation completeness
- Efficiency indicators

### Patient Outcomes Section
- Health improvement measures
- Readmission rates
- ED utilization
- Hospital admission rates
- Patient engagement

## Types of Scorecards

### Individual Provider Scorecard
**For**: Physician performance monitoring

**Includes**:
- Quality metrics (individual performance)
- Patient panel characteristics
- Care gap closure rates
- Patient satisfaction
- Peer benchmarking
- Revenue/productivity

**Use**: Performance evaluation, improvement planning, compensation

### Team Scorecard
**For**: Department or care team performance

**Includes**:
- Team aggregate metrics
- Care team productivity
- Team patient outcomes
- Team satisfaction
- Financial performance
- Peer comparison (other teams)

**Use**: Team management, resource allocation, recognition

### Organizational Scorecard
**For**: Leadership, board reporting

**Includes**:
- Overall quality performance
- Financial health
- Market position
- Strategic KPIs
- Year-over-year trends
- Industry benchmarking

**Use**: Strategic planning, board reporting, public reporting

## Accessing Scorecards

### Finding Your Scorecard
1. Click **Performance** or **Analytics**
2. Select **Scorecards**
3. Choose scorecard type (individual, team, organizational)
4. System loads your scorecard

### Customizing View
1. Click **Customize**
2. Choose metrics to include
3. Set comparison periods
4. Select benchmarking approach
5. Save custom view

## Interpreting Scorecards

### Color Coding
- 🟢 **Green**: Meeting or exceeding target
- 🟡 **Yellow**: Below target but acceptable
- 🔴 **Red**: Significantly below target
- 🔵 **Blue**: Not measured or no target

### Performance Levels
- **Exceeding**: 95-100% of target (excellent)
- **Meeting**: 85-95% of target (acceptable)
- **Approaching**: 75-85% of target (needs improvement)
- **Below**: <75% of target (urgent attention)

### Action Required
System may flag metrics requiring action:
- Metrics trending down
- Metrics well below target
- Metrics showing no improvement
- Opportunities for improvement

## Using Scorecards for Improvement

### Identifying Opportunities
1. Review scorecard
2. Find metrics below target
3. Sort by impact (biggest gap first)
4. Identify root causes
5. Plan interventions

### Setting Improvement Goals
1. Select metric to improve
2. Set realistic target (not just 100%)
3. Plan specific interventions
4. Assign responsibility
5. Set timeline (usually 90 days)
6. Monitor progress

### Tracking Improvement Progress
1. Re-run scorecard monthly
2. See improvement over time
3. If improving: continue approach
4. If stalled: change strategy
5. Document results

## Benchmarking

### Peer Comparison
Scorecards show:
- Your performance
- Peer average
- Top 25% performance
- Bottom 25% performance

### Using Benchmarks
- **Better than peers**: Maintain approach
- **Worse than peers**: Seek peer learning
- **At average**: Opportunity to improve
- **Top performers**: Ask what they do differently

## Financial Scorecards

### Financial Metrics
- **Revenue**: Total patient revenue
- **Cost per patient**: Average cost to manage one patient
- **Utilization**: Hospital, ED, office visits
- **Efficiency**: Revenue per FTE staff
- **ROI**: Return on investment for programs

### Financial Performance
- Revenue targets met?
- Cost within budget?
- Efficiency improving?
- ROI meeting expectations?

## Patient Satisfaction Integration

### Satisfaction Metrics
Scorecards may include:
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Appointment satisfaction
- Communication satisfaction
- Overall experience rating
- Would recommend rating

### Using Satisfaction Data
- Identify satisfaction gaps
- Correlate with clinical performance
- Plan patient experience improvements
- Address specific complaints

## Communicating Performance

### Sharing Results with Staff
1. Review scorecard before sharing
2. Context: What do numbers mean?
3. Highlight wins (exceeding targets)
4. Identify opportunities (below targets)
5. Involve team in improvement planning
6. Set shared goals

### Public Reporting
Some organizations publish performance:
- Quality measure performance
- Patient satisfaction
- Safety data
- Innovation initiatives

Know what's publicly reported.

## Best Practices

### Scorecard Excellence
1. ✅ Understand what each metric means
2. ✅ Review scorecards regularly (monthly)
3. ✅ Use for improvement, not punishment
4. ✅ Involve team in setting goals
5. ✅ Monitor progress toward goals
6. ✅ Celebrate improvements
7. ✅ Share learning across organization
8. ✅ Benchmark against top performers

### Common Mistakes
❌ Focusing only on red metrics
❌ Not celebrating green/improving metrics
❌ Setting unrealistic targets
❌ Not involving team in improvement
❌ Ignoring external benchmarks
❌ Making major changes without data

## Troubleshooting

### "My Scorecard Shows Different Number Than Report"
**Causes**: Different time period, data lag
**Solution**: Verify date range, allow time for updates

### "One Metric Seems Wrong"
**Solution**: Drill down to patient list, verify denominator, contact IT

### "How Can I Improve This Metric?"
**Solution**: Work with supervisor, review top performers' approaches
