## Escalation Procedures

### Determining Escalation Level

**Level 1: Self-Service (Lowest)**
- Documentation/knowledge base
- In-app help features
- Platform FAQs
- Troubleshooting guides

**Level 2: First-Line Support**
- IT Help Desk (technical)
- Immediate supervisor (work-related)
- Department colleagues (expertise)

**Level 3: Supervisor/Manager**
- Unresolved Level 2 issues
- Permission/access changes
- Policy clarification
- Performance concerns

**Level 4: Department Leadership**
- Unresolved Level 3 issues
- Cross-departmental concerns
- Significant policy violations
- Patient safety escalations

**Level 5: Executive/Director**
- Enterprise-wide issues
- System outages
- Critical incidents
- Board-level concerns

### Technical Issue Escalation

```
Problem occurs
   ↓
Troubleshoot using documentation (Level 1)
   ↓
Still not resolved? → Contact IT Help Desk (Level 2)
   ↓
Submit ticket with details
   ↓
Wait for response (1-4 hours depending on severity)
   ↓
Still not resolved? → Reply to ticket "Escalate"
   ↓
IT Supervisor follows up (Level 3)
   ↓
Still not resolved? → IT Director involved (Level 4)
   ↓
Resolution or workaround identified
```

### Clinical/Work Issue Escalation

```
Question or concern
   ↓
Ask immediate supervisor (Level 2)
   ↓
Still unresolved? → Department Head (Level 3)
   ↓
Still unresolved? → Medical Director/CMO (Level 4)
   ↓
Still unresolved? → Chief Executive Officer (Level 5)
```

### Patient Safety Issue Escalation

**IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED - Don't wait for normal escalation**

```
Patient safety concern identified
   ↓
CALL immediately - don't email (Level 4)
   ↓
Notify: Your supervisor AND physician AND IT (if system issue)
   ↓
Clinical leadership determines response
   ↓
May involve: CMO, Risk Management, Compliance
   ↓
Document incident
   ↓
Follow-up and resolution
```

### Privacy/Security Issue Escalation

```
Privacy or security issue identified
   ↓
Contact Compliance Officer immediately (Level 3)
   ↓
Use confidential email or hotline if necessary
   ↓
Don't delay for normal processes
   ↓
Compliance Officer determines escalation
   ↓
May involve: Legal, IT, Breach response team
```

## When to Escalate

### Technical Issues Escalate When:
- ❌ Issue not resolved by troubleshooting
- ❌ Error messages indicate system problem
- ❌ Multiple users affected
- ❌ Data appears corrupted
- ❌ Security concern exists
- ❌ System unavailable
- ❌ Critical patient impact

### Workflow Issues Escalate When:
- ❌ Unclear policy application
- ❌ Need permission/access change
- ❌ Cross-department coordination needed
- ❌ Patient care impacted
- ❌ Quality/compliance concern
- ❌ Unusual or novel situation
- ❌ Need authorization above your level

### Patient Safety Issues Escalate When:
- ❌ Any potential patient harm
- ❌ Data discrepancy affecting care
- ❌ Quality/safety concern
- ❌ Medication safety issue
- ❌ Privacy breach
- ❌ Access/permission concern
- ❌ Any safety-related uncertainty

## Escalation Best Practices

### Information to Provide
- **Clear description**: What's the issue?
- **Impact**: Who/what affected? How many?
- **Timing**: When did it occur? How often?
- **Steps taken**: What troubleshooting done?
- **Error details**: Exact messages, screenshots
- **Urgency**: Why does this matter now?
- **Patient info**: Relevant patient context if applicable

### Communication Guidelines
- **Be respectful**: Professional tone always
- **Be clear**: Specific, not vague
- **Be brief**: Concise description
- **Be accurate**: Facts, not assumptions
- **Be calm**: Don't blame or get frustrated
- **Be patient**: They're helping
- **Follow up**: Check on status if needed

### Documentation
- **Keep records**: Save ticket numbers
- **Note progression**: Track what happened
- **Document conversations**: Who said what/when
- **Save evidence**: Screenshots, error messages
- **Timeline**: Exact dates and times
