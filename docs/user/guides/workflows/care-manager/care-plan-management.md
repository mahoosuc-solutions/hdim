# Care Plan Management

Care plans are your primary tool for coordinating comprehensive patient care. This guide covers creating, maintaining, and monitoring care plans.

## Understanding Care Plans

### What Is a Care Plan?
A care plan is a documented set of goals and interventions that:
- Defines patient's health priorities
- Outlines steps to address those priorities
- Coordinates care across the team
- Tracks progress over time
- Ensures patient engagement

### Care Plan Components
1. **Patient Goals**: What patient wants to achieve
2. **Clinical Goals**: What clinical team wants to achieve
3. **Interventions**: Specific actions to reach goals
4. **Responsible Parties**: Who does what
5. **Timeline**: When goals should be met
6. **Progress Tracking**: Monitoring how patient progresses

### When to Create/Update Plans
- **New patient to care management**: Create initial plan
- **Major life event**: Hospitalization, new diagnosis, loss of housing
- **Goal met**: Update with new goal
- **Plan not working**: Revise approach
- **Annual review**: Required reassessment
- **Patient request**: If patient wants to change focus

## Creating a New Care Plan

### Step 1: Patient Assessment
Before writing plan:
1. Click patient name to open detail
2. Review **Risk Assessment**:
   - What makes this patient high-risk?
   - What are primary health threats?
   - What social factors affect health?
3. Review **Recent Activity**:
   - Recent visits
   - Hospitalizations
   - Lab results
   - Medication changes
4. Review **Patient Preferences**:
   - What matters most to patient?
   - What goals interest patient?
   - What are barriers?

### Step 2: Start Care Plan
1. From patient detail page, click **Create Care Plan** or **New Plan**
2. System shows plan template
3. Confirm:
   - Patient name
   - Start date
   - Care manager (you)
   - Primary physician

### Step 3: Set Patient Goals
1. Click **Add Goal**
2. Select goal category or enter custom goal
3. Common goal categories:
   - **Chronic Disease Management** (Diabetes, HTN, COPD, etc.)
   - **Mental Health** (Depression, anxiety management)
   - **Preventive Health** (Screenings, vaccinations)
   - **Medication Management** (Adherence, optimization)
   - **Lifestyle** (Weight loss, smoking cessation, exercise)
   - **Social/Functional** (Housing stability, employment, social engagement)

**Goal Framework** (SMART Goals):
- **S**pecific: Clear what needs to happen
  - NOT: "Improve diabetes"
  - YES: "Achieve A1C <7% for diabetes control"
- **M**easurable: Can track progress
  - Include numbers/metrics where possible
- **A**chievable: Realistic for this patient
  - Consider patient's current functioning and barriers
- **R**elevant: Matters to patient and clinical team
  - Align with patient's priorities when possible
- **T**ime-bound: Target date for achievement
  - Usually 30-90 days for realistic goals

**Example Goal**:
"Patient will achieve A1C <7% within 90 days through medication adherence and diet modification."

### Step 4: Document Baseline Status
1. For each goal, document starting point:
   - Current A1C = 8.2%
   - Current systolic BP = 155 mmHg
   - Current weight = 215 lbs
   - Smoking status = 1 pack/day
2. This lets you measure progress
3. System tracks changes over time

### Step 5: Add Interventions
For each goal, document **how** to achieve it:

1. Click **Add Intervention**
2. Enter intervention details:
   - **What**: Specific action
   - **Who's Responsible**: You, physician, patient, team member
   - **Frequency**: When/how often
   - **Timeline**: When to complete

**Example Interventions for Diabetes Goal**:
- "Patient will schedule diabetes education class" (Patient responsibility; by week 1)
- "Patient will check blood sugar daily and log results" (Patient; ongoing)
- "Care manager will call weekly to review logs and problem-solve" (You; weekly for 8 weeks)
- "Physician will review A1C and adjust medications if needed" (MD; at 6 weeks)
- "Patient will follow [specific diet plan]" (Patient; ongoing)

**Good Interventions**:
- Specific (not vague)
- Actionable (someone can actually do it)
- Patient-centered (include patient actions)
- Team-coordinated (involve multiple providers)
- Trackable (can monitor completion)

### Step 6: Identify Barriers and Supports
1. Click **Barriers** section
2. Document known barriers:
   - Cost of medications
   - Transportation to appointments
   - Limited health literacy
   - Work schedule conflicts
   - Social isolation
   - Housing instability
3. Click **Supports** section
4. Document available supports:
   - Family member to help with medications
   - Transportation assistance available
   - Community resources (free clinics, support groups)
   - Patient's motivation/strengths

### Step 7: Set Review Schedule
1. Click **Review Schedule**
2. Set review dates:
   - First review: 2-4 weeks
   - Subsequent reviews: Monthly or quarterly
   - Annual review: Required
3. System will remind you of review dates

### Step 8: Save and Share
1. Click **Save Plan**
2. System saves and generates plan document
3. Plan is now active
4. Click **Share with Team** to notify care team
5. Team members get notification and access

## Managing Active Care Plans

### Viewing Active Plan
1. From patient detail, click **Active Care Plan** or **Care Plans**
2. Shows all current plans with:
   - Goals and status
   - Interventions and progress
   - Next review date
   - Responsible parties

### Updating Plan During Care

**Ongoing Documentation** (weekly/biweekly):
1. Click patient name
2. From **Care Plan** section, click **Add Progress Note**
3. Document:
   - **Intervention status**: Which actions completed
   - **Patient progress**: Updates toward goals
   - **Barriers encountered**: Issues coming up
   - **Modifications needed**: Adjustments to plan
   - **Patient engagement**: How is patient responding

**Example Progress Note**:
"Patient attended diabetes education class. Very engaged and asking good questions. Taking metformin and lisinopril as prescribed. Blood sugar logs show good compliance. One barrier: patient working night shift, affecting meal timing. Will discuss alternative meal schedule. Continue current plan. Follow-up call in 1 week."

### Modifying Interventions
If current approach not working:

1. Click **Edit Plan**
2. Review interventions in that section
3. For interventions not working:
   - Click **Edit** on intervention
   - Change frequency, approach, or responsible party
   - Document reason for change
   - Save changes
   - Notify team of changes

**Example Change**:
Original: "Care manager calls weekly"
Modified to: "Care manager calls twice weekly" (reason: patient calling with questions more frequently; needs more support)

## Monitoring Progress

### Goal Achievement Tracking
1. At each review, assess goal progress:
   - **On track**: Patient doing well toward goal
   - **Partial progress**: Moving toward goal but slower than expected
   - **No progress**: No change in status
   - **Declined**: Patient got worse
   - **Goal met**: Achieved target

2. Update status in system
3. If goal met: Celebrate with patient! Discuss next goal.
4. If off track: Problem-solve barriers and adjust plan

### Barrier Management
When patient not progressing:

1. **Identify barrier**:
   - Lack of resources?
   - Health literacy issue?
   - Motivation?
   - Social/environmental?
   - Medical complication?

2. **Problem-solve with patient**:
   - What's getting in the way?
   - What would help?
   - What are options?
   - What can patient do?

3. **Adjust plan**:
   - Address identified barrier
   - Add resources or supports
   - Modify interventions
   - Increase care coordination
   - Consider referral if appropriate

### Data Trending
System shows:
1. **Baseline vs. Current**: How much progress?
2. **Rate of Change**: Fast improvement or slow?
3. **Time to Goal**: On pace to meet target date?
4. **Trend Line**: Direction of change (improving/declining)

Review quarterly to ensure real progress, not just activity.

## Special Care Plan Situations

### Patient with Multiple Conditions
**Approach**:
1. **Prioritize ruthlessly**: Focus on 2-3 most important goals
2. **Connect goals**: Link related conditions where possible
   - Example: Diabetes management + cardiovascular risk reduction
3. **Prevent overwhelming patient**: Too many goals = failure
4. **Regular reassessment**: Adjust priorities as conditions change

### Complex/High-Risk Patient
**Approach**:
1. **More frequent contact**: Weekly or twice-weekly
2. **Intensive interventions**: More frequent clinic visits, home visits, etc.
3. **Team approach**: Coordinate across multiple providers
4. **Safety focus**: Emphasize preventing crisis (ED/hospital)
5. **Social support**: Arrange additional community supports

### Palliative/End-of-Life Patient
**Approach**:
1. **Goals shift**: From cure to comfort and quality of life
2. **Focus changes**: Pain management, dignity, family support
3. **Different metrics**: Success is comfort and peace, not clinical numbers
4. **Shared decision-making**: Patient and family preferences drive care
5. **Spiritual care**: Address spiritual/existential needs

## Care Plan Reviews

### Scheduled Reviews
1. System reminds you of upcoming review dates
2. Before review meeting/call:
   - Pull up current plan
   - Gather recent data (labs, vitals, visits)
   - Review progress on each goal
   - Identify barriers/successes
   - Prepare revised plan if needed
3. During review:
   - Discuss progress with patient
   - Celebrate wins
   - Problem-solve barriers
   - Adjust plan as needed
4. After review:
   - Document outcomes
   - Update plan in system
   - Notify team of changes
   - Schedule next review

### Annual Care Plan Review
Required yearly reassessment:

1. **Comprehensive Review**:
   - How is patient doing overall?
   - Goals still relevant?
   - Conditions changed?
   - Life circumstances changed?
   - Patient priorities changed?

2. **Physical/Social Assessment**:
   - Current health status
   - Social situation changes (housing, employment, relationships)
   - Access to care changes
   - Financial situation
   - Any new barriers/supports

3. **Plan Renewal**:
   - Revise existing goals
   - Add new goals as appropriate
   - Remove goals no longer relevant
   - Update interventions based on learning
   - Set new review dates

## Care Plan Best Practices

### Writing Effective Goals
✅ DO:
- Use patient-friendly language
- Include measurable outcomes
- Make timelines realistic
- Involve patient in goal-setting
- Focus on patient's priorities
- Break larger goals into smaller steps

❌ DON'T:
- Use medical jargon
- Set unrealistic goals
- Ignore patient preferences
- Create too many goals
- Be vague about what success looks like

### Engaging Patients in Plans
1. **Involve from start**: Ask patient's priorities
2. **Use their language**: Use words patient understands
3. **Connect to their values**: "This helps you [patient's stated goal]"
4. **Get commitment**: "Can you do this?" not just "You need to..."
5. **Regular communication**: Update patient on progress
6. **Celebrate wins**: Acknowledge progress
7. **Adjust when needed**: Don't stick with failing plan

### Coordinating with Team
1. **Share plan**: Ensure all providers see it
2. **Define roles**: Clear who's responsible for each intervention
3. **Communicate changes**: Alert team when plan changes
4. **Resolve conflicts**: If team disagrees, discuss early
5. **Regular updates**: Keep team informed of progress

## Troubleshooting Care Plan Issues

### "Plan Not Improving Patient Outcomes"
**Possible Causes**:
1. Goals too ambitious
2. Interventions not addressing real barrier
3. Patient not engaged
4. Insufficient intensity of care
5. Medical issue not recognized

**Solution**:
1. Meet with patient to understand barriers
2. Reassess baseline issue
3. Involve clinical team
4. Consider referrals
5. Adjust timeline/goals

### "Patient Not Following Interventions"
**Possible Causes**:
1. Patient doesn't understand plan
2. Interventions too difficult
3. Patient doesn't agree with goals
4. Barriers not addressed
5. Patient depressed/overwhelmed

**Solution**:
1. Explore why patient not complying
2. Simplify interventions
3. Problem-solve barriers
4. Adjust to patient's preferences
5. Increase support/frequency

### "Care Team Not Engaging with Plan"
**Possible Causes**:
1. Plan not shared effectively
2. Roles not clear
3. Team disagrees with approach
4. Too much documentation burden
5. Competing priorities

**Solution**:
1. Meet with team to review plan
2. Clarify roles
3. Discuss concerns
4. Simplify documentation
5. Align with team's workflow
