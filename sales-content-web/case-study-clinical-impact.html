Case Study: Clinical Impact at Regional Care Organization

How real-time gap detection improved quality metrics and generated $265K in Year 1 ROI

Organization Profile

Metric Value
Organization Type Regional integrated care network
Attributed Patients 15,000
Network Providers 45 (primary care + specialists)
Care Coordinators 10 FTE
Annual Org Budget $180M
Payer Mix 60% Medicare Advantage, 40% commercial

The Challenge

This organization was performing well on Star ratings (3.4 out of 5) but knew they were leaving gaps—both clinical and financial—on the table.

The Specific Problem: Care coordinators were spending 3-4 hours per week hunting for gaps across fragmented systems (Epic EHR, external claims, lab results). They identified only 45% of eligible patients for intervention. The rest were missed entirely—meaning quality measures went unclosed and revenue bonuses went unrealized.

The Solution Deployed

The organization implemented Health Data In Motion to:

  • Identify care gaps in real time (not batch reports)
  • Integrate with existing Epic EHR without disruption
  • Prioritize gaps by impact (highest-value first)
  • Automate care coordinator workflow and tracking
  • Ensure compliance audit trail for every decision

Implementation Timeline

Weeks 1-2 (Discovery): Current state assessment, workflow mapping, ROI assumptions confirmed with finance and medical leadership
Weeks 3-6 (Build): Data integrations (Epic, claims, external labs), 52 HEDIS measures configured, care coordinator dashboard built
Weeks 7-8 (Test): Pilot testing with 3 care coordinators, workflow refinement, user feedback integration
Weeks 9-10 (Deploy): Full care coordination team trained, go-live, daily monitoring and support
Month 3+ (Optimize): Performance analysis, workflow optimization, expansion planning

Key Success Factors

  • Leadership alignment: CEO, CFO, Chief Medical Officer all aligned on ROI and timeline before launch
  • Care coordinator adoption: Workflow designed with coordinators' input, not imposed on them
  • Realistic ROI assumptions: Validated with finance team before implementation
  • Phased rollout: Pilot with 3 coordinators before full team deployment
  • Daily monitoring: First month had daily check-ins to catch issues early
  • Measure-driven optimization: Alerts tuned based on actual coordinator feedback and action rates

Lessons Learned

  • Speed matters: Real-time alerts create urgency. Batch reports do not. Coordinators act on 80% of real-time alerts vs 20% of batch reports.
  • Integration is critical: When HDIM alerts appear in the Epic workflow instead of a separate system, adoption is immediate.
  • Prioritization reduces alert fatigue: 20-30 prioritized alerts/day have 80% action rate vs 100+ random alerts/day with 5% action rate.
  • Compliance is a hidden benefit: Customer didn't expect the audit efficiency gain, but it's been transformative.
  • Measure results fast: They were seeing gap closure improvements in Week 4 (after go-live). This builds momentum for expansion.

Future Expansion (Year 2)

Based on Year 1 success, the organization is planning to:

  • Expand to 3 additional specialty practices (orthopedics, cardiology, behavioral health)
  • Add chronic disease management workflows (diabetes, hypertension, COPD)
  • Implement patient-facing engagement (text alerts, patient portals)
  • Build predictive risk scoring (identify at-risk patients before they gap)

These expansions are expected to add an additional $200K+ Year 2 benefit with minimal additional cost.

Bottom Line

Health Data In Motion delivered exactly what was promised: 68% gap closure (vs 45% baseline), +0.6 Star rating improvement, $265K net Year 1 ROI, and a 4.9-month payback period.

For a 15K-patient organization, that translates to $270K in quality bonuses, $45K in labor savings, and $120K in shared savings—all from a tool that cost $200K to implement.

Recommendation: Deploy in organizations with 10,000+ attributed patients, value-based contracts, and an already-strong care coordination team. ROI and implementation success are highest in this segment.
