Clinical Sales Strategy

Complete sales and marketing framework for busy clinical users

Executive Summary

Health Data In Motion solves a critical problem for busy clinical organizations: identifying and closing care gaps faster than competitors. This strategy focuses on quantifying the pain, demonstrating ROI, and positioning HDIM as the must-have solution for quality improvement.

Key Insight: Care coordinators waste 2-3 hours per week hunting for gaps manually. At $65K/year salary, that's $6,500+ per coordinator in lost productivity. Multiply by 10 coordinators = $65K/year opportunity per organization.

1. Clinical Pain Points (Quantified)

Pain Point #1: Fragmented Gap Identification

The Problem: Care coordinators manually review EHR notes, claims, and lab results to identify care gaps. No integrated view. Results: gaps are missed or identified too late.

Time Impact: 2-3 hours/week per coordinator
Financial Impact: $6,500+/year per coordinator (salary × time wasted)
Gap Impact: Only 45% of eligible patients identified for intervention
Customer Quote: "We used to find gaps by accident, during chart reviews. Now we know who needs help on day one."

Pain Point #2: Manual Compliance Reporting

The Problem: Compliance teams spend weeks pulling data for audits. No automated proof of decision-making. High risk of finding issues during external audits.

Time Impact: 40-60 hours per audit
Financial Impact: $2,000-$3,000 in staff time + risk of $50K+ penalties
Automation Opportunity: HDIM logs every access, every decision, every policy enforcement
Customer Quote: "We went from 2-week audit prep to 2 hours with HDIM."

Pain Point #3: Consent Workflow Complexity

The Problem: Managing patient consent across care teams, systems, and regulations is manual and error-prone. Leads to delayed care and compliance issues.

Impact: Workflow delays, potential consent violations, provider confusion
Solution: HDIM enforces policy-based consent automatically
Customer Quote: "No more guessing about what data we can access. HDIM tells us."

Pain Point #4: Alert Fatigue & Poor Prioritization

The Problem: Many systems send 100+ alerts/day. Coordinators ignore most of them. Real gaps get buried in noise.

Impact: 95%+ of alerts ignored
HDIM Solution: Smart prioritization (highest-impact gaps first, actionable only)
Result: 20-30 targeted alerts/day, 80%+ action rate

Pain Point #5: Care Coordination Fragmentation

The Problem: Care gaps identified but scattered across teams and systems. Leads to duplicate work, missed handoffs, and poor outcomes.

Impact: Coordination delays, rework, poor patient outcomes
HDIM Solution: Unified dashboard, integrated workflows, audit trail
Result: Clear ownership, faster resolution, measurable outcomes

4. Buyer Personas & Messaging

Persona #1: Chief Medical Officer

Primary Need: Improve quality metrics (Star rating, HEDIS scores) with measurable impact

Primary Message: "Get to a 4.0 Star rating (and keep it). Real-time gap alerts close measures 20% faster. Proven: +0.6 Star, +$250K revenue in year 1."

ROI Frame: Quality bonus revenue + reputation + payer relationships

Persona #2: Director of Care Coordination

Primary Need: Get coordinators back to patient care (not paperwork)

Primary Message: "Free up 2-3 hours per coordinator, per week. HDIM finds gaps. Your team closes them. Less hunting, more impact."

ROI Frame: Labor savings (don't hire 5 new coordinators) + quality improvements (same team, better outcomes)

Persona #3: Compliance Officer

Primary Need: Pass audits and prove every decision

Primary Message: "Zero-trust compliance. Every access logged. Every decision tracked. Every policy enforced. Audit prep: 2 weeks → 2 hours."

ROI Frame: Audit efficiency + avoided penalties + reduced risk

Persona #4: CFO

Primary Need: ROI and cash flow

Primary Message: "Conservative model: $200K+ Year 1 net benefit. Aggressive model: $530K+. Payback in 5 months. Pure financial play."

ROI Frame: Quality bonuses + labor savings + compliance savings + shared savings

5. Go-to-Market Strategy

Greenfield (Organization not using gap management)

  • Paint the problem: 45% of eligible patients missed for care gaps
  • Show the opportunity: +$250K annual revenue potential
  • Demo the solution: Real-time alerts, care coordinator workflows
  • Close on: Competitive entry, first-mover advantage, faster than competitor implementations

Replacement (Using competitor or manual process)

  • Lead with dissatisfaction: "Batch reporting too slow, alerts overwhelming, no compliance logging"
  • Show the difference: Real-time + consent enforcement + audit trail
  • Quantify the gap: "You're leaving 20% of gaps on the table"
  • Close on: Better metrics, faster payback, proven results

Expansion (In one department, expand to others)

  • Lead with success: "Care coordination saw 68% gap closure, +0.6 Star"
  • Expand scope: Other measures, other departments, connected workflows
  • Accelerate: Already integrated, proven ROI, team trained
  • Close on: Momentum + proven internal ROI

6. 90-Day Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Discovery & Design (Weeks 1-2)

  • Map current workflows and systems
  • Identify priority measures and care gaps
  • Design new workflows (coordinator, physician, compliance)
  • Confirm ROI assumptions

Phase 2: Build & Test (Weeks 3-8)

  • Set up data integrations (EHR, claims, patient registries)
  • Configure measures engine (52 HEDIS measures + custom)
  • Build care coordinator dashboard and workflows
  • User testing and feedback

Phase 3: Train & Launch (Weeks 9-10)

  • Team training (end users, admins, leadership)
  • Go-live with pilot group (care coordination team)
  • Monitor performance and handle issues

Phase 4: Optimize & Expand (Weeks 11-12+)

  • Measure results (gap closure %, alerts actioned, time saved)
  • Optimize workflows based on data
  • Plan expansion to other departments/measures

Key Success Factors

  • Clear ROI: Organizations care about money. Show them the numbers upfront.
  • Fast time to value: 4-8 week implementation. Competitors take 3-6 months.
  • Proof points: Case studies from their peer organizations.
  • Easy integration: Works with their current EHR/claims systems.
  • Compliance focus: Especially important for value-based care organizations.
  • Care coordinator adoption: If they don't use it, it doesn't work. Make it easy.
