Clinical Sales Implementation Summary

How to use all assets together and deploy successfully

Asset Overview

Asset Purpose Audience Format
Clinical Sales Strategy Complete strategy with pain points, ROI, competitive analysis Sales team, leadership, CMO 8,000-word document
One-Pager Executive summary for prospect outreach Prospects, all decision-makers 2 pages, print-ready
Case Study Proof of ROI with real customer example Prospects in discovery/proposal phase 5-page document
Quick Reference Daily sales tool with pitches, objections, discovery Q's Sales reps Laminate-ready guide
Email Sequence 7-email 14-21 day nurture campaign Marketing team, prospects Copy-paste ready
ROI Calculator Interactive financial modeling tool Prospects, working sessions Web-based tool

How Assets Work Together

The Sales Motion:

Week 1 (Awareness): Cold outreach using One-Pager + Email #1

Week 2-3 (Education): Email #2-4 deliver problem story, solution demo, competitive positioning

Week 3-4 (Proof): Case study + ROI calculator show realistic outcomes

Week 4-5 (Action): Working session using Quick Reference discovery questions + ROI calculator

Week 5+ (Close): Proposal, implementation planning, contract

4-Week Implementation Timeline

📋 Week 1: Team Alignment & Preparation

  • Sales team reviews Clinical Sales Strategy (focus on personas and pain points)
  • Sales team reviews Sales Quick Reference (memorize 30-second pitch and key numbers)
  • Marketing team sets up Email Nurture Sequence in marketing automation
  • Print One-Pagers (100+ copies) and Quick Reference (10+ copies)
  • Laminate 2 Quick Reference copies for team
  • Brief leadership on ROI model and expected timeline to close

📊 Week 2: Deployment & First Tests

  • Activate Email Nurture Sequence (send to existing warm leads first)
  • Sales team begins using One-Pager in initial outreach
  • Sales team begins using Quick Reference in discovery calls
  • Marketing tracks email metrics (opens, clicks, bounces)
  • First working sessions using ROI calculator + Case Study
  • Collect feedback on messaging, timing, effectiveness

🔧 Week 3: Optimization & Expansion

  • Analyze email performance (open rate, click rate, booking rate)
  • Sales team feedback on Quick Reference effectiveness
  • Refine messaging based on first 5-10 calls
  • Create gated landing pages for One-Pager and Case Study (lead gen)
  • Set up ROI calculator on website (link from emails)
  • Plan next assets (video demo, additional case studies)

📈 Week 4: Scale & Measure

  • Email sequence running at scale to cold prospect list
  • Sales team actively using assets in all calls
  • Measure demo booking rate (target: 1-2% of email list)
  • Measure working session-to-proposal conversion
  • Measure proposal-to-close rate
  • Plan expansion phase (new markets, new personas, new messaging)

FAQ: Implementation Questions

Q: How do I modify the ROI model for different organization sizes?

A: The base model assumes 15,000 attributed patients. To scale: multiply quality bonus by (org size / 15,000). Labor savings scale similarly by coordinator count. See Clinical Sales Strategy Section 2.3 for detailed scaling examples.

Q: Can I customize the email sequence for specific verticals?

A: Yes! The sequence is generic (works across all customer types). To customize: adjust pain points in Email #2, adjust competitive references in Email #3, customize ROI numbers in Email #5 for their vertical.

Q: How do I track which document drove the most value?

A: Add UTM parameters to links in emails (e.g., ?utm_source=email2&utm_campaign=nurture). Track which emails and links drove working session bookings. Also ask prospects in discovery calls: "What material was most helpful?"

Q: Should I email the case study, or have them download it?

A: Both work. Email as attachment for Email #5 (high engagement). Also offer gated download on website (for lead gen from organic/paid traffic). Watermark the emailed version to track sharing.

Q: How often should I update these materials?

A: Update quarterly based on: (1) New customer results, (2) Competitive changes, (3) Sales feedback on what's working, (4) ROI model validation (compare projections to actual customer results). Keep versioning in filenames (e.g., Case-Study-v1.0.pdf).
