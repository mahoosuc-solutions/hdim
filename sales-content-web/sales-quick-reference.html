30-Second Elevator Pitch

3-Minute Elevator Pitch

The Problem: Care coordinators are drowning in manual work hunting for gaps. Only 45% of eligible patients get identified. Meanwhile, you're leaving $300K+ annually on the table in unrealized quality bonuses.

The Solution: Real-time gap alerts integrated into your existing workflow. Your coordinators stop hunting and start closing. Smart prioritization means 20-30 actionable alerts per day, not 100+.

The Result: 68% gap closure (up from 45%), +0.6 Star rating improvement, $265K Year 1 ROI for a 15K-patient organization.

Speed: Go-live in 8-12 weeks. ROI in 5 months. Proven track record.

Discovery Questions (11)

Ask these in discovery calls to understand fit and build the business case:

1. What's your current gap closure rate?
(If <50%, you're a perfect fit. If >70%, less urgent.)
2. How much time do your coordinators spend hunting for gaps?
(If 2+ hours/week, the labor savings ROI is strong.)
3. What's your current Star rating?
(Each 0.1-point improvement = $45K+ for a 15K-patient org. Big incentive.)
4. Which HEDIS measures are you furthest from closing?
(Tells you where to focus and what the biggest opportunity is.)
5. What systems are your care coordinators currently using?
(Epic/Cerner? Good. Fragmented custom system? Even better fit for us.)
6. Who are your biggest competitors on quality?
(If they're ahead on Star rating, urgency increases.)
7. Do you have compliance audit requirements?
(If yes, emphasize our audit trail + policy enforcement features.)
8. What would happen if you improved your Star rating by 0.5 points?
(Let them do the math on quality bonus revenue. Usually eye-opening.)
9. Have you looked at other solutions?
(If they've tried competitors: "What was your biggest frustration?")
10. How long would it take you to implement a new system?
(If they're slow decision-makers, emphasize our 8-12 week timeline.)
11. Who needs to approve this decision?
(Identify all stakeholders: CMO, CFO, CIO, compliance. Plan accordingly.)

Objection Responses

Objection: "Our EHR already does this"
Response: "Most EHRs have gap functionality, but it's usually batch reporting—you get alerts weeks later. We do real-time alerts that appear in your workflow within hours. Plus, our system works across EHRs (if you're on Cerner and use some external data). Also, we add consent policy enforcement—that's not in any EHR. Let me show you the difference. You'll see why dedicated is better than built-in."
Objection: "We don't have budget for new software"
Response: "I totally understand. But here's the thing—a 15K-patient organization leaves $300K+ on the table annually in unrealized quality bonuses. Our system costs $180K to implement and $100K annually. So in Year 1, you're at break-even or better. Year 2+, it's pure profit. So it's not a cost—it's an investment that pays for itself. The conversation isn't 'can you afford this'—it's 'can you afford to wait another year to capture that $300K?'"
Objection: "We need more time to evaluate"
Response: "That's fair. And we can definitely take time. But I'll point out—every month you wait, you're leaving ~$25K in quality bonus opportunity on the table (assuming $300K/year gap). So while evaluation is smart, speed matters here. How about we schedule a working session next week to map out your current state and confirm the ROI for your organization specifically? If it makes sense, we can move fast. If it doesn't, no harm done."
Objection: "Competitors offer better pricing"
Response: "Pricing definitely matters, and I'd love to see their proposal. But the question isn't cheapest—it's best ROI. Our competitors usually charge less upfront but take 12-24 weeks to implement. We do 8-12 weeks. Their systems do batch reporting. We do real-time. And they don't have consent policy enforcement. So when you factor in faster go-live = faster ROI + better clinical outcomes, we usually end up ahead. What specific features do they have that we don't?"
Objection: "Our IT team says it's too complex to integrate"
Response: "I hear you. IT integration can be a concern. That's actually why we built HDIM to be EHR-agnostic—we integrate with whatever system you have without major modifications. Can I set up a tech call with your IT team? They usually find it's much simpler than they expected. We handle most of the heavy lifting."
Objection: "We tried something similar and it didn't work"
Response: "What happened? [Listen carefully] OK, that's helpful context. Most failures I see come down to one of a few things: alerts were too noisy (100+ per day with 5% action rate), implementation took too long (stretched timelines = user resistance), or the workflow didn't match how coordinators actually work. That's exactly what we fixed with HDIM. We focus on quality over quantity for alerts, we go live in 8-12 weeks, and we design with coordinators' input. I'd love to show you how we address what went wrong before."
Objection: "We're worried about user adoption"
Response: "That's the right concern. And frankly, if coordinators hate using it, it won't work. That's why we design with them from day one. We do workflow design workshops, pilot with a small group first, and tune the system based on actual usage patterns. Our success metric isn't 'is it implemented'—it's 'are coordinators actually using it.' We have 95%+ adoption rates because we make it easy, not because we force it."
