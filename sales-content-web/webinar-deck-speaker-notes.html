SEGMENT 2: THE PROBLEM STATEMENT (4 minutes)

Slide 4: Depression Statistics

Timing: 1:15-2:15

Speaker Notes: "Let's start with the core data. Here are five facts about depression in primary care that most health systems don't talk about:

FACT 1: 50% of primary care patients with major depression go undiagnosed. Not undertreated—undiagnosed. They walk out of your clinic, and you have no documentation that depression exists.

FACT 2: These undiagnosed patients cost you more. They have 50% higher healthcare costs than patients without depression because they're using the ED instead of outpatient care, they're not taking their diabetes medication, they're not showing up for preventive care.

FACT 3: Depression directly impacts your quality scores. If you're measuring HEDIS, CDC (diabetes care in patients with depression), or CBP (blood pressure control), undiagnosed depression is a silent quality killer. Your score looks worse even though your clinical care is fine.

FACT 4: Most health systems don't connect these dots. They're managing depression in behavioral health, managing diabetes in primary care, and the two teams never talk. So depression becomes invisible in your primary care quality data.

FACT 5: When you do diagnose depression, most health systems handle it manually. 1 screening form per patient × 50K patients × 12 months = 600,000 manual data entry points. That's not scalable.

So here's the real problem: You're losing revenue and quality points because of a gap in screening, not because of a gap in clinical care."

SEGMENT 3: THE SOLUTION - HDIM APPROACH (5 minutes)

Slide 7: Four-Step Automated Process

Timing: 4:00-5:30

Speaker Notes: "Here's how HDIM solves this. We've automated the entire workflow in four steps:

STEP 1: SMART SCREENING (Patient Check-in)

  • Patient arrives for their appointment

  • iPad check-in at registration (or at home via patient portal)

  • Adaptive PHQ-9 survey (not the full 9-question form—just the 2-3 questions needed to identify risk)

  • Takes 90 seconds

  • Result: Real-time depression risk score (low/medium/high)

STEP 2: INTELLIGENT ROUTING (Automatic Triage)

  • HDIM analyzes the PHQ-9 score in real time

  • If score < 5 (no depression): No alert. Patient continues to appointment as normal.

  • If score 5-14 (mild to moderate): HDIM flags the visit summary and creates a task for your care coordinator

  • If score > 14 (moderate to severe): HDIM creates a priority alert AND automatic referral to your behavioral health team

  • Routing: All automated based on your workflow rules. No manual work.

STEP 3: INTEGRATED DOCUMENTATION (Auto-Gap Creation)

  • HDIM documents the depression screening in the patient's EHR as a completed HEDIS element

  • Automatically creates any care gaps (e.g., 'Follow-up needed: Depression screening positive - no documented treatment plan')

  • Updates your quality dashboards in real time

  • Care coordinators can see the gap and address it in the same workflow

STEP 4: OUTCOME TRACKING (Quality Measurement)

  • When the patient is referred to behavioral health, HDIM tracks the referral status

  • When treatment is initiated, HDIM updates the gap closure status

  • Your quality team gets real-time visibility: X% of depressed patients screened, Y% of screened patients referred, Z% of referred patients treated

  • This becomes your CDC HEDIS metric, automatically tracked

This entire process, from check-in to documentation to gap creation, takes 3 minutes per patient. Not 6 weeks. Not 4 weeks. 3 minutes."

Key Talking Points:

  • Walk through each step sequentially (easier to follow)

  • Emphasize speed (90 seconds, 3 minutes)

  • Emphasize automation (no manual work)

  • Connect to their pain points (routing, documentation, gaps)

  • Show the outcome (quality metrics automatically updated)

A: "No. We integrate into your existing check-in workflow. Instead of paper screening forms, patients screen on a tablet during check-in. PRESENTER TIPS

Energy & Tone

  • Start strong: "Good morning everyone, and welcome. We're going to talk about something that's costing your organization millions..."

  • Vary pace: Slow down for key numbers (ROI, financial benefit), speed up for explanations

  • Use contrasts: "Most health systems do X... but here's what we're seeing..."

  • Tell stories: "Let me tell you about Sarah Williams..." (people remember stories)

  • Build urgency: "This is a problem that's sitting in front of you right now..."

  • End on action: "Here's what I recommend you do in the next week..."

Handling Objections (Real-time)

  • If someone challenges the ROI: "That's a great question. The $3M is specific to St. Mary's, which is a 50K-patient system. What's your patient volume? Let's calculate what this looks like for you."

  • If someone asks about Epic: "Epic absolutely has screening workflows. Our advantage is that we're pre-configured for quality measurement and depression specifically. Epic's a general tool; we're a specialty tool."

  • If someone says they don't have behavioral health capacity: "That's the most common concern. HDIM uncovers the need, but you need to match it with care capacity. This is a great opportunity to talk to your board about telepsychiatry partnerships or in-clinic therapists."

If Things Go Wrong

  • Tech failure: "We're having a technical difficulty. Let me share some screenshots instead. I've seen this demo work 100 times, so let me walk you through it."

  • Slow attendance: "We have a great group here today. Let's focus on depth for this group. Those who couldn't make it will get the recording."

  • Awkward silence (no questions): "I know there's a lot to absorb. Let me rephrase the key question: What's the ONE thing you'd most want to change about how you screen for depression?"

  • Attendee making it personal/negative: "That's a fair perspective. Let me ask: What would it take for you to consider this?" (turns it into an exploration, not a debate)

Pacing Tips

  • Segment 1 (Opening): Keep it light, build interest, establish credibility

  • Segment 2 (Problem): Be empathetic, use numbers to anchor the problem, show you understand their world

  • Segment 3 (Solution): Slow down, walk through step-by-step, do the demo deliberately

  • Segment 4 (Competitive Advantage): Be fair to competitors, but clear on why HDIM is different

  • Segment 5 (Implementation): Show the speed (90 days), make it feel achievable

  • Segment 6 (Q&A): Give yourself permission to say "that's a great question, let me research and follow up"
