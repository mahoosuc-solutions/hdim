Webinar Flow (30 minutes)

Segment 1 – Opening (3 minutes)

Speaker: Sales Lead · Tone: energetic and personal.

  • Slide 1 – Title: "Mental Health is the Hidden Lever".
  • Slide 2 – Agenda: Why depression matters, automation impact, customer story, next steps.
  • Slide 3 – Hook: ask "What percentage of depressed patients go undiagnosed?" → reveal 50% and connect to missing closures.
Segment 2 – The Problem (5 minutes)

Speaker: Clinical Director · Tone: data-driven, empathetic.

  • Slide 4 – Stats: 50% undiagnosed, 35% without care plan, 3x hospitalization, 3-6 month treatment delays.
  • Slide 5 – Clinical & Financial Impact table (ED utilization $170K-$240K, comorbidity drag $85K-$120K, burnout $40K-$60K, missed measures $50K-$100K).
  • Slide 6 – Why systems fail (manual questioning, clunky EHR modules, self-report bias, disconnected tools).
  • Slide 7 – Live poll: "Do you screen every visit?" to prime urgency.
Segment 3 – The Solution (8 minutes)

Speakers: Clinical Director (workflow) + Sales Lead (business impact).

  • Slide 8 – 4-step workflow: Automated screening → real-time gap detection → comorbidity-aware workflows → outcome tracking.
  • Slide 9 – Step 1 deep dive with portal + kiosk visuals.
  • Slide 10 – Step 2 deep dive: alerts by severity, comorbidity recommendations, diabetes + depression linkage.
  • Slide 11 – Step 3 deep dive: BH referral automation, coordinator task list.
  • Slide 12 – Step 4 deep dive: dashboard, patient story, Star rating math (0.5 Star ≈ $90K for 15K lives).
  • Slide 13 – Before/After comparison: closure rate 65% → 92%, staff time 120 hrs → 20 hrs per 1K patients.
Segment 4 – Customer Case Study (7 minutes)

Hybrid storytelling: Clinical Director narrates patient journey; Sales Lead covers financial scaling.

  • Slide 14 – Overview: Community Health Network (8 clinics, 2,000 attributed patients, Star rating 3.2).
  • Slide 15 – Patient story (Sarah Williams: T2D + HTN + undiagnosed depression) with timeline from implementation to treatment.
  • Slide 16 – Outcomes table: HbA1c drop 1.4 points, BP down 18/8, PHQ-9 14 → 4, adherence 60% → 95%.
  • Slide 17 – Financial extrapolation: 120 treated patients × $16K benefit = ~$1.9M Year 1; Star rating +0.5 adds $150K-$250K; emphasize smaller org baseline ($300K-$450K Year 1) scaling upward with panel size.
  • Slide 18 – Quote or 30-second testimonial from Dr. Amanda Chen.
Segment 5 – Competitive Context (2 minutes)
  • Slide 19 – Comparison table (Manual review, EHR module, standalone screener vs HDIM) focusing on setup time, automation depth, comorbidity integration, closure speed.
  • Slide 20 – Why customers choose HDIM: real-time gaps, comorbidity-aware workflows, automatic measure closure, 6-9 month payback.
Segment 6 – Call to Action & Offer (3 minutes)
  • Slide 21 – 3-step path: Week 1 assessment → 4-week pilot → enterprise scale (6-12 weeks).

Live Q&A (prepare for 8 minutes)

  1. Implementation timeline: 1 week system setup, 5 weeks enablement. Emphasize low IT lift.
  2. EHR compatibility: Epic/Cerner/Meditech via FHIR or HL7; pilot can run stand-alone.
  3. Pricing: Tiered by panel size; $8K-$12K/month for 5K lives typically breaks even inside Year 1 via Star gains.
  4. Modularity: Start with mental health screening alone; most customers expand into quality automation once results land.
  5. Existing screening tools: Integrate or layer on top to add comorbidity linking + quality automation.
  6. Compliance: HIPAA BAA included; optional 42 CFR Part 2 workflows for SUD data.
  7. Reporting: CMS/payer-ready Star reports, exportable; custom formats supported.
  8. Patient adoption: 85%+ completion with portal + kiosk mix; emphasize patient appreciation for whole-person care.
