- Value-based care adoption accelerating

**2. Template-Driven Measures**
- Add new HEDIS measures in hours, not weeks
- Enables rapid response to NCQA updates
- Competitors hardcode measures

**3. FHIR R4 Native**
- Built on modern standard from day one
- No legacy data model to maintain
- Easier integrations, lower maintenance

**4. Multi-Tenant Efficiency**
- True SaaS architecture
- Marginal cost per customer near zero
- Enterprise vendors can't restructure

### 4.3 Business Moats

**1. Price Point**
- $80/month starting vs $50,000/month
- Opens entirely new market segment

**2. Speed to Value**
- 2-4 week implementation vs 12-18 months
- Lower switching cost = easier adoption
- Faster feedback loop = faster improvement

**3. Domain Expertise**
- Founder has 15 years healthcare IT experience
- Understands customer pain firsthand
- Can't be replicated by hiring

**4. Customer Segment Focus**
- Enterprise vendors ignore small/mid market
- Cultural mismatch to serve SMB
- HDIM built for this segment from day one

*Probability: Low (15%)*

Why unlikely:
- Would cannibalize enterprise revenue
- Different sales motion required
- Cultural mismatch (enterprise DNA)
- Board/investor pressure for margins

HDIM response:
- Already 2+ years ahead in market
- Deeper SMB relationships
- Lower cost structure enables price war
- Focus on product, not brand

---

**Scenario 2: Epic adds real-time quality**

*Probability: Medium (30%)*

Why possible:
- Epic has resources
- Already has data
- Customer demand exists

Why limited threat:
- Epic-only (not multi-source)
- Epic shops are not HDIM target
- Epic quality still basic
- Long development cycles

HDIM response:
- Focus on non-Epic customers (majority)
- Emphasize multi-source data
- Deeper HEDIS coverage
- Faster innovation cycle

---

**Scenario 3: Well-funded startup enters**

*Probability: Medium (40%)*

Characteristics:

- Could raise more capital

HDIM advantages:
- 2+ year head start

- Customer relationships

HDIM response:
- Move fast, own the market
- Build customer loyalty
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Consider strategic partnerships
