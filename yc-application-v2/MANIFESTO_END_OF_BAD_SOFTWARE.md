# The End of Bad Software: A Christmas Manifesto

**December 2025**

---

## The Thesis

**The era of "we can't afford good software" is over.**

For decades, critical systems—healthcare, education, government, small business—have been held hostage by a simple economic reality: good software costs millions and takes years to build.

That constraint has been lifted.

AI-assisted development has collapsed the time and cost of building complex software by 10-40x. What took 18 months now takes 3. What cost $1.7 million now costs $46,000.

This isn't incremental improvement. It's a phase change.

**The implication:** Every organization that has been making do with bad software—or no software—can now have good software.

The only question is who will build it for them.

---

## The Old World (2010-2024)

```
Complex Software Project:
─────────────────────────
Team:        10-20 developers
Timeline:    12-24 months
Cost:        $1-5 million
Result:      Often late, over budget, mediocre
Who gets it: Only well-funded organizations
```

**Why healthcare software is bad:**
- Building it is expensive
- Organizations that need it most can't afford it
- Vendors optimize for enterprise buyers, not users
- Legacy systems persist because replacement is too costly

**The result:**
- Doctors still use fax machines
- Care gaps are discovered days after patient visits
- Small clinics use spreadsheets for quality tracking
- 92% of patients don't receive all recommended preventive care

---

## The New World (2025+)

```
Complex Software Project:
─────────────────────────
Team:        1-3 developers + AI
Timeline:    2-6 months
Cost:        $25K-200K
Result:      Production-quality, tested, maintainable
Who gets it: Anyone with a problem worth solving
```

**What changed:**
- AI handles 60-80% of boilerplate code
- Domain experts can build directly (less translation loss)
- Testing and documentation are now easy (not afterthoughts)
- One person can architect AND implement

**The opportunity:**
- Every underserved market can now have good software
- Every frustrated domain expert can become a builder
- Every "it would cost too much" problem has a new answer
