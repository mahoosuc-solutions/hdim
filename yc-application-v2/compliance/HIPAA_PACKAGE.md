# HDIM HIPAA Compliance Package

> Comprehensive HIPAA compliance documentation for healthcare organizations.

---

## Executive Summary

HDIM is fully committed to HIPAA compliance as a Business Associate handling Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of healthcare providers (Covered Entities). This package documents our technical, administrative, and physical safeguards.

**Key Commitments:**
- Sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all customers
- Encrypt all PHI at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Maintain formal incident response procedures
- Conduct annual HIPAA risk assessments
- Provide breach notification within 24 hours

#### RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a Covered Entity or Business Associate under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, Public Law 104-191 ("HIPAA"), and the regulations promulgated thereunder, including the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E), the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C), and the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D);

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides healthcare quality measurement and analytics services that require access to Protected Health Information ("PHI") as defined under HIPAA;

WHEREAS, pursuant to HIPAA, Business Associate and Covered Entity are required to enter into a Business Associate Agreement that meets certain requirements with respect to the Use and Disclosure of PHI;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and obligations set forth in this Agreement, the parties agree as follows:

---

#### ARTICLE I - DEFINITIONS

Terms used, but not otherwise defined, in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as those terms in the HIPAA Rules.

**1.1 "Breach"** shall have the meaning given such term in 45 CFR 164.402.

**1.2 "Designated Record Set"** shall have the meaning given such term in 45 CFR 164.501.

**1.3 "Electronic PHI" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media.

**1.4 "HIPAA Rules"** shall mean the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164.

**1.5 "Individual"** shall have the same meaning as the term "individual" in 45 CFR 160.103 and shall include a person who qualifies as a personal representative in accordance with 45 CFR 164.502(g).

**1.6 "Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** shall have the meaning given such term in 45 CFR 160.103, limited to the information created, received, maintained, or transmitted by Business Associate from or on behalf of Covered Entity.

**1.7 "Required By Law"** shall have the meaning given such term in 45 CFR 164.103.

**1.8 "Secretary"** shall mean the Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services or the Secretary's designee.

**1.9 "Security Incident"** shall have the meaning given such term in 45 CFR 164.304.

**1.10 "Services"** shall mean the healthcare quality measurement and analytics services provided by Business Associate to Covered Entity pursuant to the Service Agreement between the parties.

---

#### ARTICLE II - OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures.** Business Associate may Use or Disclose PHI only as permitted or required by this Agreement, the Service Agreement, or as Required By Law. Business Associate shall:

(a) Use and Disclose PHI only as necessary to perform the Services, as described in the Service Agreement;

(b) Not Use or Disclose PHI in any manner that would constitute a violation of the HIPAA Rules if done by Covered Entity, except as permitted in Section 2.1(e);

(c) Use appropriate safeguards to prevent Use or Disclosure of the PHI other than as provided for by this Agreement;

(d) Report to Covered Entity any Use or Disclosure of the PHI not provided for by this Agreement of which it becomes aware, including breaches of unsecured PHI as required by 45 CFR 164.410;

(e) Use PHI, if necessary: (i) for the proper management and administration of Business Associate; or (ii) to carry out the legal responsibilities of Business Associate, provided that any Disclosure made under this subsection shall be made only if Required By Law or if Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the recipient will (A) hold such information in confidence and use or further disclose it only as Required By Law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed, and (B) promptly notify Business Associate of any breaches of confidentiality.

**2.2 Subcontractors.** Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of the Business Associate agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply through this Agreement to the Business Associate with respect to such information.

**2.3 Access to PHI.** Business Associate shall make available PHI in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual, as required under 45 CFR 164.524. Business Associate shall respond to such requests within fifteen (15) business days.

**2.4 Amendment of PHI.** Business Associate shall make any amendment(s) to PHI in a Designated Record Set as directed or agreed to by the Covered Entity pursuant to 45 CFR 164.526, or take other measures as necessary to satisfy Covered Entity's obligations under 45 CFR 164.526.

**2.5 Accounting of Disclosures.** Business Associate shall document Disclosures of PHI and information related to such Disclosures as would be required for Covered Entity to respond to a request by an Individual for an accounting of disclosures of PHI in accordance with 45 CFR 164.528. Business Associate shall make such information available to Covered Entity within fifteen (15) business days of a written request.

**2.6 Access to Policies and Records.** Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the Use and Disclosure of PHI received from, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of, Covered Entity available to the Secretary for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with the HIPAA Rules.

**2.7 Security Safeguards.** Business Associate shall implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI that it creates, receives, maintains, or transmits on behalf of the Covered Entity, as required by the Security Rule.

**2.8 Minimum Necessary.** Business Associate shall request, use, and disclose only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the purpose of the request, use, or disclosure.

---

#### ARTICLE III - SECURITY MEASURES

**3.1 Administrative Safeguards.** Business Associate shall:
(a) Designate a security official responsible for the development and implementation of security policies;
(b) Conduct workforce training on security policies and procedures;
(c) Implement policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations;
(d) Conduct periodic risk assessments.

**3.2 Physical Safeguards.** Business Associate shall:
(a) Implement policies to limit physical access to electronic information systems and facilities;
(b) Implement policies and procedures that specify the proper functions to be performed and the manner in which those functions are to be performed.

**3.3 Technical Safeguards.** Business Associate shall:
(a) Implement technical policies and procedures for electronic information systems that maintain ePHI to allow access only to authorized persons or software programs;
(b) Implement mechanisms to record and examine activity in information systems that contain or use ePHI;
(c) Implement electronic mechanisms to corroborate that ePHI has not been altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner;
(d) Implement technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to ePHI that is being transmitted over an electronic communications network.

**3.4 Encryption Standards.** Business Associate shall encrypt all ePHI:
(a) At rest using AES-256 encryption or equivalent;
(b) In transit using TLS 1.2 or higher.

---

#### ARTICLE IV - BREACH NOTIFICATION

**4.1 Discovery.** A Breach shall be treated as discovered as of the first day on which such Breach is known to the Business Associate, or by exercising reasonable diligence, would have been known.

**4.2 Notification.** Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity following the discovery of a Breach of Unsecured PHI. Such notification shall:
(a) Be made without unreasonable delay and in no case later than twenty-four (24) hours following discovery;
(b) Include, to the extent possible: (i) identification of each Individual whose PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed; (ii) a description of what happened; (iii) the types of PHI involved; (iv) steps Individuals should take to protect themselves; (v) what Business Associate is doing to investigate, mitigate, and prevent recurrence.

**4.3 Cooperation.** Business Associate shall cooperate with Covered Entity in investigating and responding to any Breach, including providing information for Covered Entity to notify affected Individuals, the Secretary, and media outlets as required.

---

#### ARTICLE V - TERM AND TERMINATION

**5.1 Term.** This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and shall terminate when all PHI provided by Covered Entity to Business Associate, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, is destroyed or returned to Covered Entity.

**5.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement if the other party materially breaches any term of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of such breach.

**5.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Business Associate shall:
(a) Return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity or created or received on behalf of Covered Entity;
(b) Certify in writing that all PHI has been returned or destroyed;
(c) If return or destruction is infeasible, extend the protections of this Agreement to the PHI and limit further Uses and Disclosures to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible.

---

#### ARTICLE VI - GENERAL PROVISIONS

**6.1 Regulatory References.** A reference to a section in the HIPAA Rules means the section as in effect or as amended.

**6.2 Amendment.** The parties agree to amend this Agreement as necessary to comply with the requirements of HIPAA and the HIPAA Rules.

**6.3 Survival.** The obligations of Business Associate under this Agreement shall survive termination.

**6.4 Interpretation.** Any ambiguity in this Agreement shall be resolved in favor of a meaning that permits Covered Entity to comply with the HIPAA Rules.

**6.5 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Service Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof.

## Breach Notification Procedures

### 1. Breach Detection

**Sources of Detection:**
- Automated security monitoring (SIEM alerts)
- Employee reports
- Customer reports
- Third-party notifications
- Penetration testing findings

**Detection Triggers:**
- Unauthorized access to PHI
- Unauthorized disclosure of PHI
- Loss or theft of devices containing PHI
- Malware/ransomware affecting PHI systems
- Improper disposal of PHI

### 2. Initial Assessment (Within 4 Hours)

Upon potential breach detection:

1. **Contain the incident**
   - Isolate affected systems
   - Revoke compromised credentials
   - Block malicious IPs/actors

2. **Assemble response team**
   - Security Lead
   - CTO
   - Legal/Compliance
   - Customer Success (if customer-facing)

3. **Document initial findings**
   - Date/time of discovery
   - How detected
   - Systems/data affected
   - Initial scope assessment

### 3. Breach Confirmation (Within 24 Hours)

**Assessment Questions:**
- Was PHI involved?
- Was the PHI unsecured (unencrypted)?
- Was the access/disclosure unauthorized?
- Is there a low probability of compromise? (Consider 4 factors below)

**Low Probability Assessment (45 CFR 164.402):**
1. Nature and extent of PHI involved
2. Who received/accessed the PHI
3. Whether PHI was actually acquired or viewed
4. Extent to which risk has been mitigated

**If Breach Confirmed → Proceed to Notification**

### 4. Customer Notification (Within 24 Hours of Confirmation)

**Notification Contents:**
- Brief description of what happened
- Date of breach and date of discovery
- Types of PHI involved
- Steps we are taking
- Steps customer should take
- Contact information for questions

**Notification Method:**
- Phone call to primary contact
- Followed by email documentation
- Follow-up communication as investigation progresses

### 5. Documentation

**Breach Log Entry:**
```
Incident ID: [YYYY-MM-DD-###]
Date Discovered: [Date/Time]
Date Reported: [Date/Time]
Description: [Brief description]
PHI Involved: [Types of data]
Individuals Affected: [Count]
Root Cause: [Description]
Remediation: [Actions taken]
Customer Notified: [Yes/No - Date/Time]
HHS Notification: [If applicable]
```

**Retention:**
- All breach documentation retained for 6 years
- Available for HHS audit

### 6. HHS Notification Support

For breaches affecting 500+ individuals:
- HDIM supports customer with HHS notification
- Provides information for required notification content
- Assists with media notification if required

For breaches affecting <500 individuals:
- HDIM provides documentation for annual HHS log
- Assists customer with required annual submission
