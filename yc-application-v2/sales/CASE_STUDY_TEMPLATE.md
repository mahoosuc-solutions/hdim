## Metrics Collection Checklist

### Pre-Implementation (Collect at Sales/Onboarding)

#### Baseline Quality Scores
- [ ] Overall quality composite score
- [ ] Individual measure performance (top 5-10 measures)
- [ ] National/peer benchmark comparison
- [ ] Trend data (past 12 months if available)

#### Current State Operations
- [ ] Hours/week spent on quality reporting
- [ ] Number of staff involved
- [ ] Current tools/systems used
- [ ] Report generation time
- [ ] Data freshness (how old is quality data?)

#### Financial Baseline
- [ ] Current quality software spend
- [ ] Consulting/contractor costs
- [ ] Previous year quality bonus/penalty
- [ ] Medicare revenue (for MIPS customers)
- [ ] Attributed lives (for ACO customers)

### Post-Implementation (Collect at 30/60/90 Days)

#### 30-Day Check
- [ ] Integration complete (Y/N, days to complete)
- [ ] Users active
- [ ] Initial impressions (qualitative)
- [ ] Any early wins

#### 60-Day Check
- [ ] Quality score updates (compare to baseline)
- [ ] Care gaps identified and closed
- [ ] Time savings (staff survey)
- [ ] Feature utilization

#### 90-Day Full Assessment
- [ ] Quality score improvement (%)
- [ ] Measures improved (count and list)
- [ ] Care gaps closed (count)
- [ ] Hours saved per week
- [ ] Reports generated
- [ ] Financial impact calculation
- [ ] User satisfaction (NPS or rating)
- [ ] Testimonial/quote collection

### Annual Review

- [ ] Full year quality score comparison
- [ ] Quality bonus/penalty outcome
- [ ] Total ROI calculation
- [ ] Renewal discussion
- [ ] Reference customer agreement

### Example 1: Solo Practice

# Valley Family Medicine: From MIPS Penalty to Top-Tier Performance

> **A solo family medicine practice in rural Oregon transformed from facing MIPS penalties to earning a 5% bonus—in just 90 days.**

---

## Quick Stats

| Metric | Before HDIM | After HDIM | Improvement |
|--------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| MIPS Score | 42/100 | 86/100 | +105% |
| Quality Reporting Time | 8 hrs/week | 1 hr/week | -88% |
| Care Gaps Closed | 15/month | 65/month | +333% |
| MIPS Outcome | -4% penalty | +5% bonus | +$18,000/year |

---

## About Valley Family Medicine

**Organization Type:** Solo practice
**Location:** Grants Pass, Oregon
**Size:** 1 physician, 2 staff, 1,200 patients
**EHR:** DrChrono
**Quality Programs:** MIPS

Valley Family Medicine serves a rural community where many patients travel 30+ miles for care. Dr. Sarah Chen has practiced here for 12 years, building deep relationships with multi-generational families.

---

## The Challenge

### Situation
Dr. Chen received a MIPS penalty for two consecutive years, losing $12,000-$15,000 annually in Medicare payments. She knew her care was good—but couldn't prove it.

### Pain Points
- **No visibility:** Quality data was compiled manually, quarterly, always out of date
- **Reporting burden:** Office manager spent 8+ hours/week on quality tracking
- **Missing gaps:** Patients fell through the cracks between visits
- **Technology gap:** DrChrono's built-in reports didn't meet MIPS requirements

### Previous Approach
Excel spreadsheets with patient lists, manually updated after each visit. Monthly "quality huddles" to review aging spreadsheets that were already out of date.

### Impact
- Two years of MIPS penalties totaling $27,000
- Staff overtime and frustration
- Patients missing preventive care
- Dr. Chen considering joining a larger group

> **"I was spending more time on paperwork than with patients. And I was still getting penalized. Something had to change."**
> — Dr. Sarah Chen, MD

---

## The Solution

### Why HDIM
Dr. Chen found HDIM through a Google search for "affordable MIPS software." She was skeptical that a $49/month tool could solve her problem—but the 30-day trial removed the risk.

### Implementation
- **Timeline:** 2 days (CSV export → first dashboard)
- **Effort Required:** 30 minutes from office manager
- **Integration Method:** CSV upload from DrChrono

### Key Features Used
1. **Real-time dashboard:** Knew exactly where she stood on every measure
2. **Care gap worklist:** Prioritized patients for outreach
3. **MIPS report generator:** One-click submission-ready files

---

## The Results

### Quantitative Outcomes

#### Quality Improvement
| Measure | Before | After | Change |
|---------|--------|-------|--------|
| Diabetes Control (HbA1c) | 58% | 78% | +34% |
| Blood Pressure Control | 62% | 81% | +31% |
| Preventive Screenings | 45% | 72% | +60% |

#### Operational Efficiency
- **Time Saved:** 7 hours/week (from 8 to 1)
- **Staff Productivity:** Office manager now handles billing expansion
- **Report Generation:** From 4 hours to 5 minutes

#### Financial Impact
- **MIPS Bonus Earned:** $18,000 (vs. $12,000 penalty prior year)
- **Swing:** $30,000/year improvement
- **HDIM Cost:** $588/year
- **Total ROI:** 51x in first year

### Qualitative Outcomes
- Dr. Chen regained confidence in her practice's quality
- Staff morale improved (no more spreadsheet drudgery)
- Patients receiving proactive outreach for screenings

> **"I went from dreading MIPS to looking forward to reporting season. My score went from 42 to 86. I'm keeping $30,000 more per year—for $49 a month."**
> — Dr. Sarah Chen, MD

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Small practices can compete:** Real-time quality data levels the playing field
2. **Simple wins:** CSV upload took 2 days, not 6 months
3. **ROI is immediate:** Payback was less than one week

### Example 2: FQHC

# Community Care Network: Closing 10,000 Care Gaps in Year One

> **A five-site FQHC in Ohio used HDIM to identify and close 10,000+ care gaps, improving UDS scores and capturing $200K in additional grant funding.**

---

## Quick Stats

| Metric | Before HDIM | After HDIM | Improvement |
|--------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| Care Gaps Identified | ~500/month | ~2,500/month | +400% |
| Care Gaps Closed | ~200/month | ~1,000/month | +400% |
| UDS Clinical Quality | 68% composite | 84% composite | +24% |
| Grant Funding | Baseline | +$200,000 | New funding |

---

## About Community Care Network

**Organization Type:** FQHC
**Location:** Greater Cleveland, Ohio
**Size:** 5 sites, 18 providers, 22,000 patients
**EHR:** NextGen + Quest Labs
**Quality Programs:** UDS, Medicaid Quality, HEDIS (for MCO contracts)

Community Care Network serves predominantly uninsured and Medicaid populations across urban and suburban communities. Their mission is to provide high-quality care regardless of ability to pay.

---

## The Challenge

### Situation
With 22,000 patients across 5 sites and 18 providers, the quality team was overwhelmed. They couldn't identify care gaps fast enough, and by the time they ran reports, patients had already been seen without addressing preventive needs.

### Pain Points
- **Data silos:** NextGen for clinical, Quest for labs, different system for referrals
- **Batch reporting:** Weekly reports were already stale when distributed
- **Site variation:** Some sites performing well, others struggling—no visibility
- **Staff burden:** 1.5 FTE dedicated to quality reporting

### Previous Approach
A combination of NextGen's built-in reports (limited), manual chart review, and quarterly consulting engagement for UDS prep. Cost: $75,000/year.

### Impact
- UDS scores stagnating at 68%
- Medicaid MCOs threatening contract non-renewal
- Missed opportunities for HRSA bonus funding
- Burned-out quality staff

> **"We had data everywhere but insight nowhere. Our providers wanted to do the right thing, but they didn't know which patients needed what—until after the visit was over."**
> — Maria Santos, Director of Quality Improvement

---

## The Solution

### Why HDIM
Community Care Network evaluated three options: expand their consulting engagement, implement Azara, or try HDIM. HDIM's real-time capability and FQHC discount made it the clear choice.

### Implementation
- **Timeline:** 3 weeks (FHIR connection + lab data integration)
- **Effort Required:** 8 hours from IT, 4 hours from quality team
- **Integration Method:** FHIR R4 to NextGen + n8n workflow for Quest labs

### Key Features Used
1. **Multi-site dashboard:** Compare performance across all 5 locations
2. **Pre-visit planning:** Care gap reports for each day's schedule
3. **Provider scorecards:** Transparent, provider-level quality visibility
4. **UDS report alignment:** Measures mapped to UDS clinical quality section

---

## The Results

### Quantitative Outcomes

#### Quality Improvement
| Measure | Before | After | Change |
|---------|--------|-------|--------|
| Diabetes HbA1c Control | 52% | 71% | +37% |
| Childhood Immunizations | 65% | 88% | +35% |
| Cervical Cancer Screening | 48% | 69% | +44% |
| Depression Screening | 55% | 82% | +49% |

#### Operational Efficiency
- **Time Saved:** 40 hours/week across quality team
- **Care Gaps Identified:** 5x increase (500 → 2,500/month)
- **Care Gaps Closed:** 5x increase (200 → 1,000/month)

#### Financial Impact
- **Medicaid Quality Bonus:** $150,000 (new, previously not earned)
- **HRSA Bonus Funding:** $200,000 (due to UDS improvement)
- **Consulting Eliminated:** $75,000/year
- **Total Value:** $425,000/year
- **HDIM Cost:** $10,188/year (with FQHC discount)
- **ROI:** 42x

### Qualitative Outcomes
- Quality team shifted from data collection to improvement coaching
- Providers engaged in "friendly competition" on quality scores
- MCO contract renewed with expanded panel

> **"We closed 10,000 care gaps in our first year with HDIM. That's 10,000 patients who got a screening, a vaccine, or a chronic disease intervention they would have missed. That's why we're here."**
> — Dr. James Wright, Chief Medical Officer

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Real-time matters:** Batch reports arrive too late; real-time enables action
2. **Multi-source data:** Labs + EHR + referrals = complete picture
3. **ROI is massive:** 42x return when quality drives grant funding

### Example 3: ACO

# Lakeshore Health Partners: Real-Time Quality for 42,000 Lives

> **A mid-size ACO replaced a $600K/year quality platform with HDIM, achieving better results at 95% lower cost.**

---

## Quick Stats

| Metric | Before HDIM | After HDIM | Improvement |
|--------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| Annual Quality Spend | $600,000 | $30,000 | -95% |
| Data Latency | 48 hours | <200ms | Real-time |
| ACO Quality Score | 72nd percentile | 88th percentile | +22% |
| Shared Savings Impact | $1.2M | $2.1M | +$900K |

---

## About Lakeshore Health Partners

**Organization Type:** MSSP ACO
**Location:** Milwaukee, Wisconsin
**Size:** 45 practices, 120 providers, 42,000 attributed lives
**EHR:** Epic (35 practices), Cerner (10 practices)
**Quality Programs:** MSSP ACO, HEDIS (payer contracts)

Lakeshore Health Partners is an independent physician-led ACO that has participated in MSSP since 2017. They've consistently generated shared savings but believed they were leaving money on the table due to quality score limitations.

---

## The Challenge

### Situation
Lakeshore was using a well-known enterprise quality platform (Arcadia) at $50,000/month. Despite the investment, their quality data was always 48+ hours old, and their ACO team spent excessive time on "care gap chase" instead of improvement.

### Pain Points
- **Stale data:** By the time care gaps were identified, patients had already visited
- **Cost:** $600K/year for quality infrastructure, plus implementation fees
- **Complexity:** Required dedicated analyst to operate the platform
- **Multi-EHR challenges:** Epic and Cerner data didn't integrate well

### Previous Approach
Arcadia for quality measurement, supplemented by EHR-specific reports and manual reconciliation. Total team: 2 FTE analysts + $600K software.

### Impact
- Quality scores stuck at 72nd percentile
- Shared savings capped by quality multiplier
- Provider frustration with "old" care gap lists
- Board questioning ROI of quality investment

> **"We were spending $600,000 a year on quality software, and our providers were still getting care gap lists from last week. Something was fundamentally broken."**
> — David Kim, CEO

---

## The Solution

### Why HDIM
Lakeshore's CMO saw HDIM at a conference and was skeptical—how could a $2,500/month tool beat a $50,000/month enterprise solution? They ran a 90-day pilot alongside Arcadia.

### Implementation
- **Timeline:** 4 weeks (parallel with Arcadia)
- **Effort Required:** 12 hours IT, 8 hours quality team
- **Integration Method:** FHIR R4 to Epic + Cerner, Bulk FHIR export for nightly sync

### Key Features Used
1. **Real-time care gaps:** Alerts within 200ms of data change
2. **ACO-wide dashboard:** All 45 practices, 120 providers, one view
3. **Provider-level benchmarking:** "Am I above or below my peers?"
4. **SMART on FHIR integration:** Embedded in Epic workflow

---

## The Results

### Quantitative Outcomes

#### Quality Improvement
| Measure | With Arcadia | With HDIM | Change |
|---------|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Diabetes Control | 68% | 79% | +16% |
| Depression Follow-up | 55% | 71% | +29% |
| Statin Therapy Adherence | 72% | 85% | +18% |
| Colorectal Screening | 61% | 74% | +21% |

#### Operational Efficiency
- **Time Saved:** 80 hours/week across ACO team
- **Platform Cost:** $570,000/year savings
- **Analyst Redeployment:** 1.5 FTE moved to improvement initiatives

#### Financial Impact
- **Platform Savings:** $570,000/year (Arcadia → HDIM)
- **Increased Shared Savings:** $900,000/year (from quality improvement)
- **Total Value:** $1,470,000/year
- **HDIM Cost:** $30,000/year
- **ROI:** 49x

### Qualitative Outcomes
- Providers actively use quality dashboards (engagement up 3x)
- ACO team shifted from data wrangling to quality coaching
- Board now sees quality as value driver, not cost center

> **"We improved our quality percentile by 16 points and saved $570,000 in software costs. In the same year. I didn't think that was possible."**
> — Dr. Angela Martinez, CMO

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Enterprise doesn't mean better:** Real-time beats expensive batch systems
2. **Cost reduction + quality improvement:** Not a tradeoff—HDIM delivers both
3. **Pilot before committing:** 90-day parallel pilot proved the value

### Design Guidelines

**Colors:**
- Primary: HDIM brand blue (#0066CC)
- Accent: Success green (#00AA55) for improvements
- Text: Dark gray (#333333)
- Background: White with subtle gray (#F5F5F5) for stat boxes

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Bold, 24-28pt
- Subheads: Bold, 16-18pt
- Body: Regular, 11-12pt
- Stats: Bold, 36-48pt

**Photography:**
- If customer provides photos: Use real images of their facility/team
- If no photos: Use abstract healthcare imagery (no stock photos of stethoscopes)
- Minimum resolution: 300 DPI for print

**Customer Approval:**
- All case studies require written customer approval before publication
- Use approval form template (see appendix)
- Allow 2 weeks for customer review
